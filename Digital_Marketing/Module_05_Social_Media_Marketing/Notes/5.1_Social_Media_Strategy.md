### Social Media Marketing vs. Social Media Management

| Aspect | Social Media Marketing | Social Media Management |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Strategy, growth, ROI, campaigns | Day-to-day operations, posting, responding |
| **Goal** | Drive business outcomes | Maintain consistent brand presence |
| **Activities** | Campaign planning, paid ads, influencer collabs, analytics | Scheduling posts, replying to comments, monitoring |
| **Metrics** | ROAS, leads, conversions, revenue | Engagement rate, response time, follower growth |
| **Scope** | Strategic | Tactical |
| **Who** | Marketing strategist / CMO | Social media coordinator / manager |

**Real-Time Example:**
Wendy's social media operation illustrates both sides. Their **management** team replies to hundreds of tweets daily with witty responses (maintaining brand voice). Their **marketing** team planned the "National Roast Day" campaign that generated 1.5M+ impressions in 24 hours. Management keeps the lights on; marketing drives growth.

### Organic vs. Paid Social Media Strategies

| Dimension | Organic Social | Paid Social |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost** | Free (except labor and tools) | Budget required (CPM, CPC, CPA models) |
| **Reach** | 1-5% of followers (declining) | Unlimited (based on budget) |
| **Speed** | Slow, compounds over time | Immediate results |
| **Trust** | Higher (feels authentic) | Lower (marked as ad) |
| **Targeting** | Broad (whoever sees it) | Precise (demographics, behaviors, interests) |
| **Longevity** | Evergreen content lasts | Stops when budget stops |
| **Best for** | Community, brand, loyalty | Acquisition, retargeting, launches |

**The Organic Reach Crisis:**
- Facebook organic reach in 2012: ~16% of followers
- Facebook organic reach in 2025: ~1.5-2.5% of followers
- Instagram organic reach in 2025: ~3-5% of followers
- LinkedIn organic reach in 2025: ~8-12% of followers (highest among major platforms)
- TikTok organic reach in 2025: ~10-20%+ (FYP algorithm favors discovery)

**Real-Time Example — Organic vs. Paid Working Together:**
Glossier built a $1.8B beauty brand primarily through organic social. Their Instagram community of 2.7M+ followers shares UGC featuring Glossier products. They rarely ran paid ads in early years. However, as organic reach declined, they layered paid campaigns to amplify top-performing organic posts (a strategy called "boosting winners"). Result: 70% of their growth came from organic community, 30% from paid amplification.

## 5.1.2 Developing a Social Media Strategy

### Social Media Audit: Where Are You Now?

A social media audit is a systematic review of your current social presence, performance, and competitive position. Do this quarterly at minimum.

**Audit Framework (6-Step Process):**

**Step 1: Profile Inventory**

| Platform | Handle | Followers | Verified? | Bio Optimized? | Link Active? | Last Post |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | @yourbrand | 15.2K | Yes | Needs update | Linktree active | 2 days ago |
| LinkedIn | /company/yourbrand | 8.5K | Yes | Good | Website link | 5 days ago |
| TikTok | @yourbrand | 2.1K | No | Missing CTA | No link | 3 weeks ago |
| Facebook | /yourbrand | 22K | Yes | Outdated | Broken link | 1 week ago |

**Step 2: Performance Benchmarking (Last 90 Days)**

| Metric | Instagram | LinkedIn | TikTok | Facebook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Avg. engagement rate | 2.1% | 3.4% | 5.8% | 0.8% |
| Follower growth rate | +1.2%/mo | +2.5%/mo | +8%/mo | -0.3%/mo |
| Top content type | Reels | Text posts | Trends | Video |
| Best posting time | Tues 11am | Wed 9am | Fri 7pm | Sat 10am |
| Link clicks/month | 450 | 1,200 | 80 | 320 |

**Step 3: Content Audit**
- What content types perform best? (Video vs. image vs. text vs. carousel)
- What themes/topics get the most engagement?
- What is the posting frequency? Is it consistent?
- Are you repurposing content across platforms?

**Step 4: Audience Analysis**
- Who is actually following you vs. who you want to reach?
- What is the demographic breakdown (age, gender, location)?
- When are they online?
- What other accounts do they follow?

**Step 5: Competitor Analysis**

| Metric | Your Brand | Competitor A | Competitor B | Industry Avg |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram followers | 15.2K | 45K | 28K | 25K |
| Engagement rate | 2.1% | 3.8% | 1.9% | 2.5% |
| Posting frequency | 3x/week | 5x/week | Daily | 4x/week |
| Top content type | Carousel | Reels | UGC | Mixed |
| Response time | 4 hours | 30 min | 12 hours | 2 hours |

**Step 6: SWOT Analysis**

```
┌─────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────┐
│       STRENGTHS             │       WEAKNESSES            │
│ - Strong LinkedIn presence  │ - Inconsistent posting      │
│ - High engagement on Reels  │ - No TikTok strategy        │
│ - Loyal community           │ - Slow customer response    │
│ - Quality visual content    │ - No paid social budget     │
├─────────────────────────────┼─────────────────────────────┤
│       OPPORTUNITIES         │       THREATS               │
│ - TikTok growth potential   │ - Competitor gaining share  │
│ - Short-form video trend    │ - Algorithm changes         │
│ - Social commerce           │ - Rising ad costs           │
│ - AI content tools          │ - Platform policy changes   │
└─────────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────────┘
```

**Real-Time Example — Audit in Action:**
When Spotify audited their social presence in 2022, they discovered that their TikTok engagement rate (7.2%) was 3x higher than Instagram (2.4%) despite having 10x fewer TikTok followers. They shifted 40% of their creative resources to TikTok and launched "Spotify Wrapped" TikTok templates. Result: Spotify Wrapped 2023 generated 150M+ TikTok views and became the biggest annual social media moment for any brand.

### Crisis Communication Plan for Social Media

**Crisis Response Framework (RACE Model):**

```
R — RECOGNIZE: Identify the issue early (social listening alerts)
A — ASSESS: Evaluate severity (1-5 scale), determine stakeholders
C — COMMUNICATE: Respond transparently, provide updates
E — EVALUATE: Post-crisis analysis, update protocols
```

**Crisis Severity Scale:**

| Level | Description | Example | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 - Minor | Negative comment, minor complaint | Bad product review | Standard response, resolve privately |
| 2 - Moderate | Multiple complaints, trend forming | Shipping delays at scale | Public acknowledgment, updates |
| 3 - Significant | Viral negative post, media attention | Employee misconduct video | Exec-level response, official statement |
| 4 - Severe | Brand safety issue, boycott threat | Offensive ad campaign | Full PR team, CEO statement, ad pull |
| 5 - Critical | Legal, health/safety, data breach | Customer data leak | Legal + C-suite + comms, all posts paused |

**Real-Time Example — Crisis Handled Well:**
When a United Airlines video of a passenger being dragged off a plane went viral in 2017 (100M+ views in 24 hours), their initial response was defensive ("re-accommodated"). After massive backlash, CEO Oscar Munoz issued a personal apology, announced policy changes, and committed to customer rights within 48 hours. Lesson: The first response sets the trajectory. The initial defensive statement cost them $1.4B in market cap.

**Real-Time Example — Crisis Handled Poorly Then Recovered:**
Peloton's holiday ad in 2019 was mocked as "sexist" and went viral for the wrong reasons. Stock dropped 9% ($940M). But aviation gin company Ryan Reynolds hired the same actress within 48 hours for a cheeky ad, showing how a brand can newsjack a crisis and win. Peloton learned to pre-test messaging with diverse focus groups before launch.

### Content Mix Ratios

**The 80/20 Rule:**
- 80% value-driven content (educate, entertain, inspire)
- 20% promotional content (sell, pitch, CTA)

**The 70/20/10 Rule (More Granular):**
- 70% proven, safe content that performs consistently
- 20% innovative content experimenting with new formats
- 10% high-risk, experimental content (new platforms, bold ideas)

**The 5-3-2 Rule (Per 10 Posts):**
- 5 posts: curated content from others (industry news, reshares)
- 3 posts: original content you created
- 2 posts: personal/human content (team, BTS, culture)

**Real-Time Example:**
Red Bull follows a 90/10 approach — 90% of their content is extreme sports, athletes, and adventure (entertainment, no product push), and only 10% explicitly features their product. Their Instagram (16M+ followers) barely shows a can of Red Bull, yet they sell 12B+ cans/year. Content that entertains builds the brand; explicit promotion doesn't need to dominate.

### Trending Topics and Real-Time Marketing

**How to Newsjack Effectively:**

1. **Speed:** You have 2-6 hours max before a trend peaks
2. **Relevance:** Only jump on trends that connect to your brand (forced trends backfire)
3. **Brand-safe:** Avoid political, controversial, or tragic events
4. **Add value:** Don't just comment — add a unique angle

**Trending Content Decision Tree:**

```
Is the trend relevant to your brand?
├── YES → Is it brand-safe (not political/tragic)?
│    ├── YES → Can you add unique value/angle?
│    │    ├── YES → CREATE content within 2-4 hours
│    │    └── NO → SKIP (don't force it)
│    └── NO → SKIP (never touch controversial trends)
└── NO → SKIP (irrelevant trends make you look desperate)
```

**Real-Time Example:**
During the 2024 Super Bowl, Oreo's social team went viral in 2013 with "You can still dunk in the dark" during the power outage. That tweet earned 15K+ retweets in hours and $0 in ad spend. Their secret: a war room with creative, legal, and brand teams all present and pre-authorized to approve real-time content in minutes, not days.

```

├── TikTok/Reel (60-sec summary with talking head)

This "pillar content → micro content" model means one hour of creation produces a week of content across all platforms.

## 5.1.4 Social Media Governance

### Social Media Policy for Organizations

A social media policy protects the company and empowers employees to represent the brand safely online.

**Policy Components:**

1. **Purpose and scope** — Who the policy applies to and why
2. **Official accounts** — Who owns and manages brand accounts
3. **Personal accounts** — Guidelines for employees' personal social use
4. **Confidentiality** — What can and cannot be shared
5. **Legal compliance** — Copyright, FTC disclosure, privacy laws
6. **Brand guidelines** — Voice, visuals, approved messaging
7. **Crisis protocol** — What to do if something goes wrong
8. **Consequences** — Violations and enforcement

**Real-Time Example:**
Dell's social media policy is publicly available and considered an industry benchmark. It covers 5 principles: (1) Protect information, (2) Be transparent, (3) Follow the law, (4) Be responsible, (5) Be nice. It empowers 100K+ employees to be brand advocates while protecting confidential information.

### Employee Advocacy Guidelines

Employee advocacy turns your workforce into a distributed marketing team. Employee content gets 8x more engagement than brand content.

**Employee Advocacy Program Framework:**

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| Platform | LinkedIn is primary for B2B; Instagram for B2C |
| Content provided | Pre-approved posts, images, talking points (employees customize) |

| Tools | Sprout Social Advocacy, Bambu, LinkedIn Elevate, EveryoneSocial |

**Real-Time Example:**
Adobe's employee advocacy program generates 3x more engagement per post than Adobe's official brand accounts. They trained 30K+ employees on social best practices and provide a weekly "Content Kit" with pre-approved posts employees can personalize and share. Result: $3M+ in equivalent media value from employee social posts annually.

## Strategies

1. **Platform-Focus Strategy:** Master 2-3 platforms before expanding. Depth beats breadth in social media.
2. **Community-First Strategy:** Prioritize building a 1,000-person highly engaged community over a 100,000-follower vanity metric.
3. **Pillar Content Repurposing Strategy:** Create one pillar piece per week and distribute 10+ derivative pieces across platforms.
4. **Data-Driven Posting Strategy:** Test 3 different posting times per platform over 30 days, then lock in the top performer.
5. **Organic-Paid Flywheel Strategy:** Use organic content to test messaging; amplify winners with paid. Never run paid ads on content that failed organically.
6. **Real-Time Agility Strategy:** Keep 20% of your content calendar open for trending/newsjacking opportunities.

## Best Practices

1. **Audit before you strategize.** Never build strategy on assumptions — audit your current performance and competitor landscape first.
2. **Document everything.** Your social media strategy should be a living document, not tribal knowledge in one person's head.
3. **Quality beats quantity.** Posting 3 excellent pieces/week outperforms 3 mediocre pieces/day.
4. **Respond to every comment.** Social is a two-way channel. Brands that reply to comments see 4x higher engagement.
5. **Test relentlessly.** A/B test captions, posting times, content formats, and CTAs. Let data decide, not opinions.
6. **Plan for failure.** Have a crisis plan before you need one. The worst time to build a crisis plan is during a crisis.
7. **Invest in video.** Short-form video has the highest organic reach and engagement across all platforms in 2025-2026.
8. **Build systems, not one-offs.** Content batching, templates, and workflows scale your output without scaling your team.

## Additional Insights

### The 1% Rule of Social Media
In any online community, roughly 1% of users create content, 9% engage (like, comment), and 90% lurk (consume silently). Your metrics should account for this — a post with 100 likes may have been seen by 10,000 people.

### The Compound Effect of Social Media
Social media compounds like interest. A brand that posts consistently for 12 months builds an audience, credibility, and algorithm favor that makes month 13 dramatically easier than month 1. Most brands quit in month 3 — the ones that persist win.

### Dark Social: The Hidden 80%
Research shows 80%+ of social sharing happens through "dark social" — private channels like DMs, WhatsApp groups, email, and Slack. Your analytics only capture 20% of actual sharing. When someone says "I saw your post," they likely received it from a friend, not from their feed.

### The Algorithm Reality
Every platform algorithm rewards one thing above all: **retention**. Content that keeps people on the platform longer gets distributed more. This is why educational, entertaining, and story-driven content outperforms promotional content — it keeps people watching, reading, and engaging instead of scrolling past.
