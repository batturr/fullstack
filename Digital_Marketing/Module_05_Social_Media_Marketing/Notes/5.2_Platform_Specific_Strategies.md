## 5.2.1 Facebook / Meta

### Facebook Page Optimization

Facebook remains the world's largest social network (3.07B MAU). Despite declining organic reach, it is still critical for community building, local business, and paid advertising.

**Page Optimization Checklist:**

| Element | Best Practice | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Profile photo | Logo, 170x170px, consistent across platforms | Brand logo with no text cutoff |
| Cover photo/video | 820x312px, updated seasonally, include CTA | Current campaign or seasonal graphic |
| Username/URL | facebook.com/yourbrand (short, clean) | /nike not /nikeofficial2023 |
| About section | Complete all fields: description, category, hours, contact | Include keywords for search |
| CTA button | Match business goal: Shop Now, Book Now, Contact Us | "Shop Now" linking to e-commerce store |
| Page tabs | Prioritize: Posts, About, Reviews, Shop, Groups | Remove unused tabs |
| Pinned post | Best-performing or most important current content | Welcome video or current offer |
| Messenger | Enable auto-reply and business hours | "Thanks for reaching out! We'll reply within 1 hour" |

### Content Types and Strategy

| Content Type | Format | Algorithm Priority | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reels | Vertical video (9:16) | Highest reach | Discovery, entertainment |
| Live | Real-time broadcast | High (notifications sent) | Events, Q&A, launches |
| Groups | Community posts | High (dedicated feed) | Community building |
| Stories | 24-hour ephemeral | Medium | Casual, behind-scenes |
| Feed Video | Landscape/square | Medium | Education, storytelling |
| Photos | Image posts | Low-Medium | Brand shots, announcements |
| Links | External URL posts | Lowest | Driving traffic (but penalized) |

### Facebook Groups Strategy

Facebook Groups are the platform's strongest organic play — they receive 50%+ more engagement than Pages.

**Group Strategy Framework:**

| Element | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Type | Create a branded community group (not a "fans of" group) |
| Name | Include keyword + community purpose: "Digital Marketers Hub by [Brand]" |
| Rules | Set 5-7 clear rules; pin them; enforce consistently |
| Onboarding | Welcome post + 3 screening questions for new members |
| Content rhythm | Daily prompt, weekly theme, monthly event |
| Engagement | Respond to every post within 4 hours; highlight top contributors |
| Growth | Invite customers post-purchase; cross-promote from Page/email |

**Real-Time Example:**
Peloton's official Facebook Group has 400K+ members who share workout achievements, tips, and motivation. Members post 5,000+ times/day. The group drives brand loyalty so strongly that 94% of Peloton owners keep their membership — far above the fitness industry average of 70%. The group is essentially a free retention engine.

### Algorithm Understanding

**Facebook Algorithm Ranking Signals (2025-2026):**

```
RANKING PRIORITY (highest → lowest):
1. Content from close friends/family (most interactions)
2. Content type user typically engages with (personalized)
3. Reels and short-form video (platform push)
4. Posts with meaningful engagement (comments > reactions > shares)
5. Group posts
6. Page posts (organic)
7. External links (lowest priority — penalized)
```

**Key Algorithm Insights:**
- Comments are weighted 5x more than likes
- Shares to Stories/DMs count as "meaningful" engagement
- Posts that spark conversation (not just reaction) are amplified
- First 30-60 minutes of engagement determine distribution
- Posting frequency: quality > quantity; posting too often cannibalizes your own reach

### Messenger Marketing and Chatbots

**Messenger Marketing Stats:**
- 80% open rate (vs. 20% for email)
- 4.4x higher CTR than email
- 1.3B people use Messenger monthly

**Chatbot Use Cases:**

| Use Case | Implementation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Lead qualification | Automated questionnaire bot | "What's your budget? What services do you need?" |
| Customer FAQ | Keyword-triggered responses | "What are your hours?" → Auto-reply with hours |
| Order tracking | API integration with e-commerce | "Where's my order?" → Real-time tracking link |
| Appointment booking | Calendar integration | "Book a demo" → Available time slots |
| Content delivery | Drip sequence via Messenger | Daily tips series over 7 days |

**Tools:** ManyChat, Chatfuel, MobileMonkey

**Real-Time Example:**
Sephora's Messenger chatbot lets users take a quiz, get personalized product recommendations, book in-store makeovers, and receive order updates. It drove an 11% increase in in-store bookings and a $50 average purchase from chatbot-initiated conversations.

### Tracking with Meta Pixel and Conversions API

**Meta Pixel** tracks user actions on your website after they interact with your Facebook/Instagram ad:
- Page views, add to cart, purchases, leads, registrations
- Enables retargeting, lookalike audiences, and conversion optimization

**Conversions API (CAPI)** sends server-side events directly to Meta:
- More reliable than browser pixel (avoids ad blockers, cookie restrictions)
- Required for iOS 14.5+ privacy changes
- Works alongside pixel for "redundant matching"

### Instagram Reels Strategy and Algorithm

**Reels Algorithm Ranking Signals:**

| Signal | Weight | How to Optimize |
|---|---|---|
| Watch time / completion rate | Highest | Hook in first 1-2 seconds; keep under 30 seconds for completion |
| Shares (DMs + Stories) | Very High | Create "send to a friend" content |
| Likes + comments | High | End with a question or CTA |
| Saves | High | Provide reference-worthy value |
| Audio popularity | Medium | Use trending sounds (check Reels tab) |
| Account authority | Medium | Consistent posting history in your niche |
| Hashtag relevance | Low-Medium | Use 3-5 niche-specific hashtags |

**Reel Structure Formula:**

```
HOOK (0-3 sec) → "Stop scrolling if you..."
CONTEXT (3-8 sec) → Set up the problem or promise
VALUE (8-25 sec) → Deliver the content
CTA (last 2-3 sec) → "Save this for later" or "Follow for more"
```

**Real-Time Example:**
Duolingo's TikTok/Reels strategy turned their mascot Duo the Owl into a chaotic, unhinged character who threatens users who skip lessons. This character-driven approach earned 12M+ TikTok followers and 3M+ Instagram followers. Their secret: they entertain first, brand second. Only 5% of their content mentions the product.

## 5.2.3 LinkedIn

### Personal Profile Optimization for Thought Leadership

LinkedIn is the #1 platform for B2B marketing. Personal profiles get 10x more reach than Company Pages.

**Profile Optimization Checklist:**

| Element | Best Practice | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | Value proposition, not job title (220 chars) | "Helping SaaS companies 3x revenue through content marketing | CMO at [Company]" |
| Banner | Branded, includes CTA or value proposition | "Download my free marketing playbook → link" |
| About section | First person, storytelling, keywords, CTA (2,600 chars) | Hook → Story → Expertise → CTA |
| Featured section | Pin top content, case studies, media links | Top 3 performing posts + lead magnet link |
| Experience | Achievement-based, not task-based | "Grew organic traffic 400% in 18 months" not "managed SEO" |
| Skills | Top 5 skills aligned with expertise | Endorse and get endorsements strategically |
| Recommendations | 5+ from clients, peers, managers | Proactively request after successful projects |
| Creator Mode | Enable for Follow button + newsletter access | Signals thought leadership to algorithm |

### LinkedIn Content Types and Strategy

| Content Type | Reach Potential | Best For | Tips |
|---|---|---|---|
| Text posts | Highest organic reach | Storytelling, insights, opinions | Use hooks, line breaks, 1,200-1,500 chars optimal |
| Carousels (PDF) | Very High | Frameworks, how-tos, listicles | 8-12 slides, bold text, one idea per slide |
| Polls | High (drives comments) | Audience research, engagement | 4 options max, ask "why" in comments |
| Video (native) | High | Personal stories, tips, behind-scenes | Square or vertical, captions required, <2 min |
| Articles | Medium | Deep dives, SEO value | 800-2,000 words, include visuals |
| Newsletters | Growing | Subscriber building | 1-2x/month, consistent value |
| Documents | Medium-High | Reports, guides, presentations | Educational, data-driven |
| Live | Medium (notifications) | Events, panels, Q&A | Promote 1 week ahead |

### LinkedIn Algorithm (2025-2026)

**Ranking Signals:**

| Signal | Weight | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| Dwell time | Highest | How long people spend reading your post |
| Comments (meaningful) | Very High | Substantive comments > "Great post!" |
| Shares/reposts | High | Indicates high-value content |
| Early engagement (first hour) | High | Strong initial response triggers wider distribution |
| Relevance to network | High | Content related to your expertise and connections' interests |
| Post format | Medium | Native content > external links |
| Hashtags | Low-Medium | Use 3-5 relevant hashtags |
| External links | Low | Links in comments perform better than in post |

**LinkedIn Post Structure Formula:**

```
HOOK LINE (grab attention — first 2 lines visible)
↓
Empty line (visual break)
↓
STORY/CONTEXT (3-5 short paragraphs, 1-2 sentences each)
↓
KEY INSIGHT/LESSON (the "aha" moment)
↓
CTA (question, "agree?", "share your experience")
↓
Hashtags (3-5 at the end)
```

**Real-Time Example:**
Justin Welsh grew from 0 to 750K+ LinkedIn followers in 3 years as a solopreneur. His formula: post daily at 7:45am EST, use personal storytelling hooks ("I was fired at 33. Best thing that ever happened."), and always end with a question. He generates $5M+/year from LinkedIn-driven digital product sales with zero paid advertising.

## 5.2.5 TikTok

### Understanding TikTok's Audience and Culture

TikTok is not just a Gen Z platform anymore — 30% of users are 35+ and growing. It is a culture-shaping platform.

**TikTok Culture Pillars:**
- Authenticity over production quality
- Trends and sounds as creative frameworks
- Creator economy and personality-driven
- Education disguised as entertainment ("edutainment")
- Participation culture (Duets, Stitches, challenges)

### TikTok Algorithm: For You Page (FYP) Signals

**What the algorithm measures:**

| Signal | Weight | Optimization |
|---|---|---|
| Watch time / completion rate | Highest | Keep videos short (15-30s); hook in first 1 second |
| Replays (loop) | Very High | Create content worth watching twice |
| Shares | Very High | "Send this to someone who..." |
| Comments | High | Ask controversial or opinion-based questions |
| Follows from video | High | Give people a reason to see more |
| Likes | Medium | Satisfying content = likes |
| Sound usage | Medium | Use trending sounds for algorithm boost |
| Hashtag relevance | Low-Medium | 3-5 targeted hashtags |
| Account age/authority | Low | New accounts can go viral (unlike Instagram) |

**The TikTok Advantage:** Unlike Instagram/Facebook where followers matter, TikTok's FYP shows content from anyone to anyone. A brand-new account can go viral on its first video if the content is compelling.

### TikTok SEO

TikTok is now the #2 search engine for Gen Z (after Google, ahead of YouTube for some queries).

**TikTok SEO Optimization:**

| Element | Optimization | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Caption | Include searchable keywords naturally | "How to start a small business in 2026" |
| On-screen text | Keywords visible in video text overlays | Text overlay: "3 Instagram growth hacks" |
| Voiceover/speech | Say keywords aloud (TikTok transcribes audio) | Verbally say "social media marketing tips" |
| Hashtags | 3-5 keyword-based hashtags | #SocialMediaTips #MarketingStrategy |
| Comments | Pin a keyword-rich comment | "Part 2 of our digital marketing series!" |

**Real-Time Example:**
When searching "best restaurants in NYC" on TikTok, users often trust TikTok reviews more than Yelp or Google. A small restaurant, Emily Pizza in Brooklyn, went from 50 customers/day to 200+ after a TikTok video reviewing their pizza went viral (15M views). They didn't create the video — a customer did. The restaurant then leaned into TikTok by creating their own content, optimizing for food-related keywords.

### TikTok Shop and E-commerce

TikTok Shop enables in-app purchasing directly from videos and livestreams.

**TikTok Shop Stats:**
- $20B+ GMV in 2024 (growing 150% YoY)
- 70% of TikTok Shop purchases are impulse buys
- Livestream shopping events average 3x higher conversion than standard e-commerce

### YouTube Shorts Strategy

YouTube Shorts (vertical, <60 seconds) is YouTube's answer to TikTok and Reels.

**Shorts vs. Long-form:**

| Aspect | Shorts | Long-form |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | Very high (Shorts shelf) | Moderate (search + suggested) |
| Subscriber conversion | Low (casual viewers) | High (committed viewers) |
| Revenue | Lower RPM | Higher RPM |
| Best for | Awareness, teasers, trends | Education, depth, loyalty |
| Strategy | Use Shorts to funnel viewers to long-form | Long-form builds deep relationship |

**Real-Time Example:**
Ali Abdaal (4.7M subscribers) uses Shorts to tease his long-form videos. A 30-second Short about "3 productivity apps" gets 2-5M views, and he directs viewers to his full 20-minute review. This Shorts-to-Long-form funnel helped him grow 1M subscribers in 12 months.

## Strategies

1. **Platform-Native Content Strategy:** Never cross-post identical content. Adapt every piece to fit the platform's culture, format, and audience expectations.
2. **Short-Form Video Priority Strategy:** Allocate 40-50% of content resources to Reels, TikTok, and Shorts — they offer the highest organic reach in 2025-2026.
3. **LinkedIn Personal Brand Strategy:** For B2B, invest more in personal profiles of leadership than company pages — personal posts get 10x more reach.
4. **Pinterest Evergreen Strategy:** Create keyword-optimized pins that drive traffic for 6-12 months, unlike ephemeral content on other platforms.
5. **Platform Experimentation Strategy:** Allocate 10% of time to emerging platforms (Threads, BeReal, Lemon8) to be an early mover.

## Best Practices

1. **Go where your audience already is.** Don't chase new platforms because they're trendy — follow your customer data.
2. **Respect platform culture.** LinkedIn is not Instagram; TikTok is not YouTube. The same message needs different packaging.
3. **Video-first on every platform.** Every major platform now prioritizes video content. If you're not making video, you're limiting your reach.
4. **Optimize profiles as landing pages.** Your social profile is often the first brand impression — treat it like a homepage.
5. **Use platform-native features.** Platforms reward you for using their newest features (Reels on Instagram, Newsletters on LinkedIn, Shorts on YouTube).
6. **Study competitor top performers.** Don't copy — learn what content types and hooks work in your industry on each platform.
7. **Build for the algorithm, but create for humans.** Understand ranking signals, but never sacrifice authenticity for algorithmic tricks.

## Additional Insights

### The Multi-Platform Paradox
Being on every platform is worse than being excellent on 2-3. Spreading thin leads to mediocre content everywhere. Focus creates excellence which drives results. Start with 2, master them, then expand.

### Platform Lifecycle
Every platform follows a lifecycle: Launch → Growth → Maturity → Decline. The biggest organic reach opportunities exist in the Growth phase. TikTok is in late Growth. LinkedIn is in early Maturity. Facebook is in Maturity. Being early matters.

### The Content-Platform Fit Matrix
The #1 reason social content fails is poor content-platform fit. A polished, scripted brand video on TikTok will flop because TikTok rewards authenticity. A raw selfie video on LinkedIn will underperform because LinkedIn rewards insight and professionalism. Match the content to the platform, always.
