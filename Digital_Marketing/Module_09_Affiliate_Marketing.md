# Module 09: Affiliate Marketing

## 9.1 Affiliate Marketing Fundamentals

### 9.1.1 What Is Affiliate Marketing
- Definition: performance-based marketing where affiliates earn commissions for driving sales/actions
- How affiliate marketing works: merchant → network → affiliate → customer
- History and evolution of affiliate marketing
- Affiliate marketing industry size and growth trends
- Affiliate marketing vs. influencer marketing vs. referral marketing
- The role of affiliate marketing in the digital mix

### 9.1.2 Key Players in the Affiliate Ecosystem
- Merchants/Advertisers: brands that offer affiliate programs
- Affiliates/Publishers: individuals or companies promoting products
- Affiliate Networks: intermediaries connecting merchants and affiliates
- Affiliate Managers: in-house or agency professionals managing programs
- Customers: the end buyers
- Technology providers: tracking, attribution, fraud prevention

### 9.1.3 Affiliate Marketing Models
- Pay-per-sale (PPS): commission on completed purchases
- Pay-per-lead (PPL): payment for qualified leads
- Pay-per-click (PPC): payment for clicks (rare, high fraud risk)
- Pay-per-install (PPI): app install commissions
- Revenue sharing: ongoing percentage of customer lifetime value
- Hybrid models: combination of upfront + recurring
- Tiered commission structures
- Performance bonuses and incentives

### 9.1.4 Types of Affiliates
- Content creators and bloggers
- Coupon and deal sites
- Cashback and loyalty sites
- Comparison and review sites
- Email marketers
- Social media influencers
- YouTube creators
- PPC/search affiliates
- Technology partners (browser extensions, apps)
- Sub-affiliate networks
