## 16.3.1 Lead Capture

### Why Lead Capture Matters

Lead capture is the systematic process of collecting prospect information at various touchpoints, converting anonymous visitors into identifiable contacts that can be nurtured toward a purchase decision. Without effective lead capture, your website is a leaky bucket — traffic comes in, but nothing is retained.

**The Math:**
- Average website conversion rate (visitor → lead): 2.35% (across industries)
- Top 25% of companies convert at 5.31%
- Top 10% convert at 11.45%
- The difference between 2% and 5% for a site with 50,000 monthly visitors = 1,500 additional leads/month

### Lead Capture Methods

#### 1. Website Forms — Progressive Profiling & Smart Fields

**Traditional forms** ask for all information upfront — name, email, phone, company, title, budget, timeline — creating friction that kills conversion rates.

**Progressive profiling** collects data incrementally across multiple interactions. The first form asks for email only. The second asks for company and title. The third asks for budget and timeline. Each interaction deepens the profile without overwhelming the visitor.

| Form Visit | Fields Shown | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1st visit | Email, First name | Minimal friction; capture identity |
| 2nd visit | Company, Job title | Firmographic qualification |
| 3rd visit | Phone, Team size | Sales readiness signals |
| 4th visit | Budget range, Timeline | Purchase intent |

**Smart fields** automatically hide fields the CRM already has, replacing them with new questions. If HubSpot already knows the contact's company, it shows "What is your biggest marketing challenge?" instead.

**Real-Time Example — HubSpot Progressive Profiling:**
Marketo (Adobe) uses progressive profiling on their own resource downloads:
- **First download (eBook):** Email, first name, last name
- **Second download (whitepaper):** Company, job title, company size (email pre-filled)
- **Third download (case study):** Phone, industry, marketing automation currently used
- **Fourth download (ROI calculator):** Budget range, purchase timeline, decision-making role

By the fourth interaction, Marketo has a fully qualified lead profile without ever showing a 10-field form. Their data shows progressive profiling increased form completion rates by 42%.

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Enable progressive profiling in your MA platform (HubSpot: form settings → Progressive fields)
2. Define field priority — which data do you need first vs. last?
3. Set 3-4 fields per form visit maximum
4. Always keep email visible (identity anchor)
5. Test with A/B: progressive vs. full forms
6. Monitor field completion rates weekly

#### 7. Lead Source Tracking and Attribution

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **First touch** | 100% credit to the first interaction | Understanding top-of-funnel channels |
| **Last touch** | 100% credit to the final interaction before conversion | Understanding closing channels |
| **Linear** | Equal credit to every touchpoint | Balanced view across all channels |
| **Time decay** | More credit to recent touchpoints | Long sales cycles where later touches matter more |
| **Position-based (U-shaped)** | 40% first, 40% last, 20% distributed across middle | Balanced emphasis on discovery and conversion |
| **Custom/algorithmic** | ML model weights touchpoints based on historical impact | Enterprises with enough data volume |

## 16.3.2 Lead Scoring Models

### What Is Lead Scoring and Why It Matters

Lead scoring assigns numerical values to leads based on their attributes (who they are) and behaviors (what they do), ranking them from cold to hot so sales focuses on the most likely to convert.

**Without lead scoring:** Sales calls 100 leads randomly; connects with 10; closes 1.
**With lead scoring:** Sales calls the top 20 leads by score; connects with 12; closes 4.

**The Impact:**

| Metric | Without Scoring | With Scoring | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sales productivity | 5 calls to reach 1 prospect | 2 calls to reach 1 prospect | 60% more efficient |
| Lead-to-opportunity rate | 5% | 15% | 3x improvement |
| Sales cycle length | 90 days | 65 days | 28% faster |
| Marketing-sales alignment | "These leads are garbage" | "These leads are qualified" | Friction eliminated |

### Demographic/Firmographic Scoring

Score based on **who the person is** — their fit with your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

| Criteria | High Fit (+Points) | Medium Fit (+Points) | Low Fit (-Points) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Job Title** | VP/Director/Head of Marketing (+15) | Marketing Manager (+10) | Intern/Student (-10) |
| **Seniority** | C-level (+20) | Director (+15) | Individual contributor (+5) |
| **Company Size** | 200-5000 employees (+15) | 50-199 (+10) | 1-10 (0) |
| **Industry** | SaaS/Technology (+15) | Professional Services (+10) | Government (-5) |
| **Revenue** | $10M-$500M (+15) | $1M-$10M (+10) | <$500K (+0) |
| **Location** | US/UK/DACH (+10) | Rest of Europe (+5) | Outside target markets (0) |
| **Department** | Marketing (+15) | Sales (+10) | Unrelated (-5) |

**Real-Time Example — HubSpot's Own Lead Scoring:**
HubSpot scores leads for their sales team:
- **+20:** C-suite at a company with 200+ employees
- **+15:** Director of Marketing at a company using a competitor (Marketo, Pardot)
- **+10:** Marketing Manager who has previously used CRM software
- **+5:** Located in a tier-1 market (US, UK, Germany, Australia)
- **-10:** Email domain is @gmail.com or @yahoo.com (likely not a business buyer)
- **-15:** Job title contains "student" or "intern"
- **-20:** Company is a direct competitor

### Behavioral Scoring

Score based on **what the person does** — their engagement and buying signals.

| Behavior | Points | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Visited pricing page** | +20 | Strongest buying intent signal |
| **Requested a demo** | +25 | Direct sales readiness signal |
| **Downloaded ROI calculator** | +20 | Late-stage evaluation behavior |
| **Attended webinar** | +15 | Active engagement with brand |
| **Downloaded eBook** | +10 | Interest in topic area |
| **Opened 3+ emails in 7 days** | +10 | Growing engagement |
| **Visited 5+ pages in one session** | +10 | Active research behavior |
| **Read case study** | +15 | Evaluating proof/social validation |
| **Visited careers page** | -10 | Likely a job seeker, not a buyer |
| **Visited blog post (single)** | +3 | Low-intent, early research |
| **Unsubscribed from email** | -15 | Disengaged |
| **Submitted support ticket (non-customer)** | -5 | May be existing user, not new buyer |

### Negative Scoring

Negative scores prevent false positives and keep sales focused:

| Negative Signal | Points | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Email domain: @competitor.com | -50 | Competitor research, not a buyer |
| Email domain: @university.edu | -25 | Likely student, not buyer |
| Job title contains "student" | -30 | Not in buying role |
| No activity in 90+ days | -20 | Gone cold; needs re-engagement first |
| Unsubscribed from emails | -25 | Active opt-out |
| Bounced email (hard bounce) | -50 | Invalid contact |
| Country outside serviceable market | -15 | Can't serve them |
| Free email domain (gmail, yahoo) | -10 | Lower B2B likelihood |

### Engagement Scoring — Recency and Frequency

| Time Since Last Activity | Score Modifier |
|---|---|
| Within 24 hours | +15 (hot) |
| Within 7 days | +10 |
| Within 30 days | +5 |
| 30-60 days | 0 (neutral) |
| 60-90 days | -10 (cooling) |
| 90+ days | -20 (cold) |

**Engagement Decay Model:**
- Scores should decay over time — a webinar attended 6 months ago shouldn't carry the same weight as one attended yesterday
- Implement a monthly decay: reduce behavioral scores by 10-20% every 30 days of inactivity
- This prevents "zombie MQLs" — leads that scored high once but went dark

### Predictive Lead Scoring — Machine Learning Models

| Approach | How It Works | Tools | Accuracy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rule-based** (manual) | Marketing defines point values per criteria | Any MA platform | Depends on marketer's judgment (60-70% accuracy) |
| **Statistical** | Regression analysis on historical conversion data | Excel, R, Python | Better than manual (70-80%) |
| **Machine Learning** | Algorithms analyze thousands of data points to predict conversion | Salesforce Einstein, HubSpot Predictive, 6sense, MadKudu | Highest accuracy (80-95%) |

**How Predictive Scoring Works (Simplified):**

```
INPUT DATA (thousands of data points)
├── Firmographic: industry, size, revenue, tech stack, funding
├── Behavioral: page views, email engagement, content consumed
├── Intent: third-party intent data (Bombora, G2 visits)
├── Timing: when they engage, frequency patterns
└── Historical: which past leads with similar profiles converted

         │
         ▼
    ML MODEL (trains on historical win/loss data)
         │
         ▼

OUTPUT: Probability score 0-100
• 90+ = "Very likely to buy in 30 days"
• 70-89 = "Likely to buy in 60 days"
• 50-69 = "Needs nurturing"
• <50 = "Not ready"
```

**Real-Time Example — 6sense Predictive Scoring:**
A cybersecurity company uses 6sense to predict buying intent:
- 6sense monitors anonymous website visits, third-party review site activity (G2, TrustRadius), and intent signals (searches for "endpoint security comparison")
- Before the prospect ever fills out a form, 6sense identifies the company and scores their buying stage: Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Purchase
- When 6sense detects that Acme Corp employees have visited 3 competitor websites, read 5 analyst reports on endpoint security, and visited the pricing page twice this week — their "buying stage" moves to "Decision" with a score of 92
- The sales team gets an alert: "Acme Corp is in-market for endpoint security. Key contacts: CISO, VP IT. Recommended action: outbound call + personalized demo offer"
- Result: 3x higher win rate on 6sense-identified leads vs. inbound-only leads

### Score Thresholds and MQL Definitions

| Score Range | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 0-25 | Cold Lead | Automated nurture only |
| 26-50 | Warm Lead | Marketing nurture with increasing personalization |
| 51-70 | Marketing Engaged | Priority nurture; prepare for handoff |
| 71-85 | MQL | Hand off to sales; expect 4-hour follow-up |
| 86-100 | Hot Lead / Sales Ready | Immediate sales action; demo or call within 1 hour |

### Lead Scoring Maintenance and Calibration

| Activity | Frequency | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Score-to-close analysis** | Monthly | Do high-score leads actually close? Adjust if not |
| **Threshold review** | Quarterly | Is the MQL threshold too high (too few leads) or too low (low quality)? |
| **Sales feedback integration** | Monthly | Ask sales: "Are the MQLs you receive genuinely qualified?" |
| **New signal testing** | Quarterly | Test new scoring signals (e.g., LinkedIn engagement, chatbot interactions) |
| **Decay rate adjustment** | Semi-annually | Is score decay too aggressive or too slow? |
| **Full model recalibration** | Annually | Rebuild scoring model with latest conversion data |

## 16.3.3 Lead Nurturing

### What Is Lead Nurturing and Its Impact on Conversions

Lead nurturing is the process of building relationships with prospects at every stage of the funnel through targeted, relevant content and communication — guiding them toward a purchase decision without aggressive selling.

**Key Statistics:**
- 96% of website visitors are not ready to buy (Marketo)
- Nurtured leads produce a 20% increase in sales opportunities vs. non-nurtured (DemandGen Report)
- Nurtured leads make 47% larger purchases than non-nurtured (Annuitas Group)
- Companies that excel at nurturing generate 50% more sales-ready leads at 33% lower cost (Forrester)

### Drip Campaigns vs. Dynamic Nurturing

| Feature | Drip Campaign | Dynamic Nurturing |
|---|---|---|
| **Structure** | Fixed, linear sequence of emails | Adaptive path based on behavior |
| **Logic** | Time-based: send email every 3 days | Event-driven: "if opened, send X; if not, send Y" |
| **Personalization** | Basic (name, company) | Deep (content, channel, timing based on engagement) |
| **Complexity** | Simple to build; low maintenance | Complex; requires behavioral tracking and branching |
| **Best For** | Simple sequences (welcome, onboarding) | Multi-stage nurture with diverse personas |
| **Example** | 5-email welcome series, 3 days apart | Persona + funnel stage determines next content piece |

**Real-Time Example — Grammarly Win-Back:**

4. **Day 30:** "Your writing stats while you were Premium" — showing improvement data as emotional trigger
- Win-back rate: 15% of churned users return within 30 days

### Lead Aging and Recycling Processes

| Aging Threshold | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| MQL untouched for 24 hours | Escalation alert to sales manager | SLA violation; speed-to-lead matters |
| MQL untouched for 72 hours | Auto-reassign to backup rep | Prevent lead from going cold |
| SQL with no activity for 14 days | Alert to rep + manager | Deal may be stalling |
| Opportunity stuck for 30+ days | Review call with sales leadership | Pipeline hygiene |
| MQL rejected by sales | Return to marketing nurture with "recycled" status | Lead may become ready later |
| Closed-lost for 90+ days | Enter win-back nurture program | Circumstances may have changed |

### Funnel Velocity Analysis

Funnel velocity measures how fast leads move through each stage:

**Pipeline Velocity Formula:**
```
Pipeline Velocity = (Number of Opportunities × Win Rate × Average Deal Size) ÷ Sales Cycle Length

Example:
(200 opportunities × 25% win rate × $10,000 avg deal) ÷ 60 days = $8,333/day in pipeline velocity
```

| Stage Transition | Target Time | Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| New → Connected | < 4 hours | > 24 hours |
| Connected → MQL | < 14 days | > 30 days |
| MQL → SQL | < 48 hours | > 7 days |
| SQL → Opportunity | < 7 days | > 14 days |
| Opportunity → Close | < 45 days | > 90 days |

## Best Practices for Lead Management

| # | Best Practice | Details |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Start scoring simple** | Begin with 5-10 criteria; add complexity after 3 months of data |
| 2 | **Calibrate with sales feedback** | Monthly: "Were these MQLs actually qualified?" |
| 3 | **Implement score decay** | Reduce scores for inactivity to prevent stale MQLs |
| 4 | **Map content to journey** | Every nurture email must serve a purpose at a specific stage |
| 5 | **Respect frequency** | No more than 2-3 nurture emails/week; include opt-down options |
| 6 | **A/B test everything** | Subject lines, send times, content formats, CTA placement |
| 7 | **Track lifecycle velocity** | If leads stall at a stage, investigate and fix the bottleneck |
| 8 | **Clean lists relentlessly** | Remove bounced, unsubscribed, and permanently dormant contacts quarterly |
| 9 | **Align scoring with revenue** | Validate that high scores correlate with closed-won deals |
