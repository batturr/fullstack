## 20.1 Data Privacy Regulations

### 20.1.1 GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- What is GDPR and who it applies to (EU residents, global scope)
- Key principles: lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization
- Legal bases for processing: consent, legitimate interest, contract, legal obligation
- Rights of data subjects: access, rectification, erasure (right to be forgotten), portability, objection
- Data Protection Officer (DPO) requirements
- Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
- Data breach notification (72-hour requirement)
- Penalties: up to 4% of global annual revenue or €20 million
- Impact on marketing: consent management, email opt-in, cookie policies, tracking

### 20.1.2 CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act/Rights Act)
- What is CCPA/CPRA and who it applies to
- Consumer rights: know, delete, opt-out of sale, non-discrimination, correct, limit
- "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" requirements
- Categories of personal information
- Service provider agreements
- Penalties and enforcement
- Comparison with GDPR: similarities and differences

### 20.1.3 Other Global Privacy Regulations
- ePrivacy Directive (EU) and upcoming ePrivacy Regulation
- UK Data Protection Act (post-Brexit GDPR)
- LGPD (Brazil)
- PIPEDA (Canada)
- PDPA (Singapore, Thailand)
- APPI (Japan)
- PIPL (China)
- State-level US laws: Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut, Utah, Texas
- Preparing for a global privacy compliance framework

### 20.1.4 Cookie Compliance & Consent Management
- Types of cookies: strictly necessary, performance, functional, targeting/advertising
- Cookie consent requirements by jurisdiction
- Cookie banner design and UX best practices
- Consent Management Platforms (CMPs): OneTrust, Cookiebot, TrustArc, Osano
- Google Consent Mode v2 implementation
- Consent mode impact on analytics and advertising
- Server-side tracking and privacy
- Cookie-less tracking alternatives
