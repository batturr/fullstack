# 1.4 Digital Marketing Channels Overview — Comprehensive Notes

---

## The PESO Model (Paid, Earned, Shared, Owned)

Before diving into each channel, understand that every marketing channel falls into one of four categories. The best strategies use all four working together.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                      │
│    PAID ◄────────────── CONVERGE ──────────────► EARNED             │
│    (You pay for it)                              (You earn it)       │
│           ↕                                          ↕              │
│    SHARED ◄────────────── AMPLIFY ──────────────► OWNED             │
│    (Community creates)                           (You control it)    │
│                                                                      │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 1.4.1 Owned Media

### Definition
Channels and assets you own and control completely. These are your long-term digital assets.

### Channel Deep Dive

#### Website & Blog
The headquarters of your digital presence. Every other channel drives traffic here.

**Key elements:**
- Homepage (first impression — 0.05 seconds to form opinion)
- Product/service pages (conversion-focused)
- Blog/content hub (SEO + thought leadership)
- Landing pages (campaign-specific, single-CTA)
- About/team page (trust + brand story)

**Performance benchmarks:**
| Metric | Good | Great | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Page load time | <3 seconds | <2 seconds | <1.5 seconds |
| Bounce rate | 40-55% | 30-40% | <30% |
| Conversion rate | 2-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| Pages per session | 2-3 | 3-5 | 5+ |
| Mobile responsiveness | Responsive | Mobile-first | PWA |

**Real-Time Example — Stripe.com:**
Stripe's website is considered the gold standard in B2B web design:
- Clean, developer-friendly design
- Interactive code examples (show the product in action)
- Case studies with real numbers (Shopify, Amazon, Lyft)
- Documentation as marketing (developers discover Stripe through docs)
- Result: $95B valuation. 70% of revenue attributed to inbound web traffic.

#### Email Lists
The only digital channel you truly own. Social platforms can change algorithms overnight. Your email list is yours.

**Why email is king:**
- Average ROI: $42 for every $1 spent (highest of any channel)
- 4 billion email users globally (more than any social platform)
- You own the data — no algorithm changes
- Direct access to customer's inbox

**Real-Time Example — The Hustle Newsletter:**
- Started as a free daily business newsletter
- Grew to 2.5 million subscribers through a referral program
- Monetized through sponsored content ($20K-50K per sponsor per day)
- Acquired by HubSpot for a reported $27 million in 2021
- Built entirely on owned media (email list)

#### Mobile Apps
A dedicated mobile presence for high-engagement brands.

**When you need an app:**
- Repeat/daily usage (Uber, Spotify, banking)
- Complex features requiring native performance
- Loyalty program integration
- Push notification strategy

**When you DON'T need an app:**
- Informational websites
- Low-frequency purchases
- Small audience (app development costs $50K-500K+)

**Real-Time Example — Starbucks App:**
- 31.2 million active users (25% of US customers)
- Mobile order-ahead reduces wait time → increases visit frequency
- Loyalty rewards drive repeat purchases
- Push notifications: "Your favorite Caramel Macchiato is half off today"
- Holds $1.6 billion in stored value (more than some banks)
- Result: 53% of Starbucks revenue comes through the app

#### Social Media Profiles (Organic)
Your owned presence on rented platforms. You control what you post, but the platform controls who sees it.

**Organic reach reality (2024):**
| Platform | Average Organic Reach | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook Page | 2-5% of followers | Declining steadily |
| Instagram Feed | 5-10% of followers | Declining |
| Instagram Reels | 20-40%+ (non-followers included) | Growing |
| LinkedIn | 5-15% of connections | Stable |
| TikTok | 15-50%+ (non-followers included) | Growing |
| X (Twitter) | 2-5% of followers | Declining |
| YouTube | Based on search/recommendation | Stable (evergreen) |

### Benefits of Owned Media
- **Full control:** You decide the message, design, timing
- **Cost-effective:** After initial setup, marginal cost is low
- **Long-term asset:** Blog posts compound traffic for years; email lists grow over time
- **Data ownership:** You own all visitor/subscriber data (first-party data)
- **Brand building:** Consistent experience across touchpoints

### Owned Media Strategy Matrix

| Asset | Primary Purpose | Secondary Purpose | Key Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| Website | Conversion hub | Brand credibility | Conversion rate |
| Blog | SEO + education | Lead generation | Organic traffic, leads |
| Email list | Nurture + sell | Retention, loyalty | List size, open rate, revenue |
| App | Engagement + retention | Transaction convenience | DAU/MAU, session length |
| Social profiles | Brand awareness | Community building | Engagement rate, follower growth |

## 1.4.2 Earned Media

### Definition
Exposure you earn through quality, reputation, and relationships. You can't buy it directly — you earn it by being worth talking about.

### Channel Deep Dive

#### Organic Search Rankings (SEO)
The most valuable earned media — showing up when people actively search for solutions.

**Why organic search matters:**
- 53% of all website traffic comes from organic search
- 75% of users never scroll past page 1 of Google
- Organic search has 20x more traffic opportunity than PPC
- SEO leads have 14.6% close rate vs. 1.7% for outbound

**Real-Time Example — Zapier:**
Zapier built their entire growth engine on SEO:
- Created 25,000+ landing pages for integration combinations ("connect Gmail to Slack")
- Each page targets a specific long-tail keyword
- 6M+ monthly organic visits
- 95% of revenue driven by organic search
- Zero sales team needed — product-led growth powered by SEO

#### Social Media Shares and Mentions
When your content is so good that people share it voluntarily.

**Factors that drive organic sharing:**
- Emotional resonance (awe, humor, outrage, inspiration)
- Practical value (useful tips, tools, templates)
- Social currency (makes the sharer look smart/funny/in-the-know)
- Story-driven content (narratives over facts)
- Visual format (images shared 40x more than text; video shared 1200x more)

**Real-Time Example — Spotify Wrapped:**
- Users voluntarily share their listening data on every social platform
- 2023: 185M+ users engaged with Wrapped
- Generated 60B+ social impressions (estimated)
- Cost to Spotify: Engineering effort + minimal marketing spend
- Competitors (Apple Music, YouTube Music) had to create their own versions
- Pure earned media — users ARE the marketing

#### Press Coverage and PR
Getting featured in publications, podcasts, and media outlets.

**Digital PR tactics:**
1. **HARO/Connectively:** Respond to journalist queries with expert quotes
2. **Data-driven PR:** Original research/surveys that journalists want to cite
3. **Newsjacking:** Tie your brand to trending news stories
4. **Founder storytelling:** Share your origin story with startup/business media
5. **Product launches:** Coordinate exclusive previews with top journalists

**Real-Time Example — Notion's PR Strategy:**
Notion didn't spend on ads in its early years. Instead:
- Got featured in Product Hunt (#1 product of the day, multiple times)
- Targeted productivity bloggers and YouTubers for organic reviews
- Created a "Notion Template Gallery" that generated user-created press
- Result: Grew from 0 to 30M+ users primarily through earned media

#### Word-of-Mouth and Referrals
The oldest form of marketing — and still the most trusted.

**Statistics:**
- 92% of consumers trust recommendations from friends over any advertising
- Word-of-mouth generates 2x the sales of paid advertising
- Referred customers have 37% higher retention and 16% higher CLV
- 64% of marketing executives rank word-of-mouth as the most effective form of marketing

**Real-Time Example — Tesla:**
Tesla spends $0 on traditional advertising. Their entire growth engine is:
- Product quality → Owner enthusiasm → Social media posts → Media coverage
- Referral program: Owners get credits for referring new buyers
- Elon Musk's personal brand amplifies every product announcement
- Result: $800B+ market cap with $0 ad spend

#### Reviews and Testimonials
User-generated evaluations of your product/service.

**Impact by platform:**
| Platform | Best For | Key Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Google Reviews | Local businesses, service providers | Star rating on Google Business Profile |
| Amazon Reviews | E-commerce products | Star rating + "verified purchase" |
| G2/Capterra | B2B software | Rating + category ranking |
| Yelp | Restaurants, local services | Star rating + photo reviews |
| Trustpilot | E-commerce, consumer services | Trust score |
| App Store/Play Store | Mobile apps | Rating + review count |
| Glassdoor | Employer brand | Company rating (impacts hiring marketing) |

**Real-Time Example — How Airbnb Uses Reviews:**
- Every stay is double-reviewed (host and guest)
- 500M+ cumulative reviews
- Listings with 10+ reviews get 3x more bookings
- Superhost badge (earned through consistent 4.8+ rating) drives 60% more bookings
- Reviews are the primary trust mechanism — Airbnb's entire business depends on earned credibility

### Benefits of Earned Media
- **Highest credibility:** Third-party endorsement > self-promotion
- **Trust multiplier:** Consumers trust earned media 2-3x more than paid media
- **Amplification:** One great review can influence thousands
- **Compounding returns:** Reviews and rankings build over time
- **Cost-efficient:** No direct media spend (though requires time and quality investment)

## 1.4.3 Paid Media

### Definition
Any channel where you pay for visibility, clicks, or conversions. The fastest way to reach your audience, with precise targeting and measurable ROI.

### Channel Deep Dive

#### Search Ads (Google Ads, Microsoft Ads)
Ads that appear when people actively search for a keyword.

**Why search ads are powerful:**
- Intent-based: People are actively looking for a solution
- Google processes 8.5 billion searches per day
- Average ROAS for search ads: 200% (2:1 return)

**Key metrics:**
| Metric | Industry Average | Top Performers |
|---|---|---|
| CTR (Search) | 3.17% | 6%+ |
| CPC | $2.69 | Varies by industry ($1-$50+) |
| Conversion rate | 3.75% | 7%+ |
| Cost per conversion | $48.96 | $20-30 |

**Real-Time Example — A Personal Injury Law Firm:**
- CPC for "personal injury lawyer" = $150-$400 per click
- But average case value = $50,000-$500,000+
- Even at $300/click and 5% conversion rate: $6,000 to acquire a case worth $100K+
- ROI: 1,500%+
- This is why legal, insurance, and finance dominate Google Ads spending

#### Social Media Ads

| Platform | Best For | Average CPM | Unique Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Meta (FB/IG)** | B2C, e-commerce, lead gen | $7-15 | Most advanced targeting, massive reach |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B, SaaS, recruiting | $30-80 | Job title/company/industry targeting |
| **TikTok** | Gen Z, brand awareness | $6-10 | Native-feeling ads, viral potential |
| **X (Twitter)** | B2B, tech, media | $5-10 | Real-time conversation targeting |
| **Pinterest** | E-commerce, lifestyle, wedding | $5-8 | High purchase intent (users plan purchases) |
| **Snapchat** | Gen Z, apps, entertainment | $3-7 | AR filters, interactive formats |
| **YouTube** | Brand awareness, education | $10-20 | 6-second bumper to 60+ second formats |

**Real-Time Example — Glossier's Meta Ads Strategy:**
- Budget: Estimated $20-30M/year on Meta ads
- Strategy: UGC-style creative (looks like a friend's Instagram post, not an ad)
- Targeting: Lookalike audiences based on existing customers
- Result: 80% of revenue from returning customers acquired through social ads
- Key insight: Their ads intentionally look "not like ads" — authentic beats polished

#### Display & Programmatic Advertising
Automated buying and placement of banner/video ads across millions of websites.

**How programmatic works:**
```
Advertiser → Demand-Side Platform (DSP) → Ad Exchange ← Supply-Side Platform (SSP) ← Publisher
                                    ↑
                              Real-Time Bidding
                          (auction in milliseconds)
```

**Targeting options:**
- Contextual: Ads appear on relevant content pages
- Behavioral: Based on user's browsing history
- Retargeting: Reach people who visited your site
- Lookalike: Find new users similar to your customers
- Demographic: Age, gender, income
- Geographic: Location-based

**Real-Time Example — Retargeting in Action (Amazon):**
You browse a specific laptop on Amazon. Over the next 7 days:
- You see that exact laptop in banner ads on ESPN.com
- It appears in your Facebook feed
- It shows up in Gmail ads
- You get a push notification: "Price drop on your recently viewed item"
- Amazon's retargeting converts at 26% (vs. 2-3% for cold traffic)

#### Native Advertising
Paid content that matches the form and function of the platform it appears on.

**Types:**
- Sponsored articles on news sites (Forbes BrandVoice)
- Promoted posts in social feeds
- Recommended content widgets (Taboola, Outbrain)
- Sponsored podcast segments

**Real-Time Example — Netflix x The New York Times:**
Netflix sponsored a NYT interactive article "Women Inmates: Why the Male Model Doesn't Work" to promote Orange is the New Black. It was a genuine, high-quality piece of journalism that happened to align with the show's themes. Result: 1M+ page views, widespread praise for quality native advertising.

#### Sponsored Content & Influencer Partnerships
Paying influencers or content creators to promote your product.

**Influencer tiers:**

| Tier | Followers | Average Cost/Post | Engagement Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nano** | 1K-10K | $10-100 | 5-10% | Hyper-niche, local |
| **Micro** | 10K-100K | $100-1,000 | 3-7% | Niche authority |
| **Mid** | 100K-500K | $1,000-5,000 | 2-5% | Brand awareness |
| **Macro** | 500K-1M | $5,000-25,000 | 1-3% | Mass reach |
| **Mega** | 1M+ | $25,000-500K+ | 0.5-2% | Celebrity endorsement |

**Real-Time Example — Daniel Wellington's Influencer Strategy:**
- Gave free watches to thousands of micro-influencers on Instagram
- Each got a unique discount code (tracking + incentive)
- Spent $0 on traditional advertising
- Result: Built a $230M/year watch brand entirely through influencer marketing
- At peak, #danielwellington had 2.4M+ Instagram posts

### Benefits of Paid Media
- **Immediate results:** Traffic within hours of launching
- **Precise targeting:** Reach exact demographics, behaviors, interests
- **Scalable:** Increase budget to increase reach proportionally
- **Testable:** A/B test creatives, audiences, placements in real-time
- **Measurable:** Track every dollar from impression to conversion

## 1.4.4 Shared Media

### Definition
Content created through collaboration between brands and their communities. The line between creator and consumer blurs.

### Channel Deep Dive

#### Social Media Communities
Brand-owned or brand-affiliated community spaces.

**Types of brand communities:**
- Facebook Groups (controlled community)
- Discord servers (real-time community)
- Slack communities (professional community)
- Reddit subreddits (organic community)
- Circle/Mighty Networks (paid community platforms)

**Real-Time Example — Peloton Community:**
- Official Facebook Group: 400K+ members
- Members share ride stats, celebrate milestones, support each other
- User-generated challenges (#PelotonPR, #PelotonCentury)
- Community drives 70% of Peloton's retention
- Churn rate for community members is 0.65% vs. 3.2% for non-community members

#### User-Generated Content (UGC)
Content created by customers about your brand.

**UGC statistics:**
- 79% of people say UGC highly impacts their purchasing decisions
- UGC is 9.8x more impactful than influencer content for purchasing decisions
- UGC photos convert 5x more than professional photography on product pages
- Brands using UGC see 29% higher web conversions

**Real-Time Example — GoPro:**
GoPro's entire marketing strategy IS user-generated content:
- GoPro Awards: Pays users for their best footage
- YouTube channel: Most content is user-submitted
- Social: #GoPro has 50M+ posts on Instagram
- The product sells itself when users share incredible footage
- Marketing spend is fractional compared to competitors because customers ARE the marketing team

#### Co-Marketing and Partnerships
Two brands collaborating to reach each other's audiences.

**Types:**
- Joint webinars/events
- Co-authored content (ebooks, reports)
- Bundled offers
- Integration partnerships
- Cross-promotional campaigns

**Real-Time Example — Uber x Spotify:**
- Users can connect Spotify to Uber and play their music during rides
- Both brands promoted the partnership to their audiences
- Uber gets differentiation from Lyft
- Spotify gets usage context and exposure
- Win-win shared media with cross-promotional value

#### Forums and Community Platforms (Reddit, Quora)

**Reddit marketing:**
- 52M+ daily active users
- Subreddits for every niche imaginable
- Authenticity is mandatory — overt marketing is downvoted/banned
- Strategy: Answer questions genuinely, share knowledge, subtle brand mentions
- r/technology, r/marketing, r/entrepreneur are goldmines for B2B brands

**Quora marketing:**
- 300M+ monthly users
- Long-form answers rank in Google search results
- Strategy: Answer industry questions with expertise, link to relevant blog posts
- Answers can drive traffic for years (evergreen)

**Real-Time Example — Ahrefs on Quora:**
Ahrefs' CMO Tim Soulo answered 100+ questions about SEO on Quora over 2 years:
- Each answer subtly referenced Ahrefs data or tools
- Answers now rank in Google for competitive SEO keywords
- Estimated 50K+ monthly visits to Ahrefs from Quora answers
- Cost: Time only (no ad spend)

### Benefits of Shared Media
- **Community trust:** Peer recommendations are most trusted
- **Organic amplification:** Community members share with their networks
- **User insights:** Direct access to customer feedback and ideas
- **Lower cost:** Community-driven content is free or low-cost
- **Retention:** Community members have 2-3x higher retention

|---|---|---|

## Implementation Steps for Section 1.4

### Step 1: Audit Your Current Channel Mix (Week 1)
- List every active channel (paid, owned, earned, shared)
- Rate each: performance (1-5), investment ($/time), growth trend
- Identify your strongest and weakest channels
- **Tool:** Spreadsheet template with PESO framework

### Step 2: Identify Channel Gaps (Week 1-2)
- Compare your channel mix to competitors
- Identify high-potential channels you're not using
- Prioritize based on audience presence + business fit
- **Tool:** SimilarWeb for traffic sources, SparkToro for audience platforms

### Step 3: Build Your Owned Media Foundation (Week 2-4)
- Optimize website for conversion (speed, mobile, CTAs)
- Start/grow email list with lead magnets
- Establish consistent social posting schedule
- **Tool:** WordPress/Webflow for website, Mailchimp/ConvertKit for email

### Step 4: Develop an Earned Media Strategy (Week 3-4)
- Set up Google Alerts for brand mentions
- Claim and optimize Google Business Profile
- Create a review generation system (post-purchase email requesting review)
- Start a PR/content outreach plan
- **Tool:** Google Alerts (free), Birdeye/Podium for reviews

### Step 5: Test Paid Channels (Month 2)
- Start with one paid channel where your audience is concentrated
- Test with small budget ($500-1,000/month)
- Run 3-5 ad variations to find winning creative/audience
- **Tool:** Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, LinkedIn Campaign Manager

### Step 6: Build Community (Month 2-3)
- Launch a brand community (Facebook Group, Discord, or Slack)
- Encourage UGC with branded hashtags and incentives
- Engage in industry forums (Reddit, Quora) with genuine expertise
- **Tool:** Facebook Groups, Discord, Circle.so

## Strategies

1. **The 70-20-10 Channel Rule:** 70% of budget on proven channels, 20% on emerging channels, 10% on experimental.
2. **Owned-First Strategy:** Build your owned media foundation (website, email, blog) before investing heavily in paid. Owned compounds; paid doesn't.
3. **Earned Media Snowball:** Every piece of owned content should be designed to earn shares, links, and mentions. Write for shareability, not just SEO.
4. **Community-Led Growth:** Invest in building a genuine community around your product. Community members are 2-3x more likely to upgrade, renew, and refer.
5. **Channel Arbitrage:** Find channels where your audience is present but competitors aren't investing. Example: B2B brands on TikTok in 2023-2024 had very low CPMs because few B2B advertisers were there.

---

## Best Practices

1. **Don't build your house on rented land.** Social platforms change algorithms constantly. Always drive traffic back to owned channels.
2. **Match the channel to the funnel stage.** Display ads for awareness; search ads for intent; email for nurture; retargeting for conversion.
3. **Repurpose across channels.** One blog post = 10 social posts + 1 email + 1 video + 3 Quora answers. Create once, distribute many.
4. **Measure channel synergy, not just individual performance.** A display ad may not convert directly but assists search conversions. Attribution matters.
5. **Earn the right to sell.** Give value first (content, community, education). Ask for the sale after trust is built.
6. **Test new channels with minimum viable commitment.** 90-day test with clear KPIs before scaling or cutting.

## Additional Insights

### The Content Distribution Formula
Most marketers spend 80% on creation and 20% on distribution. Flip it:
- **20% creation, 80% distribution**
- One exceptional piece of content distributed across 10 channels > 10 mediocre pieces on 1 channel

### The Channel Lifecycle
Every marketing channel follows a lifecycle:
1. **Emergence:** Early adopters get massive results at low cost (Facebook Ads 2012, TikTok Ads 2020)
2. **Growth:** More advertisers enter; costs rise but ROI is still strong
3. **Maturity:** Channel is saturated; costs are high; optimization is critical
4. **Decline:** Users migrate; effectiveness drops; smart marketers move to the next emerging channel

The key: Be early to emerging channels, optimize on mature channels, and exit declining channels before competitors.

### The Owned Media Compound Effect
A blog post published today continues to drive traffic for 3-5 years. An email subscriber acquired today can generate revenue for years. Compare this to a paid ad that stops driving results the moment you stop paying.

**Example math:**
- **Paid ad:** $1,000 spend → 500 clicks → 15 sales → $0 ongoing value when spend stops
- **Blog post:** $500 to produce → 100 visits/month for 3 years = 3,600 total visits → 108 sales → continues compounding
