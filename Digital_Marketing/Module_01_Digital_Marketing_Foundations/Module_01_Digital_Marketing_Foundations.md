- The See-Think-Do-Care framework

### 1.2.3 Psychology of Persuasion in Digital Marketing

## 1.4 Digital Marketing Channels Overview

### 1.4.1 Owned Media

- Website and blog
- Email lists
- Mobile apps
- Social media profiles
- Benefits: full control, cost-effective, long-term asset

### 1.4.2 Earned Media

- Organic search rankings
- Social media shares and mentions
- Press coverage and PR
- Word-of-mouth and referrals
- Reviews and testimonials
- Benefits: credibility, trust, amplification

### 1.4.3 Paid Media

- Search ads (Google, Bing)
- Social media ads (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, X)
- Display and programmatic advertising
- Native advertising
- Sponsored content and influencer partnerships
- Benefits: immediate reach, precise targeting, scalable

### 1.4.4 Shared Media

- Social media communities
- User-generated content
- Co-marketing and partnerships
- Forums and community platforms (Reddit, Quora)
- Benefits: community trust, organic amplification

## 1.5 Setting Goals, KPIs & Measurement Frameworks

### 1.5.1 Goal Setting Frameworks

- SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) for marketing teams
- North Star Metric concept
- Aligning marketing goals with business objectives
- Short-term vs. long-term goal balance

### 1.5.2 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Awareness KPIs: impressions, reach, brand searches, share of voice
- Engagement KPIs: clicks, CTR, time on page, bounce rate, social interactions
- Conversion KPIs: conversion rate, cost per acquisition, lead quality score
- Revenue KPIs: ROI, ROAS, customer lifetime value (CLV), average order value
- Retention KPIs: churn rate, repeat purchase rate, net promoter score (NPS)

### 1.5.3 Measurement Frameworks

- Google's See-Think-Do-Care measurement framework
- The Pirate Metrics (AARRR): Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue
- Attribution models: first-touch, last-touch, linear, time-decay, data-driven
- Incrementality testing
- Marketing mix modeling (MMM)

### 1.5.4 Reporting & Dashboards

- Building a marketing dashboard
- Reporting cadence: daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly
- Storytelling with data
- Executive reporting vs. tactical reporting
- Tools: Google Data Studio (Looker Studio), Tableau, Power BI, Databox
