# Module 25: Capstone Project & Portfolio

---

## 25.1 Capstone Project Overview

### 25.1.1 Project Purpose
- Demonstrating comprehensive digital marketing skills
- Integrating all 24 modules into a cohesive real-world project
- Creating portfolio-worthy work samples
- Simulating real-world marketing challenges and decision-making
- Building confidence through end-to-end campaign execution

### 25.1.2 Project Options
- **Option A: Full Campaign for a Real Business** — Partner with a local business or startup
- **Option B: Simulated Launch Campaign** — Create a fictional product/brand and execute a full launch
- **Option C: Digital Marketing Audit & Strategy** — Comprehensive audit of an existing business with strategic recommendations
- **Option D: Personal Brand Launch** — Build your own personal brand from scratch with measurable results
- **Option E: E-commerce Store Launch** — Build and market an e-commerce store with real products

### 25.1.3 Project Timeline
- Weeks 1-2: Research, strategy, and planning
- Weeks 3-4: Asset creation and setup
- Weeks 5-8: Campaign execution and optimization
- Weeks 9-10: Analysis, reporting, and portfolio documentation
- Week 11: Peer review and presentation
- Week 12: Final submission and feedback

---

## 25.2 Capstone Project Requirements

### 25.2.1 Strategy & Planning Deliverables
- Market research and competitive analysis document
- 3 detailed buyer personas
- SWOT analysis
- Marketing goals and KPIs (SMART framework)
- Channel strategy with budget allocation
- Campaign calendar (minimum 8-week plan)
- Measurement and attribution plan

### 25.2.2 Execution Deliverables
- **Website/Landing Pages**: minimum 1 optimized landing page or website section
- **SEO**: keyword research, on-page optimization for 5+ pages, technical audit
- **Content**: minimum 5 pieces (blog posts, videos, infographics, ebooks, or similar)
- **Social Media**: 3+ platform setup and 30+ published posts with engagement
- **Email Marketing**: list building, 1 automation sequence (3+ emails), 2+ broadcast campaigns
- **Paid Advertising**: at least 1 paid campaign (Google Ads, Meta Ads, or LinkedIn Ads)
- **Analytics**: GA4 setup, conversion tracking, UTM tagging

### 25.2.3 Optional Advanced Deliverables (Choose 3+)
- CRO: A/B test on a landing page or key page
- Video marketing: 3+ marketing videos (YouTube, Reels, TikTok)
- Influencer outreach: identify and reach out to 10+ influencers
- Marketing automation workflow
- Lead scoring model
- Local SEO optimization (Google Business Profile)
- Affiliate program setup
- PR campaign with media outreach
- Interactive content piece (quiz, calculator, assessment)
- Mobile marketing campaign (SMS, push notifications)
- AI-generated content experiment with quality comparison

## 25.3 Campaign Execution Guidance

### 25.3.1 Week-by-Week Execution Plan
- **Week 1**: Research, persona development, competitive analysis, goal setting
- **Week 2**: Strategy document, channel selection, budget planning, content calendar
- **Week 3**: Website/landing page build, social profile setup, analytics implementation
- **Week 4**: Content creation sprint, email platform setup, ad account configuration
- **Week 5**: Campaign launch (content publishing, social posting, email sends)
- **Week 6**: Paid advertising launch, community engagement, outreach begin
- **Week 7**: Mid-campaign optimization, A/B testing, content refresh
- **Week 8**: Continued optimization, advanced deliverable execution
- **Week 9**: Campaign wind-down, data collection, analysis begins
- **Week 10**: Report creation, case study writing, portfolio assembly
- **Week 11**: Peer review presentations, feedback incorporation
- **Week 12**: Final submission, celebration

### 25.3.2 Quality Standards
- All content must be original and of professional quality
- Data must be accurately tracked and reported
- Strategic decisions must be supported by research and data
- Design must be brand-consistent and visually professional
- Writing must be error-free and audience-appropriate
- All campaigns must comply with platform policies and legal requirements

## 25.4 Analysis & Reporting

### 25.4.1 Data Analysis Framework
- Performance by channel: traffic, engagement, conversions
- Funnel analysis: awareness → consideration → conversion
- Attribution analysis: which channels contributed to goals
- ROI/ROAS calculation for paid campaigns
- Content performance ranking and insights
- Audience insights: who engaged, from where, on what devices
- Comparison to industry benchmarks
- What worked, what didn't, and why

### 25.4.2 Campaign Report Structure
- Executive summary (1 page)
- Objectives and KPIs recap
- Strategy overview
- Channel-by-channel performance analysis
- Overall campaign performance dashboard
- Key insights and learnings
- Recommendations for future campaigns
- Appendices: raw data, screenshots, examples

### 25.4.3 Storytelling with Results
- Turning data into narratives
- Visualizing results effectively
- Highlighting wins and acknowledging areas for improvement
- Connecting marketing results to business impact
- Presenting to different audiences: technical vs. executive

## 25.5 Portfolio Development

### 25.5.1 Case Study Writing
- Case study structure: Challenge → Strategy → Execution → Results
- Writing compelling case study narratives
- Quantifying results with specific metrics
- Including visuals: screenshots, charts, before/after
- Showing your process and thinking, not just outcomes
- Including client testimonials (if real client)
- Tailoring case studies for different audiences (potential employers vs. clients)

### 25.5.2 Portfolio Website
- Portfolio website must-haves: about, services/skills, case studies, blog, contact
- Showcasing diverse project types and skills
- Before-and-after demonstrations
- Results-focused presentation
- Mobile-responsive and fast-loading
- SEO optimization for personal brand searches
- Call-to-action for opportunities

### 25.5.3 Presentation & Defense
- Presenting your capstone project to peers and evaluators
- Slide deck design for marketing presentations
- Handling Q&A and defending strategic decisions
- Demonstrating strategic thinking, not just execution
- Receiving and incorporating feedback
- Peer evaluation criteria and rubric

## 25.6 Final Assessment

### 25.6.1 Grading Criteria
- **Strategy Quality (20%)**: research depth, goal clarity, channel rationale, audience understanding
- **Execution Excellence (30%)**: content quality, campaign setup, technical implementation, creativity
- **Data & Analytics (20%)**: tracking setup, data accuracy, analysis depth, attribution
- **Results & Optimization (15%)**: performance against KPIs, optimization decisions, testing rigor
- **Presentation & Documentation (15%)**: report quality, case study writing, visual presentation, portfolio

### 25.6.2 Peer Review Process
- Structured peer feedback forms
- Constructive criticism guidelines
- Cross-pollination of ideas and approaches
- Collaborative learning from different industries/niches

### 25.6.3 Continuous Improvement Plan
- Personal development areas identified
- 6-month learning plan post-course
- Skill gaps to address
- Certifications to pursue
- Portfolio maintenance and update schedule
- Community and networking plan
- Staying current with industry changes
