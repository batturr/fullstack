## 23.4.1 Connected TV (CTV) & OTT Advertising

### What Is CTV & OTT

**CTV (Connected TV):** Any television that connects to the internet and can stream digital content — smart TVs, streaming devices (Roku, Fire TV, Apple TV), and gaming consoles (PlayStation, Xbox).

**OTT (Over-The-Top):** Content delivered directly to viewers via the internet, bypassing traditional cable and satellite providers. Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, and YouTube TV are OTT services.

**The Relationship:**
```
OTT = the content/service (Netflix, Hulu, YouTube)
CTV = the device that plays it (Roku, Fire TV, Smart TV)

Users watch OTT content ON CTV devices.
Both terms are often used interchangeably in advertising contexts.
```

**Why CTV matters in 2025–2026:**
- 88% of U.S. households have at least one CTV device
- CTV ad spending exceeded $30 billion in 2025 (eMarketer)
- Traditional TV ad spend is declining 5–8% annually
- CTV offers TV-quality reach WITH digital-quality targeting and measurement
- Streaming now accounts for 40%+ of total TV viewing time (Nielsen)

### CTV Landscape

| Category | Platforms/Devices | Ad Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| **Streaming Sticks/Boxes** | Roku, Amazon Fire TV, Apple TV, Chromecast | Platform-level ads, app home screen ads |
| **Smart TVs** | Samsung (Tizen), LG (webOS), Vizio (SmartCast) | Native smart TV ad placements, home screen |
| **Gaming Consoles** | PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch | In-game ads, app advertising |
| **AVOD (Ad-Supported VOD)** | Tubi, Pluto TV, Freevee, YouTube | Free content with ads — growing fast |
| **SVOD with Ads** | Netflix (ads tier), Disney+ (ads tier), Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+ | Premium content with limited ad loads |
| **vMVPDs** | YouTube TV, Hulu + Live TV, Sling TV, FuboTV | Live TV alternative with digital targeting |
| **FAST (Free Ad-Supported TV)** | Pluto TV, Tubi, Samsung TV+, Roku Channel | Linear-style channels, free to viewer |

### Streaming Ad Platforms Deep Dive

#### Netflix Ads Tier
- Launched November 2022; over 40 million global ad-supported subscribers by 2025
- CPMs range $20–$65 (premium pricing)
- Limited ad load: 4–5 minutes per hour (vs. 15–18 minutes on traditional TV)
- Targeting: age, gender, country, content genre, daypart
- Measurement: Nielsen ONE, Integral Ad Science verification
- **Best for:** Brand awareness with premium content association

#### Disney+ Ads Tier
- Launched December 2022; rapid adoption with 50%+ of new subscribers choosing ads tier
- Offers contextual targeting aligned with Disney's content catalog (family, action, drama)
- Brand-safe environment — Disney curates ad placements
- **Best for:** Family-focused brands, entertainment, consumer goods

#### YouTube (CTV)
- YouTube on TV screens is the fastest-growing segment — 150 million monthly CTV viewers in the U.S.
- Full Google Ads targeting on TV screens: demographics, interests, custom intent, remarketing
- YouTube Shorts now available on CTV
- **Best for:** Performance-oriented brands that want TV reach with digital targeting

**Real-Time Example:**
- **Samsung** used CTV advertising across Hulu, Roku, and YouTube TV to launch the Galaxy S24 Ultra. They used household-level targeting to reach tech-forward households, served non-skippable 15-second pre-roll during premium content, and measured with Nielsen DAR (Digital Ad Ratings). Result: 28% incremental reach beyond traditional TV, 3.2x higher ad recall than display, and a 14% lift in purchase consideration tracked via brand lift studies.

### CTV Ad Formats

| Format | Description | Engagement Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Roll** | Ad plays before content starts | Moderate (captive but expected) | Brand awareness, movie trailers |
| **Mid-Roll** | Ad plays during content break | Highest (viewer is invested) | Product launches, consideration |
| **Post-Roll** | Ad plays after content ends | Lowest (viewer may leave) | Retargeting, CTA-driven |
| **Non-Skippable** | Forced full view (15–30 seconds) | High completion rate (95%+) | Brand messages, product demos |
| **Interactive** | Viewer can click/select/engage with overlay | 5–10x standard engagement | QR code scan, product exploration |
| **Shoppable** | QR code or click-to-purchase overlay | 2–5% scan rate | Direct response, e-commerce |
| **Pause Ads** | Ad appears when viewer pauses content | Non-intrusive, high attention | Gentle brand reminders |
| **Companion Ads** | Second-screen ad on phone synced with TV ad | Cross-device engagement | App installs, website visits |

### Audience Targeting for CTV

CTV combines the broad reach of TV with the precision targeting of digital.

| Targeting Type | Description | Data Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Demographic** | Age, gender, income, household size | Platform data, census-mapped |
| **Behavioral** | Past viewing habits, content preferences | Streaming platform data |
| **Contextual** | Align ads with content genre/mood | Content metadata |
| **Geographic** | DMA, zip code, city, state | IP geolocation, device registration |
| **First-Party Data** | Your own CRM data matched to CTV households | Data onboarding (LiveRamp, Experian) |
| **Interest-Based** | Segments based on web browsing + app usage | Cross-device data partners |
| **Purchase-Based** | Target by past purchase behavior | Data partners (NCS, IRI, Catalina) |
| **Lookalike** | Find CTV households similar to your best customers | DSP modeling |
| **ACR (Automatic Content Recognition)** | Target based on what viewers watched (even on traditional TV) | Smart TV data (Samba TV, Vizio Inscape) |

### Programmatic CTV Buying

Programmatic CTV allows you to buy CTV inventory through automated platforms (DSPs) with real-time bidding.

**How Programmatic CTV Works:**
```
Advertiser →  DSP (Demand-Side Platform)  →  SSP/Exchange  →  CTV Publisher/App
              (The Trade Desk, DV360,       (Magnite,         (Hulu, Pluto TV,
               Amazon DSP)                  SpotX, FreeWheel)  Roku Channel)
```

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Define campaign goals: reach, frequency, completion rate, or performance (site visit, purchase)
2. Select DSP: The Trade Desk (industry leader for CTV), Google DV360, Amazon DSP
3. Upload or build audience segments (first-party data, third-party segments)
4. Set frequency caps to prevent ad fatigue (recommend: 3–5 exposures per household per week)
5. Choose inventory sources: premium (PMP deals) vs. open exchange
6. Create CTV-specific creatives (15s and 30s non-skippable, consider interactive/QR)
7. Launch, monitor, optimize daily
8. Measure: completion rate, reach, frequency, brand lift, conversion attribution

### CTV Measurement

| Metric | What It Measures | Industry Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **Video Completion Rate (VCR)** | % who watched the full ad | 90–97% (CTV is very high) |
| **Reach** | Unique households exposed | Varies by budget |
| **Frequency** | Avg exposures per household | Target: 3–7 per week |
| **Incremental Reach** | Reach beyond traditional TV | Typically 20–40% incremental |
| **Brand Lift** | Awareness/consideration change | 5–15% lift is strong |
| **Conversion Attribution** | Visits, purchases after CTV exposure | Multi-touch attribution models |
| **Cost Per Completed View (CPCV)** | Cost for a fully watched ad | $0.015–$0.04 |
| **CPM** | Cost per 1,000 impressions | $20–$50 (varies by platform) |

**Real-Time Example:**
- **DoorDash** used CTV on The Trade Desk to reach cord-cutters aged 25–44 who order food delivery. They ran 15-second non-skippable ads on Hulu, Pluto TV, and Tubi. Using cross-device attribution, they tracked users who saw the CTV ad → then opened the DoorDash app or visited the website within 7 days. Result: 32% higher conversion rate among CTV-exposed users vs. control, 22% lower cost-per-acquisition than traditional TV, and 95% video completion rate.

### Cross-Screen Strategies: CTV + Mobile + Desktop

The power of CTV is amplified when coordinated with other screens.

**Cross-Screen Strategy:**
```
Step 1: CTV ad builds awareness (big screen, premium content)
Step 2: Mobile retargeting deepens engagement (social, display, search)
Step 3: Desktop/mobile conversion captures the sale (landing page, checkout)

Measurement: Cross-device identity graph links all touchpoints
```

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Use a DSP that supports cross-device targeting (The Trade Desk, DV360)
2. Build a unified audience segment across CTV, mobile, and desktop
3. Sequence messaging: awareness (CTV) → consideration (mobile video) → conversion (search/display)
4. Use device graph partners (LiveRamp, TransUnion) to link CTV household to mobile/desktop
5. Measure holistic campaign attribution across all screens

### CTV for Performance Marketing

CTV is no longer just for branding — performance marketers are now using it for direct response.

**Tactics:**
- **QR codes on CTV ads** — viewer scans with phone → lands on landing page (bridges TV-sized screen to mobile action)
- **Companion ads** — synced mobile push when TV ad airs
- **Shoppable CTV** — interactive overlays with product browsing
- **Retargeting from CTV** — expose on CTV → retarget on mobile/desktop with conversion-focused ads

**Real-Time Example:**
- **Coinbase Super Bowl Ad (2022)** — a simple QR code bounced around the screen for 60 seconds during the Super Bowl. 20 million people scanned it in 60 seconds, crashing Coinbase's website. Though this was traditional TV, it proved the QR-code-on-TV concept. CTV advertisers now regularly include QR codes — with 3–5% scan rates (vs. <1% for display ad CTR).

### Gap Analysis for CTV

| Common Gap | Impact | How to Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No CTV in media mix | Missing 88% of streaming households | Allocate 15–25% of video budget to CTV |
| Using TV creative on CTV | Wastes interactive capabilities | Create CTV-specific creative with QR codes, interactivity |
| No frequency capping | Ad fatigue and negative brand perception | Set 3–5 exposures per household per week |
| No cross-screen attribution | Can't measure CTV's contribution to conversions | Implement cross-device identity solution (LiveRamp) |
| Only buying open exchange | Low-quality, non-premium inventory | Negotiate PMP (Private Marketplace) deals with premium publishers |
| No first-party data integration | Missing personalized targeting on CTV | Onboard CRM data to DSP via LiveRamp or direct match |

### Best Practices

1. **CTV-specific creative** — don't repurpose 60-second TV spots; create 15s and 30s ads optimized for streaming
2. **Frequency cap religiously** — CTV completion rates are 95%+, meaning users see your full ad every time; overexposure happens fast
3. **Mix premium + FAST** — premium (Netflix, Hulu) for brand safety; FAST (Tubi, Pluto) for reach efficiency
4. **Include QR codes** — bridge the big screen to mobile; track scans as direct response metric
5. **Layer first-party data** — CRM audiences on CTV create powerful targeting
6. **Measure incrementality** — test CTV's additive reach beyond traditional TV and digital
7. **Test interactive formats** — pause ads, shoppable overlays, and choice-based ads drive higher engagement

## 23.4.2 Podcast & Audio Advertising

### Podcast Advertising Landscape

Podcasting has evolved from niche to mainstream — and so has its advertising ecosystem.

**Market Stats (2025–2026):**
- 500+ million global podcast listeners (Spotify)
- U.S. podcast ad revenue exceeded $4 billion in 2025
- 67% of podcast listeners say they can recall brands advertised on podcasts
- Average podcast listener consumes 8 podcasts per week
- Podcast listeners are 54% more likely to consider advertised brands (Edison Research)

### Ad Types

| Ad Type | Description | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Host-Read** | Host personally endorses the product in their own words | Highest trust, feels authentic, native | Expensive, less control over messaging |
| **Pre-Produced** | Brand-produced audio ad inserted into the show | Full creative control, consistent messaging | Feels more like a traditional ad |
| **Programmatic** | Dynamically inserted based on audience data | Scalable, targetable, measurable | Less personal, may feel disruptive |

### Podcast Ad Placement

| Placement | Timing | Listener Attention | CPM Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Roll** | First 30–60 seconds | Moderate (listeners may skip) | $15–$25 |
| **Mid-Roll** | During the episode (natural break) | Highest (listener is engaged) | $25–$50 |
| **Post-Roll** | Last 30–60 seconds | Lowest (many stop before end) | $8–$15 |

**Real-Time Example:**
- **Athletic Greens (AG1)** became the most ubiquitous podcast advertiser by focusing exclusively on host-read mid-roll ads. They sponsor 500+ podcasts with hosts personally describing their morning AG1 routine. The key: every host uses a unique URL (drinkag1.com/[host-name]) and promo code. Result: AG1 grew from a niche supplement to a $600 million brand — podcasts are their #1 customer acquisition channel, with a lower CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) than any other paid media.

### Podcast Ad Networks

| Network | Scale | Key Feature | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spotify for Podcasters (Megaphone)** | Largest in the world | Streaming Ad Insertion (SAI), first-party data | Brands wanting scale + data |
| **iHeart Media** | 850+ podcasts, massive reach | Cross-platform (radio + podcast) | Audio-first brands |
| **Acast** | Global network, open marketplace | Self-serve buying, programmatic | SMBs and mid-market |
| **SXM Media** | Pandora + SiriusXM + Stitcher | Cross-platform audio | Premium audio placements |
| **Podcorn** | Marketplace for host-read ads | Direct-to-creator, transparent pricing | Authentic host-read campaigns |
| **Magellan AI** | Intelligence platform | Competitive podcast ad tracking | Research and strategy |

### Dynamic Ad Insertion (DAI) vs. Baked-In Ads

| Feature | Dynamic Ad Insertion | Baked-In (Embedded) |
|---|---|---|
| **How it works** | Ads inserted at download/stream time; can change | Ads recorded as part of the episode permanently |
| **Targeting** | By geo, device, demographics, time of day | No targeting — same ad for every listener |
| **Freshness** | New ads can replace old ones on back catalog | Ad is permanent; even old episodes have original ad |
| **Measurement** | Impression-level tracking, programmatic reporting | Based on download numbers, less granular |
| **Brand safety** | Can avoid specific episodes/topics | Locked to the full episode association |
| **Best for** | Programmatic, performance, seasonal campaigns | Authentic host-read endorsements, long-term brand building |

### Audio Branding: Sonic Identity

Sonic identity is the audio equivalent of visual branding — a recognizable collection of sounds, jingles, and audio logos that make your brand instantly identifiable by ear.

**Elements of Sonic Identity:**

| Element | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Audio Logo / Sonic Logo** | 2–5 second sound signature | Netflix "ta-dum," Intel bong, McDonald's "ba da ba ba ba" |
| **Jingle** | Short, catchy musical phrase with lyrics | Kit Kat "break me off a piece," Folgers "best part of waking up" |
| **Brand Music** | Full musical composition for campaigns | Apple's use of curated indie music in product launches |
| **Voice** | Consistent brand voice/tone across audio | Siri's voice, Alexa's voice, Progressive's Flo |
| **Sound Effects** | Branded UI sounds | iPhone keyboard click, Slack notification sound |
| **Hold Music / IVR** | Branded audio in customer service | Custom hold music reflecting brand personality |

**Real-Time Example:**
- **Mastercard** launched a comprehensive sonic identity in 2019 — a melodic signature that plays across ads, in-store POS transactions, and their app. When you tap to pay with Mastercard, you hear their audio logo. Result: 77% unaided recall of the Mastercard sound after just 3 months, making it one of the most recognized sonic brands alongside Intel and Netflix. Mastercard now uses AI to generate culturally adapted versions for different markets.

### Measuring Audio & Podcast Ad Effectiveness

| Metric | Description | How to Track |
|---|---|---|
| **Downloads / Impressions** | How many times the ad-containing episode was downloaded/streamed | Hosting platform (Spotify, Megaphone) |
| **Listen-Through Rate (LTR)** | % of listeners who heard the full ad | Spotify SAI, podtrac |
| **Unique URL Visits** | Visits to podcast-specific landing page (e.g., brand.com/podcast-name) | Google Analytics 4 |
| **Promo Code Redemptions** | How many used the podcast-specific discount code | E-commerce platform |
| **Brand Lift** | Change in awareness, consideration, intent | Pre/post survey (Spotify Brand Lift, Nielsen) |
| **Attribution** | Track ad exposure → conversion | Podsights (Spotify), Chartable, ArtsAI |
| **Pixel-Based Attribution** | Track podcast listener across web/app | Podsights pixel, Claritas |

### Implementation Steps for Podcast Advertising

1. **Define objective:** brand awareness, consideration, or direct response
2. **Research podcasts:** identify shows that align with your target audience (use Magellan AI, Chartable)
3. **Choose ad type:** host-read (highest trust) vs. programmatic (maximum scale)
4. **Negotiate or self-serve:** direct deals for top shows; use Spotify Ad Studio or Acast for self-serve
5. **Create unique tracking:** custom URL + promo code per show
6. **Provide talking points, not scripts:** for host-read, give key messages but let the host be authentic
7. **Run for 4–8 weeks minimum:** podcast advertising builds over time (listeners hear it repeatedly)
8. **Measure multi-dimensionally:** combine promo codes, attribution pixels, and brand lift studies
9. **Scale winners:** double down on shows with strong performance; cut underperformers

## 23.4.3 Digital Out-of-Home (DOOH)

### What Is Programmatic DOOH

DOOH (Digital Out-of-Home) is digital advertising displayed on screens in public spaces — digital billboards, transit displays, elevators, gas stations, gyms, malls, airports, and street furniture. Programmatic DOOH allows buying this inventory through automated platforms with targeting, scheduling, and real-time optimization.

**Why DOOH is emerging (not traditional):**
- Programmatic buying makes it accessible to any advertiser (not just enterprise)
- Real-time targeting by time, weather, traffic, events, and mobile data
- Integration with digital campaigns (retargeting from DOOH exposure)
- Measurement is becoming comparable to digital channels

### How Programmatic DOOH Works

```
Advertiser → DSP (The Trade Desk, DV360) → SSP (Vistar Media, Place Exchange)
                                                        ↓
                                           DOOH Screens (Clear Channel,
                                           JCDecaux, Lamar, Outfront)
```

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Define campaign objective (awareness, foot traffic, event promotion)
2. Select DSP with DOOH capability (The Trade Desk, Hivestack, Vistar Media)
3. Choose screen types: billboards, transit, mall, airport, elevator, gas station
4. Set targeting: geography, time-of-day, weather-triggered, audience density
5. Upload creative (high-impact visual, 6–10 seconds, minimal text)
6. Set budget and bid strategy
7. Launch and monitor performance
8. Measure: impressions, reach, foot traffic lift, mobile retargeting conversions

### Location-Triggered DOOH Campaigns

DOOH can trigger different creative based on real-time conditions.

| Trigger | How It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Weather** | Ads change based on local weather conditions | McDonald's shows ice cream when temp > 80°F, hot coffee when < 40°F |
| **Time of day** | Creative adapts to breakfast/lunch/dinner/night | Subway shows breakfast menu before 10 AM, lunch menu after |
| **Traffic** | Ads trigger based on traffic density or congestion | Waze-connected DOOH shows nearby gas stations during traffic |
| **Events** | Ads near event venues activate during events | Beer brand activates billboards near stadiums on game days |
| **Foot traffic** | Screen content adjusts based on audience density | Retail ads show during peak mall hours, B2B during commute hours |

**Real-Time Example:**
- **British Airways' "Magic of Flying" Billboard** — a digital billboard near Piccadilly Circus tracked actual BA flights overhead using flight data APIs. When a plane flew over, a child on the billboard stood up, pointed at the sky, and the screen displayed the actual flight number and destination. Result: 1 million social shares, Cannes Grand Prix, and BA saw a measurable increase in flight bookings from London.

### Mobile Retargeting from DOOH Exposure

This is the most powerful bridge between DOOH and digital marketing.

**How it works:**
1. DOOH screen serves an ad in a specific location
2. Mobile devices in the vicinity are identified (via device IDs, not personal data)
3. Those device IDs are added to a retargeting audience
4. Users who were near the DOOH screen later see follow-up ads on their mobile devices

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Partner with a DOOH platform that supports device ID passback (Vistar Media, Hivestack)
2. Set exposure zones around screen locations
3. Capture device IDs of phones near screens during ad exposure
4. Onboard device IDs into your DSP as a retargeting segment
5. Serve follow-up mobile/display ads referencing the DOOH creative
6. Measure: DOOH exposure → mobile ad → website visit → conversion

**Real-Time Example:**
- **McDonald's** ran a DOOH campaign in major city centers showing their new McSpicy burger. Mobile devices within 200 meters of the DOOH screens were captured. Those users were then retargeted on their phones with a coupon for the McSpicy at the nearest McDonald's. Result: 8.5% of retargeted users redeemed the coupon — 4x higher than the control group who only saw mobile ads without prior DOOH exposure.

| **Conversion attribution** | DOOH exposure → conversion action | Multi-touch attribution platforms |

### Creative Best Practices for DOOH

1. **6-second rule** — your message must be understood in 6 seconds or less
2. **Minimal text** — 7 words maximum on a billboard
3. **High contrast** — bold colors and large typography visible from distance
4. **One message** — don't try to communicate everything; pick one CTA
5. **Location relevance** — reference the neighborhood, event, or context of the screen
6. **Motion for digital screens** — simple animation catches attention (but don't distract drivers)
7. **QR codes for interactive screens** — only in pedestrian areas where people can scan
8. **Dynamic creative** — leverage weather, time, and data triggers for relevance
