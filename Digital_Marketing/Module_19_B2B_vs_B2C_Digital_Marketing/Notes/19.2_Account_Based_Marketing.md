# 19.2 Account-Based Marketing (ABM) — Comprehensive Notes

---

## 19.2.1 ABM Strategy

### What is ABM?

Account-Based Marketing (ABM) is a strategic approach that concentrates sales and marketing resources on a defined set of target accounts and employs personalized campaigns designed to resonate with each account. Instead of casting a wide net (lead generation), ABM uses a spear — targeting specific high-value accounts.

**ABM vs. Traditional Lead Generation:**

| Dimension | Traditional Lead Gen | Account-Based Marketing |
|---|---|---|
| **Approach** | Wide net, attract many leads | Targeted, focus on specific accounts |
| **Target** | Individual leads | Entire accounts (multiple contacts) |
| **Personalization** | Segment-level | Account-level or individual-level |
| **Metrics** | MQLs, lead volume | Account engagement, pipeline, revenue |
| **Sales Alignment** | Marketing passes leads to sales | Marketing and sales co-target accounts |
| **Content** | One-to-many | One-to-few or one-to-one |
| **ROI** | Lower per-deal but higher volume | Higher per-deal, typically 171% higher ACV (TOPO) |

**Real-Time Example:**
Snowflake's ABM program targets the Fortune 500. Rather than running generic Google Ads, they:
- Identify that Walmart is evaluating cloud data platforms
- Create a Walmart-specific landing page showing retail use cases
- Run LinkedIn ads targeting Walmart's data engineering, IT, and finance teams
- Send personalized direct mail to the CTO and VP of Data
- Have the sales team coordinate outreach with marketing "air cover"
- Result: $500K–$5M deals that wouldn't close with generic marketing

---

### ABM Tiers

ABM operates at three tiers, each with different levels of personalization and scale:

| Tier | Name | Accounts | Personalization | Channels | Budget/Account |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | One-to-One | 5–25 accounts | Fully custom content per account | Custom landing pages, exec dinners, bespoke campaigns | $15K–$50K+ |
| **Tier 2** | One-to-Few | 25–100 accounts | Cluster-level (by industry, segment, or challenge) | Industry-specific content, targeted ads, small events | $2K–$15K |
| **Tier 3** | One-to-Many | 100–1,000 accounts | Technology-aided personalization | Programmatic ads, personalized email at scale, dynamic web content | $500–$2K |

**Real-Time Example — Tier 1 (One-to-One):**
Terminus (now part of DemandScience) selling to SAP:
- Built a custom microsite: "How Terminus Helps SAP's Marketing Team"
- Filmed a personalized video from the CEO addressing SAP's CMO by name
- Sent a custom gift box to the 5-person buying committee
- Ran LinkedIn ads only visible to SAP employees
- Hosted a private dinner for SAP marketing leaders at Dreamforce
- Investment: ~$25K over 6 months
- Result: $250K annual contract

**Real-Time Example — Tier 3 (One-to-Many):**
RollWorks running ABM for a cybersecurity company:
- Uploaded a list of 500 target accounts (mid-market financial services companies)
- Ran display ads personalized by industry ("Cybersecurity for Financial Services")
- Sent automated email sequences with industry-specific content
- Personalized the website homepage dynamically based on visitor company
- Investment: $1K per account over 12 months
- Result: 3x pipeline compared to non-ABM accounts

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Development

ICP is the foundational element of ABM. Without a well-defined ICP, you're targeting the wrong accounts.

**ICP Framework:**

| Dimension | Criteria | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Firmographic** | Industry, company size, revenue, location | SaaS companies, 200–2,000 employees, $20M–$200M revenue, North America |
| **Technographic** | Technology stack, tools used | Uses Salesforce, HubSpot, or Marketo; has a modern cloud infrastructure |
| **Behavioral** | Website visits, content consumption, event attendance | Visited pricing page, downloaded 3+ resources, attended webinar |
| **Intent** | Research signals, topic searches, review site visits | Searching for "ABM platform," reading G2 reviews of competitors |
| **Fit** | Alignment with your solution, budget, decision-making authority | Has dedicated marketing ops team, budget for martech, executive sponsor |

**ICP Scoring Model:**

```
SCORE = Firmographic Fit (30%) + Technographic Fit (20%) + Behavioral Signals (20%) + Intent Data (20%) + Relationship (10%)

Tier 1: Score 85–100 (Top 25 accounts — full custom ABM)
Tier 2: Score 65–84 (Next 75 accounts — cluster ABM)
Tier 3: Score 50–64 (Next 500 accounts — programmatic ABM)
Below 50: Not targeted (standard demand gen)
```

**Real-Time Example:**
6sense (an ABM platform) defines their own ICP as:
- B2B technology companies with $50M+ revenue
- Have a marketing team of 10+ people
- Currently using Salesforce or HubSpot CRM
- Have shown intent signals for "ABM," "intent data," or "demand generation"
- Located in North America, UK, or DACH region
- Already spending $500K+/year on marketing technology

### Target Account Selection and Scoring

**Selection Process:**
1. Analyze top 100 current customers — what do the best ones have in common?
2. Build ICP criteria from patterns in your best customers
3. Enrich prospect data with firmographic and technographic info (Clearbit, ZoomInfo)
4. Layer intent data to find accounts actively researching your category (Bombora, 6sense)
5. Score and rank accounts
6. Validate with sales — they know which accounts are realistic targets
7. Finalize target account list (TAL) and assign to ABM tiers

**Real-Time Example:**
Demandbase's TAL process for a cybersecurity client:
1. Analyzed 200 best customers → pattern: financial services, 1,000+ employees, using legacy SIEM
2. Built ICP → 3,000 companies matched the profile
3. Layered Bombora intent data → 400 showed active research signals
4. Sales reviewed → removed 50 (bad relationships, already lost deals)
5. Final TAL: 350 accounts across 3 tiers (15 Tier 1, 85 Tier 2, 250 Tier 3)

### ABM and Sales Alignment

ABM only works when sales and marketing are truly aligned. This is the #1 success factor.

**ABM Alignment Model:**

| Activity | Marketing's Role | Sales' Role | Shared |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Account Selection** | Data-driven ICP analysis | Field intelligence, relationship context | Final TAL approval |
| **Account Research** | Firmographic/technographic intel | Relationship mapping, org chart | Account plans |
| **Content Creation** | Build account/segment content | Provide customer objections and use cases | Messaging review |
| **Campaign Execution** | Digital ads, email, web personalization | Personal outreach, meetings, calls | Coordinated timing |
| **Measurement** | Engagement tracking, attribution | Deal progress, pipeline | Revenue influenced |

**Real-Time Example:**
Terminus's "Revenue Development" model:
- Marketing and sales sit together (literally — same floor, same team area)
- Both report to a single Chief Revenue Officer
- Weekly account reviews: "Which Tier 1 accounts are heating up? Where do we need marketing air cover?"
- Shared Slack channel for real-time account intel
- Result: 2x win rate on ABM-targeted accounts vs. non-ABM

---

### Implementation Steps for ABM Strategy

1. **Get executive sponsorship**: ABM requires cross-functional alignment — VP of Marketing and VP of Sales must co-sponsor
2. **Define your ICP**: Use historical customer data to identify patterns in your best accounts
3. **Build your TAL**: Start with 10–25 Tier 1 accounts and 50–100 Tier 2 accounts
4. **Align with sales**: Co-create account plans for Tier 1 accounts; agree on engagement plays for Tier 2
5. **Select your ABM tech stack**: Start with LinkedIn Ads + website personalization + CRM integration
6. **Create account-level content**: Develop industry-specific and account-specific content assets
7. **Launch pilot**: Run a 90-day ABM pilot on 10–15 accounts, measure engagement and pipeline
8. **Scale or iterate**: Based on pilot results, expand to more accounts or refine approach

## 19.2.2 ABM Tactics & Execution

### Personalized Content for Target Accounts

ABM content must speak directly to the account's challenges, industry, and stakeholders.

**Content Personalization Levels:**

| Level | Example | Effort | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Industry** | "How Financial Services Companies Use Our Platform" | Low | Medium |
| **Segment** | "How Mid-Market FinTech Companies Reduce Compliance Risk" | Medium | High |
| **Account** | "How [Company Name] Can Reduce Compliance Risk by 40%" | High | Very High |
| **Individual** | Personalized video from CEO to target's CTO | Very High | Highest |

**Real-Time Example:**
Engagio (now part of Demandbase) for a Tier 1 account (Microsoft):
- Created a custom landing page: "How Engagio Powers ABM at Enterprise Scale — Built for Microsoft's Marketing Organization"
- Developed an account-specific ROI projection based on Microsoft's team size and marketing spend
- Created a 90-day impact plan showing exactly what Microsoft's first quarter with Engagio would look like
- Result: Meeting with Microsoft's VP of Demand Generation within 2 weeks

---

### Account-Specific Landing Pages and Microsites

Personalized landing pages show target accounts that you understand their business and have a tailored solution.

**Microsite Anatomy:**
```
[Account Logo + Your Logo]
"How [Company Name] Can Achieve [Specific Outcome]"

Section 1: Industry challenges specific to their vertical
Section 2: How your solution addresses those challenges
Section 3: Case study from a similar company in their industry
Section 4: ROI calculator pre-populated with their data
Section 5: Personalized CTA ("Schedule a Call with [Their AE's Name]")
```

**Tools for Personalized Landing Pages:**
- **Mutiny**: AI-powered website personalization — dynamically changes headlines, images, CTAs based on visitor company
- **Folloze**: Build personalized content experiences for ABM
- **Uberflip**: Create personalized content hubs for target accounts
- **Intellimize**: AI-driven personalization and testing

**Real-Time Example:**
Mutiny's own ABM approach:
- When a visitor from a Tier 1 target account hits their website, the homepage dynamically changes
- Instead of generic "Personalize Your Website," a Salesforce visitor sees: "See How Salesforce-Using Companies Increase Conversion 30%"
- The case studies shown are filtered to match the visitor's industry
- Result: 2.5x conversion rate on personalized pages vs. generic pages

### Personalized Email Sequences for Buying Committees

ABM email is not mass email. Each email is tailored to the recipient's role, seniority, and priorities.

**Multi-Thread Email Strategy:**

| Role | Email Angle | Content Offer | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CTO/VP Engineering** | "Reduce infrastructure costs by 35%" | Technical architecture whitepaper | Data-driven, concise |
| **CFO/Finance** | "ROI of modernizing your data stack" | ROI calculator + case study | Business-focused, metrics-heavy |
| **End Users** | "See how teams like yours save 10hrs/week" | Product tour video | Practical, benefit-focused |
| **Procurement** | "Compliance and security overview" | Security whitepaper + certifications | Professional, reassuring |

**Email Sequence Structure (Tier 1 Account):**
- Email 1 (Week 1): Value proposition — specific to their challenge
- Email 2 (Week 2): Relevant case study from their industry
- Email 3 (Week 3): Personal insight — reference their recent news, earnings call, or blog post
- Email 4 (Week 4): Invite to exclusive event or offer custom briefing
- Email 5 (Week 6): Re-engage with new content or different angle

**Real-Time Example:**
Outreach (the sales engagement platform) practicing what they preach:
- SDRs send personalized emails referencing the prospect's company blog posts, job openings (signals of growth/pain), and recent funding rounds
- Each email includes a 30-second personalized video (recorded with Vidyard)
- The email references other companies in their portfolio/industry that use Outreach
- Result: 3x reply rate compared to templated sequences

### Intent Data for ABM

Intent data reveals which accounts are actively researching topics related to your solution — before they fill out a form or visit your website.

**Types of Intent Data:**

| Type | Source | Signal | Provider |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First-Party** | Your website, content, product | Page views, content downloads, trial activity | Google Analytics, HubSpot, Amplitude |
| **Second-Party** | Review sites, partner data | Reviews read, comparisons viewed | G2, TrustRadius, Capterra |
| **Third-Party** | Publisher network, search data | Topics researched across the internet | Bombora, 6sense, TechTarget |

**How Intent Data Works (Bombora Example):**
1. Bombora tracks content consumption across 5,000+ B2B publisher websites
2. When employees at Company X start reading significantly more articles about "cloud data security" than their baseline, Bombora flags a "surge" in that topic
3. Your ABM platform receives this signal: "Company X is surging on cloud data security"
4. Marketing launches ads and personalized content for Company X about cloud data security
5. Sales reaches out with a relevant message at exactly the right time

**Real-Time Example:**
6sense's intent-driven ABM for their own pipeline:
- 6sense monitors intent signals for topics like "ABM platform," "intent data," "demand generation"
- When a target account shows "Decision" stage intent (reading comparison articles, visiting pricing pages of competitors), they trigger:
  - Tier 1: Immediate SDR outreach + personalized ads + exec gift
  - Tier 2: Automated email sequence + display ads
  - Tier 3: Programmatic ads only
- Result: Accounts contacted in the "decision" stage convert 9x better than cold outreach

---

### Implementation Steps for ABM Tactics

1. **Start with Tier 2** (not Tier 1): Tier 2 ABM is more scalable and easier to prove ROI before investing in fully custom Tier 1 campaigns
2. **Build account-level content**: Create industry-specific content that can be customized per account with minimal effort
3. **Set up website personalization**: Use Mutiny or similar to dynamically personalize your website for target accounts
4. **Launch LinkedIn ABM ads**: Target your account list by company name + job title combinations
5. **Activate intent data**: Integrate Bombora or 6sense with your CRM to trigger campaigns when accounts show buying signals
6. **Coordinate with sales**: Use shared Slack channels or CRM alerts to notify sales when marketing campaigns are running for their accounts
7. **Layer direct mail**: For Tier 1, add physical touchpoints to the digital campaign to stand out

### Integrating ABM with CRM and Marketing Automation

ABM data must flow seamlessly into your CRM and marketing automation platform.

**Integration Architecture:**

```
Intent Data (Bombora/6sense) → CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot) → Account Score Updated
                                                           → Sales Alert Triggered
                                                           → Marketing Campaign Triggered
                                                                ↓
                                                        LinkedIn Ads Activated
                                                        Email Sequence Started
                                                        Website Personalized
                                                        Direct Mail Triggered
```

**Key Integrations:**
1. **Intent → CRM**: When an account shows buying signals, automatically update the account record and alert the account owner
2. **CRM → Ad Platform**: Sync target account lists from CRM to LinkedIn, Google, and programmatic platforms
3. **Web Personalization → CRM**: Log website personalization events as touchpoints in CRM
4. **Direct Mail → CRM**: Track direct mail sends and deliveries as CRM activities
5. **Engagement Scoring → CRM**: Roll up all touchpoints into an account engagement score visible to sales

## 19.2.4 Measuring ABM Success

### ABM Metrics Framework

ABM is measured at the account level, not the lead level. This is the biggest mindset shift.

| Metric | Definition | How to Measure | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Account Engagement Score** | Composite score of all marketing touchpoints for an account | Sum of ad impressions, website visits, content downloads, email opens, event attendance | Varies by model (typically 0–100) |
| **Pipeline Influenced** | Total pipeline ($) where marketing touched the account | CRM attribution — any opportunity where marketing touchpoint preceded the opportunity creation | 3–5x marketing investment |
| **Revenue Influenced** | Closed-won revenue where marketing touched the account | CRM attribution on closed-won deals | 30–50% of total revenue |
| **Deal Velocity** | Average time from first engagement to close | Compare ABM accounts vs. non-ABM accounts | 20–30% faster for ABM |
| **Win Rate** | % of opportunities that close-won | ABM accounts win rate vs. non-ABM accounts | ABM accounts win 2–3x more often |
| **Account Penetration** | # of contacts engaged per account | Track unique contacts with marketing touchpoints | 5–10 contacts per account |
| **ACV (Average Contract Value)** | Average deal size | Compare ABM deals vs. non-ABM deals | ABM deals 171% larger (TOPO Research) |
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Total ABM spend / accounts acquired | Total program cost / new customers from ABM | Varies, but aim for < 1/3 of first-year ACV |

**Real-Time Example:**
A SaaS company's ABM program results after 12 months:

| Metric | Non-ABM Accounts | ABM Accounts | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Win Rate | 12% | 28% | +133% |
| Average Deal Size | $45K | $120K | +167% |
| Sales Cycle | 180 days | 120 days | -33% |
| Contacts Engaged | 1.5 | 7.2 | +380% |
| Pipeline per Account | $85K | $310K | +265% |

### Implementation Steps for ABM Measurement

1. **Define your ABM metrics**: Agree with sales and leadership on 5–7 core metrics
2. **Set up account-level tracking**: Ensure your CRM can report at the account level, not just lead/contact level
3. **Build engagement scoring model**: Assign point values to touchpoints (website visit = 5, demo request = 50, etc.)
4. **Create comparison cohorts**: Always measure ABM accounts vs. non-ABM accounts to prove the ABM lift
5. **Build dashboards**: Create executive, account, and campaign dashboards in your BI tool
6. **Report monthly**: Share ABM results monthly with sales leadership; quarterly with executive team
7. **Iterate scoring**: Refine your engagement scoring model quarterly based on which signals actually correlate with closed deals

### Gap Analysis

| Common Gap | Impact | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Measuring leads instead of accounts | Can't prove ABM value | Restructure reporting around account-level metrics |
| No control group | Can't prove ABM lift vs. baseline | Always maintain a non-ABM comparison cohort |
| Vanity metrics only (impressions, clicks) | Looks good on paper but no revenue impact | Tie all metrics to pipeline and revenue |
| No account penetration tracking | Single-threading deals | Track # of engaged contacts per account |
| ABM and CRM disconnected | Can't attribute marketing touchpoints | Integrate ABM platform with CRM bi-directionally |

### Best Practices

- Start small: pilot with 10–25 accounts, prove ROI, then scale
- ABM is a team sport: if sales isn't bought in, ABM will fail regardless of technology
- Personalization doesn't mean manual — use technology to personalize at scale
- Multi-channel is critical: accounts touched across 3+ channels convert 2.5x better
- Patience: ABM is a long-term play — most programs take 6–12 months to show revenue impact
- Don't ABM everything: keep a demand gen engine running alongside ABM for pipeline diversification
- Measure against a control group: always compare ABM accounts to similar non-ABM accounts to prove lift
