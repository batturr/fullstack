## 25.3.1 Week-by-Week Execution Plan

### Philosophy of Execution

Execution separates strategists from marketers. A brilliant strategy that is poorly executed will always lose to a decent strategy that is executed with discipline, speed, and attention to detail. The weekly execution plan below is designed to create momentum — each week builds on the previous one, and skipping steps causes compounding problems later.

**Real-Time Example — How Slack Executed Their Launch (2013-2014):**
Slack followed a remarkably disciplined launch execution:
- **Weeks 1-2 (Research):** Stewart Butterfield spent months in beta with 8 companies, gathering feedback. They did not launch until they understood exactly how teams used the product.
- **Weeks 3-4 (Build):** Created a simple landing page with one CTA: "Request Access." The exclusivity created demand.
- **Weeks 5-6 (Launch):** Invited beta users, asked them to invite teams. First day: 8,000 signups. The press coverage came from the genuine word-of-mouth, not paid PR.
- **Weeks 7-8 (Optimize):** Tracked which onboarding steps correlated with team retention. Optimized the "first 10 messages" experience because data showed that was the activation point.
- **Weeks 9-12 (Scale):** Content marketing (Slack blog), integrations marketplace, and targeted ads to specific industries.

### Week 1: Research, Persona Development, Competitive Analysis, Goal Setting

**What to accomplish:**
This is the most important week. Resist the urge to start creating content or designing logos. Every hour spent on research saves 10 hours of wasted execution later.

**Detailed Task List:**

| Task | Time Estimate | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Industry landscape research | 4 hours | 2-3 page industry overview |
| Competitor analysis (5 competitors) | 6 hours | Competitor matrix spreadsheet |
| Customer research (surveys, forums, reviews) | 4 hours | Voice-of-customer document |
| Buyer persona development (3 personas) | 4 hours | 3 persona documents |
| SWOT analysis | 2 hours | SWOT matrix with implications |
| Goal setting (SMART) | 2 hours | Goals and KPIs document |
| Initial keyword research | 3 hours | Keyword spreadsheet (50-100 keywords) |

**Tools:**

| Tool | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| SEMrush / Ahrefs | Competitive analysis, keyword research | $119-199/mo (free trials available) |
| SparkToro | Audience intelligence | Free tier available |
| Google Trends | Trend validation | Free |
| AnswerThePublic | Question-based keyword ideas | Free tier (3 searches/day) |
| SurveyMonkey / Typeform | Primary research surveys | Free tier available |
| Make My Persona (HubSpot) | Guided persona creation | Free |
| Meta Ad Library | Competitor ad research | Free |
| Google Keyword Planner | Keyword volume and CPC estimates | Free (with Google Ads account) |

**Real-Time Example — Persona Research in Action:**
When **Spotify** expanded into podcasting, their research phase would have included:
- Analyzing existing podcast listener demographics (Edison Research data)
- Surveying current Spotify users about podcast listening habits
- Studying competitor podcast platforms (Apple Podcasts, Stitcher, Overcast)
- Identifying the gap: no music platform had deeply integrated podcasts with music
- Creating personas: "Commuter Chris" (listens during 45-min commute), "Runner Rachel" (alternates music and podcasts during workouts), "Learner Liam" (consumes educational podcasts at 1.5x speed)

**Gaps to Watch For:**
- Skipping primary research — Persona documents built only from assumptions are worthless
- Not validating assumptions with data — "I think our audience is on TikTok" vs. "Our audience research shows 68% of our target demo spends 40+ minutes/day on TikTok"
- Setting goals before understanding the landscape — You cannot set realistic targets without knowing baselines and benchmarks

### Week 3: Website/Landing Page Build, Social Profile Setup, Analytics Implementation

**What to accomplish:**
Build the infrastructure. Nothing launches without a destination (website), tracking (analytics), and distribution channels (social profiles).

**Website/Landing Page Build Checklist:**

| Task | Priority | Tools |
|---|---|---|
| Choose platform | Critical | WordPress, Webflow, Squarespace, Shopify, Unbounce |
| Select and install theme/template | Critical | ThemeForest, Webflow templates, platform native |
| Write and place copy (headline, subheads, body, CTA) | Critical | Google Docs for drafting, Hemingway for readability |
| Design/source visuals | Critical | Canva, Unsplash, Pexels, Figma |
| Mobile responsiveness check | Critical | Chrome DevTools, BrowserStack |
| Page speed optimization | High | TinyPNG (images), WP Rocket (caching), Cloudflare (CDN) |
| SSL certificate verification | Critical | Most hosts include free SSL via Let's Encrypt |
| Schema markup implementation | Medium | Schema.org, Google Structured Data Markup Helper |
| Privacy policy and cookie consent | Critical | Termly, Cookiebot, Iubenda |
| Contact form setup | High | Contact Form 7, Typeform, Gravity Forms |

**Analytics Implementation — GA4 + GTM Setup:**

```
Step 1: Create GA4 Property
├── Go to analytics.google.com
├── Create new property
├── Choose "Web" data stream
└── Copy Measurement ID (G-XXXXXXXXXX)

Step 2: Install Google Tag Manager
├── Create GTM account and container
├── Install GTM code snippets on all pages (head + body)
└── Verify installation via GTM Preview mode

Step 3: Configure GA4 via GTM
├── Create GA4 Configuration tag with Measurement ID
├── Set trigger: All Pages
├── Publish and verify in GA4 Realtime report

Step 4: Set Up Custom Events
├── Button clicks → Custom event tag (Click trigger)
├── Form submissions → Custom event tag (Form trigger)
├── Scroll depth → Built-in scroll depth trigger
├── Video plays → YouTube video trigger
├── File downloads → Click trigger with URL contains .pdf/.xlsx
└── Outbound link clicks → Click trigger with outbound URL

Step 5: Configure Conversions
├── In GA4, go to Events > mark key events as conversions
├── Key conversions: form_submit, signup_complete, purchase
└── Verify conversion tracking with test submissions

Step 6: UTM Parameter Framework
├── Create UTM builder spreadsheet
├── Define naming convention (lowercase, hyphens, consistent)
├── Generate URLs for all campaign links
└── Test UTM tracking in GA4 Traffic Acquisition report
```

**Real-Time Example — Analytics Setup That Catches Issues Early:**
**Airbnb** discovered through analytics that users who viewed 3+ listings in their first session were 4x more likely to book. This insight led them to redesign their homepage to show more listings above the fold and reduce friction in the browse-to-book flow. You will only find these insights if your analytics is properly configured from day one.

### Week 4: Content Creation Sprint, Email Platform Setup, Ad Account Configuration

**What to accomplish:**
This is the production week. Create enough content to launch with momentum, not a trickle.

**Content Batching Strategy:**

```
Day 1-2: Written Content
├── Write 2-3 blog posts (1,500+ words each)
├── Draft 15-20 social media posts (captions + CTAs)
├── Write email sequence (3-4 emails)
└── Write ad copy variations (5-10 versions)

Day 3: Visual Content
├── Design social media graphics (templates + 10 variations)
├── Create infographic or data visualization
├── Design email templates
├── Create ad creative (static images + carousels)
└── Source/edit stock photos

Day 4: Video Content (if applicable)
├── Script 2-3 short videos (30-90 seconds)
├── Record using smartphone (good lighting, clean background)
├── Edit with CapCut, Descript, or DaVinci Resolve
└── Create thumbnails

Day 5: Review and Schedule
├── Proofread all written content
├── Schedule social posts in Buffer/Later/Hootsuite
├── Upload blog posts as drafts (publish next week)
├── Queue email sequences in platform
└── Upload ad creatives to ad platforms (do not launch yet)
```

**Email Platform Setup:**

| Platform | Best For | Free Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Mailchimp | Beginners, small lists | 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month |
| ConvertKit | Creators, bloggers | 1,000 subscribers |
| Klaviyo | E-commerce (Shopify integration) | 250 contacts |
| HubSpot | Full CRM + marketing suite | 1,000 contacts |
| Brevo (Sendinblue) | Budget-conscious, transactional emails | 300 emails/day |

**Ad Account Configuration:**

```
Google Ads Setup:
├── Create Google Ads account
├── Link to GA4
├── Set up conversion tracking (import GA4 conversions)
├── Create campaigns with proper structure:
│   ├── Campaign level: budget, bidding, targeting
│   ├── Ad Group level: keyword themes
│   └── Ad level: responsive search ads (15 headlines, 4 descriptions)
├── Add negative keywords from keyword research
└── Set up ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets)

Meta Ads Setup:
├── Create Meta Business Suite account
├── Install Meta Pixel via GTM
├── Verify domain in Business Settings
├── Set up custom conversions
├── Create audiences:
│   ├── Custom: website visitors, email list
│   ├── Lookalike: based on email list or converters
│   └── Saved: interest/behavior/demographic targeting
├── Build campaign structure:
│   ├── Campaign: objective (traffic, conversions, leads)
│   ├── Ad Set: audience, placement, budget, schedule
│   └── Ad: creative formats (image, video, carousel)
└── Upload creatives (do not launch until Week 5-6)
```

### Weeks 5-8: Campaign Launch, Optimization, and Advanced Execution

**Week 5 — Launch:**

The launch is not about perfection. It is about getting live, collecting data, and beginning the optimization cycle.

**Launch Day Checklist:**
- [ ] All tracking verified (GA4 realtime, pixel helper, UTM parameters)
- [ ] Content published on schedule
- [ ] Email sequence activated
- [ ] Social posting schedule active
- [ ] Team notified (if applicable)
- [ ] Crisis response plan reviewed (what to do if something goes wrong)
- [ ] Performance dashboard accessible
- [ ] Daily monitoring schedule set (check 3x/day for first 3 days)

**Real-Time Example — Campaign Launch Discipline:**
When **Apple** launches a product, every marketing channel activates simultaneously — the website updates, emails send, social posts go live, influencers post, and ads begin — all coordinated to the second. While your capstone will not have Apple's scale, the discipline of synchronized launch across all channels is the same principle.

**Week 6 — Scale:**

```
Paid Ads Optimization:
├── Review first 3-5 days of data
├── Pause underperforming ad variations (CTR < 1%, CPC > 2x target)
├── Increase budget on winning ads by 20% (not more — budget jumps reset learning)
├── Test new audience segments
└── Check frequency — if over 3.0, expand audience or refresh creative

Content Optimization:
├── Review which social posts got highest engagement
├── Create more content in that format/topic
├── Repurpose top blog content into social snippets
└── Identify content gaps from audience questions/comments

Email Optimization:
├── Review open rates (benchmark: 20-25% for most industries)
├── A/B test subject lines on next send
├── Check click-through rates on CTAs
├── Review unsubscribe rate (should be < 0.5%)
└── Segment list based on engagement (opened vs. did not open)
```

**Week 7 — Optimize:**

This is where the capstone shifts from "doing marketing" to "being a marketer." Optimization requires analytical thinking and the courage to change what is not working.

**Mid-Campaign Review Framework:**

| Channel | Metric | Benchmark | Your Result | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Google Ads | CTR | 3-5% (search) | ___ | If below: test headlines, review keywords |
| Google Ads | CPA | Industry dependent | ___ | If above target: tighten keywords, add negatives |
| Meta Ads | CTR | 1-2% | ___ | If below: test new creative/copy |
| Meta Ads | ROAS | 2:1+ | ___ | If below: test new audiences, improve LP |
| Email | Open rate | 20-25% | ___ | If below: test subject lines, send times |
| Email | Click rate | 2-5% | ___ | If below: test CTA placement, copy |
| Social | Engagement rate | 1-5% by platform | ___ | If below: test content formats, posting times |
| Website | Conversion rate | 2-5% | ___ | If below: CRO on landing page |
| SEO | Impressions | Growing weekly | ___ | If flat: add internal links, expand content |

**A/B Testing Framework:**

```
1. Identify what to test (based on data, not gut feeling)
   └── Look for pages/ads/emails with high traffic but low conversion

2. Form a hypothesis
   └── "Changing the CTA button from 'Learn More' to 'Get My Free Guide' will 
       increase click-through rate because it emphasizes the value proposition"

3. Set up the test
   ├── Control (A): Original version
   ├── Variant (B): One change only
   ├── Traffic split: 50/50
   └── Minimum sample: 100 conversions per variant (or 1,000 visitors minimum)

4. Run for sufficient duration
   └── Minimum 7 days to account for day-of-week variations

5. Analyze with statistical significance
   └── Use a calculator: ABtestguide.com, Optimizely stats engine

6. Implement winner and document learnings
```

**Week 8 — Advanced Execution:**

Execute 3+ advanced deliverables from the optional list. This is where you differentiate yourself.

## 25.3.2 Quality Standards

### Content Quality Standards

**Writing Standards:**
- Zero grammatical or spelling errors (use Grammarly, Hemingway Editor)
- Reading level appropriate for audience (Grade 6-8 for consumer, Grade 10-12 for B2B)
- Active voice preferred (minimum 80% active voice)
- Every piece has a clear purpose, audience, and CTA
- Original content — no plagiarism (check with Copyscape if needed)
- Fact-check all statistics and claims; cite sources

**Design Standards:**
- Brand-consistent colors, fonts, and imagery across all assets
- Follow hierarchy principles (most important information = most prominent)
- Mobile-first design (view every design on phone before publishing)
- Accessible: sufficient color contrast (WCAG AA), alt text on images, readable font sizes (16px minimum body)
- File sizes optimized (images under 200KB, video under 50MB for social)

**Data Standards:**
- Every number must have a source
- Screenshots must include dates and context
- Never fabricate or inflate metrics
- Document data collection methodology
- Note any anomalies or data quality issues

**Technical Standards:**
- Pages load in under 3 seconds
- All links work (check weekly with Broken Link Checker)
- Forms submit correctly and trigger confirmation
- Tracking fires accurately (verify via GA4 DebugView)
- Mobile-responsive across devices (test on iOS and Android)
- HTTPS on all pages
- Proper canonical tags to prevent duplicate content

**Legal and Compliance Standards:**
- Disclose sponsored content and affiliate relationships (FTC guidelines)
- Include privacy policy and cookie consent
- Honor email unsubscribe requests within 10 business days (CAN-SPAM, GDPR)
- Do not make false claims in advertising
- Respect copyright — use licensed images, music, and fonts
- Follow platform-specific advertising policies

### Quality Assurance Checklist Before Publishing

```
CONTENT QA:
□ Proofread by at least one other person
□ Links tested
□ Images display correctly (desktop and mobile)
□ CTA works and leads to correct destination
□ UTM parameters appended to all campaign links
□ Content aligns with brand voice guidelines
□ Legal disclaimers present where needed

TECHNICAL QA:
□ Page loads in < 3 seconds (PageSpeed score 90+)
□ Mobile responsive (tested on actual device)
□ Analytics tracking verified (GA4 Realtime)
□ Form submission triggers correct automation
□ Email renders correctly in top 5 clients (use Litmus or Email on Acid)
□ Ad creative meets platform specs (dimensions, text ratio, file size)

DATA QA:
□ UTM naming convention followed
□ Conversion events firing correctly
□ Dashboard data matches platform data (within 5% variance)
□ Weekly data export saved as backup
```

## Strategies for Successful Execution

### Strategy 1: The 80/20 Content Rule
Spend 20% of your time creating content and 80% distributing and promoting it. A great blog post that nobody sees has zero value. For every piece of content, have a distribution plan:
- Share on social (3+ platforms)
- Email to your list
- Comment on relevant threads linking to it
- Repurpose into different formats (blog → carousel → video → email)

### Strategy 2: The Compounding Channel
Invest extra effort in the one channel that compounds over time. SEO content published in Week 5 can still drive traffic in Week 12 (and beyond). Paid ads stop the moment you stop paying. Build at least one compounding asset.

### Strategy 3: The Feedback Loop
Create a weekly feedback loop:
1. Review data (what happened)
2. Analyze (why it happened)
3. Hypothesize (what might work better)
4. Test (implement one change)
5. Measure (did it work?)
6. Document (record the learning)

### Strategy 4: Community Before Campaign
Engage authentically in your target audience's communities (Reddit, Facebook Groups, LinkedIn, Discord) before promoting anything. People trust recommendations from active community members, not drive-by promoters.

### Strategy 5: Pre-Mortem Planning
Before launching, imagine the campaign has failed. List all the reasons it could fail. Then create a mitigation plan for each. This "pre-mortem" exercise catches blind spots that post-mortems reveal too late.

## Common Execution Mistakes and Fixes

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Launching without testing tracking | No reliable data for the entire campaign | Always do a "dry run" — submit test forms, click test links, verify in GA4 before going live |
| Posting the same content everywhere | Each platform penalizes reposts; audiences notice laziness | Adapt content format and tone for each platform while keeping the core message consistent |
| Not engaging with comments | Signals to algorithms that your content is not valuable | Block 30 minutes after each post to respond to every comment |
| Changing too many variables at once | Cannot determine what caused improvement or decline | Change one thing at a time in A/B tests; document every change with date and rationale |
| Ignoring mobile experience | 60%+ of traffic is mobile; poor mobile experience kills conversions | Test every page and email on a real phone, not just Chrome's device simulator |
| Running out of content | Gaps in posting schedule, loss of momentum | Batch create 2 weeks ahead; have a "evergreen content vault" of 5-10 posts ready |
| Not documenting as you go | Scrambling in Week 10 to recreate screenshots and data | Set a weekly Friday task: "Export data, take screenshots, write 3 bullet points about this week" |

---

> **Key Takeaway:** Execution is where strategy meets reality. The plan will change — expect it. The discipline of daily monitoring, weekly optimization, and documentation is what produces both results and a portfolio worth showcasing. Speed beats perfection at launch; optimization beats speed after launch.
