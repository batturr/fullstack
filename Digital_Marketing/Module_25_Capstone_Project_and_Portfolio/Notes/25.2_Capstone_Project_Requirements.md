## 25.2.1 Strategy & Planning Deliverables

### Overview

Strategy and planning are the foundation of every successful campaign. This phase accounts for 20% of the capstone grade because poor planning leads to wasted budgets, misaligned content, and campaigns that look busy but accomplish nothing. Every deliverable in this section must exist as a formal document before any execution begins.

---

### Deliverable 1: Market Research and Competitive Analysis Document

**What it is:** A structured analysis of the industry landscape, target market size, customer behavior patterns, and a detailed breakdown of what competitors are doing across digital channels.

**Real-Time Example:**
When **Notion** was planning its expansion from a productivity tool for individuals to a team collaboration platform, their market research would have covered:
- Market size: Project management software market valued at $6.68B in 2022, projected $15.08B by 2030
- Key players: Asana (publicly traded, $600M revenue), Monday.com ($520M revenue), Trello (owned by Atlassian)
- Gap identified: Most tools were either too simple (Trello) or too complex (Jira). Notion positioned as "flexible enough for any workflow"
- Audience behavior: Target users were already using 3-4 separate tools (docs, spreadsheets, project boards, wikis). Notion's value proposition was consolidation

**Implementation Steps:**
1. **Industry Analysis** — Size the market using Statista, IBISWorld, or industry reports. Identify growth trends, regulatory factors, and seasonal patterns.
2. **Audience Research** — Use Google Analytics (demographics, interests), Meta Audience Insights, Reddit/Quora (pain points), and surveys (Google Forms, Typeform) to understand the target audience.
3. **Competitor Identification** — List 5-10 direct and indirect competitors. Use SimilarWeb for traffic estimates, SEMrush for keyword overlap, and Meta Ad Library for ad creative analysis.
4. **Competitor Deep-Dive** — For each competitor, document:
   - Website UX and messaging
   - SEO strategy (top keywords, domain authority, backlink profile)
   - Social media presence (platforms, follower count, posting frequency, engagement rate)
   - Content strategy (blog frequency, content types, gated content)
   - Paid advertising (active ads, estimated spend, messaging angles)
   - Email marketing (sign up for their emails and document sequences)
5. **Opportunity Mapping** — Identify gaps competitors are not filling, audiences they are ignoring, and channels they are underusing.

**Tools:**

| Tool | Use Case |
|---|---|
| SEMrush / Ahrefs | Competitor keyword analysis, backlink profiles, traffic estimates |
| SimilarWeb | Website traffic comparison, traffic sources, audience overlap |
| Meta Ad Library | Competitor ad creatives and messaging |
| Google Trends | Search interest trends over time |
| SparkToro | Audience research — where your target audience spends time online |
| Statista / IBISWorld | Industry market size and growth data |
| BuiltWith | Competitor technology stack analysis |
| Owler / Crunchbase | Company funding, revenue estimates, news |

**Common Gaps:**
- Relying only on secondary research without any primary data (surveys, interviews)
- Analyzing competitors superficially — just listing their social follower counts without examining engagement, content quality, or strategic positioning
- Ignoring indirect competitors who solve the same problem differently
- Not documenting where the research data came from (always cite sources)

### Deliverable 2: 3 Detailed Buyer Personas

**What it is:** Semi-fictional representations of your ideal customers, built from real data and research, that guide every marketing decision from messaging to channel selection.

**Real-Time Example:**
**HubSpot's famous buyer personas** include:
- **Marketing Mary** — VP of Marketing at a 200-person company. Her goal is to prove marketing ROI to the CEO. She struggles with too many disconnected tools. She reads Marketing Week, follows industry influencers on LinkedIn, and attends inbound marketing conferences. She evaluates tools based on integration capabilities and reporting dashboards.
- **Owner Ollie** — Small business owner wearing all hats. He has no marketing team. He needs simple, affordable tools that "just work." He searches Google for "how to get more customers" and watches YouTube tutorials.

These personas drive entirely different content, messaging, and channel strategies for the same product.

**Persona Template Structure:**

```
BUYER PERSONA: [Name]
├── Demographics
│   ├── Age range, gender, location
│   ├── Education level, income range
│   ├── Job title, company size, industry
│   └── Family/life stage
│
├── Psychographics
│   ├── Goals and aspirations
│   ├── Pain points and frustrations
│   ├── Values and priorities
│   ├── Decision-making style
│   └── Risk tolerance
│
├── Digital Behavior
│   ├── Preferred social platforms
│   ├── Content consumption habits (video, blog, podcast)
│   ├── Search behavior (informational, transactional)
│   ├── Device preferences (mobile vs. desktop)
│   └── Online communities and forums
│
├── Buying Journey
│   ├── How they discover solutions
│   ├── Research process and timeline
│   ├── Decision criteria (price, features, reviews, brand trust)
│   ├── Influencers and trusted sources
│   └── Common objections
│
└── Marketing Implications
    ├── Best channels to reach them
    ├── Messaging that resonates
    ├── Content types they prefer
    ├── CTAs that motivate action
    └── Keywords they search for
```

**Tools for Persona Development:**
- Google Analytics 4 (demographics, interests, behavior flow)
- Meta Audience Insights
- SparkToro (audience intelligence — what they read, watch, follow)
- Customer surveys (Google Forms, Typeform, SurveyMonkey)
- Reddit, Quora, and niche forums (raw voice-of-customer data)
- Sales team interviews (if available)
- Review mining (read Amazon reviews, G2 reviews, Trustpilot for pain points)

### Deliverable 3: SWOT Analysis

**What it is:** A strategic framework analyzing internal Strengths and Weaknesses, and external Opportunities and Threats, applied specifically to the digital marketing context.

**Real-Time Example — Netflix (2023 SWOT for their ad-supported tier launch):**

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** | **Strengths:** 230M+ subscribers, massive content library, powerful recommendation algorithm, global brand recognition | **Weaknesses:** High content costs ($17B/year), subscriber growth plateauing in mature markets, password sharing reducing per-user revenue |
| **External** | **Opportunities:** Ad-supported tier opens new revenue stream, live sports rights, gaming expansion, international markets (India, Africa) | **Threats:** Disney+, HBO Max, Apple TV+ competing for content and subscribers; economic downturn reducing discretionary spending; regulatory pressure in EU markets |

**How this SWOT drove digital marketing decisions:**
- Strengths → Leveraged brand trust in messaging for ad-tier launch ("same Netflix, more affordable")
- Weaknesses → Used targeted digital campaigns to reduce churn (personalized re-engagement emails)
- Opportunities → Google Ads campaigns targeting "cheap streaming" and "free streaming" keywords
- Threats → Competitive positioning ads highlighting Netflix exclusives vs. competitor libraries

**Best Practices:**
- Make it specific to digital marketing, not a generic business SWOT
- Each point should lead to a strategic action
- Use data to support claims (e.g., "Weakness: Instagram engagement rate 1.2% vs. industry average 3.5%" is better than "Weakness: low social engagement")
- Update throughout the project as new data emerges

### Deliverable 4: Marketing Goals and KPIs (SMART Framework)

**What it is:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound objectives with Key Performance Indicators that will be tracked throughout the campaign.

**Real-Time Example — A DTC skincare brand capstone project:**

| Goal (SMART) | Primary KPI | Secondary KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| Increase website traffic from 500 to 2,000 monthly sessions within 8 weeks | Monthly sessions | Organic vs. paid split, bounce rate, pages per session |
| Generate 200 email subscribers via lead magnet in 8 weeks | Total email signups | Signup conversion rate, cost per lead (if paid), lead magnet download rate |
| Achieve 3.0 ROAS on Meta Ads with $400 total spend over 4 weeks | ROAS | CPC, CTR, CPA, conversion rate, frequency |
| Grow Instagram followers from 0 to 500 with 4%+ engagement rate | Follower count + engagement rate | Reach, impressions, saves, shares, comments |
| Publish 8 SEO-optimized blog posts ranking for target keywords | Number of indexed/ranking posts | Avg position, impressions (Search Console), organic clicks |

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Start with the business objective (revenue, leads, awareness)
2. Work backward to marketing objectives
3. Define KPIs for each objective
4. Set baselines (current performance or industry benchmarks)
5. Set targets that are ambitious but realistic
6. Define measurement frequency (daily, weekly, monthly)
7. Build a KPI tracking dashboard (Google Sheets, Looker Studio, or Databox)

**Common Gaps:**
- Setting vanity metrics as primary KPIs (followers, likes) instead of business-impact metrics (leads, revenue, conversion rate)
- Not establishing baselines — you cannot measure progress without knowing where you started
- Goals that are not time-bound — "increase traffic" is not a goal; "increase traffic by 40% in 8 weeks" is

### Deliverable 5: Channel Strategy with Budget Allocation

**What it is:** A documented rationale for which digital channels will be used, what role each plays in the funnel, and how the budget is allocated across them.

**Real-Time Example — B2B SaaS capstone with $1,000 total budget:**

```
Total Budget: $1,000

Channel Allocation:
├── Google Ads (Search)     — $350 (35%)
│   └── Role: Capture high-intent searches ("project management software for agencies")
│
├── LinkedIn Organic + Ads  — $250 (25%)
│   └── Role: Reach decision-makers; sponsored content for awareness + lead gen
│
├── Content Marketing       — $150 (15%)
│   └── Role: SEO + thought leadership; freelance writer for 3 pillar articles
│
├── Meta Ads (Retargeting)  — $150 (15%)
│   └── Role: Retarget website visitors and email non-openers
│
├── Email Marketing         — $50 (5%)
│   └── Role: Nurture leads through decision funnel (Mailchimp free tier + premium template)
│
└── Tools & Creative        — $50 (5%)
    └── Role: Canva Pro, stock images, video editing
```

**Strategies for Budget Allocation:**
- Allocate based on where your personas spend time (data from persona research)
- Weight toward channels with highest expected ROI at your budget level
- Reserve 10-15% for testing new channels or scaling winners mid-campaign
- For small budgets (<$500), focus on 2-3 channels rather than spreading thin across 6+

### Deliverable 7: Measurement and Attribution Plan

**What it is:** A documented plan for how results will be measured, which tools will be used, how attribution will be handled, and how data quality will be maintained.

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Map every marketing touchpoint to a measurable event
2. Define UTM parameter naming convention
3. Set up GA4 events and conversions
4. Configure Google Tag Manager for event tracking
5. Set up cross-channel reporting dashboard
6. Define attribution model (last-click, first-click, linear, or data-driven)
7. Document data collection procedures and backup plans

**UTM Naming Convention Example:**
```
utm_source    = platform (google, facebook, linkedin, email)
utm_medium    = channel type (cpc, organic, social, email)
utm_campaign  = campaign name (spring-launch, brand-awareness)
utm_content   = specific creative or variant (video-ad-v1, carousel-testimonial)
utm_term      = keyword (only for search ads)

Example URL:
https://example.com/landing?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=spring-launch&utm_content=carousel-testimonial-v2
```

## 25.2.2 Execution Deliverables — Detailed Breakdown

### Website/Landing Pages

**Minimum: 1 optimized landing page or website section**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Dropbox's original landing page** is a legendary example. It had:
- One headline: "Your files, anywhere"
- A 2-minute explainer video
- One CTA button: "Download Dropbox"
- No navigation menu, no distractions
- Conversion rate: 10%+ (industry average is 2-5%)

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Choose platform: WordPress, Webflow, Unbounce, Instapage, or Carrd
2. Write headline that addresses the primary pain point
3. Create supporting copy: benefits (not features), social proof, objection handling
4. Design above-the-fold section with clear CTA
5. Add trust signals: testimonials, logos, security badges, statistics
6. Optimize for mobile (60%+ traffic is mobile)
7. Implement tracking: GA4 page view, scroll depth, CTA click events, form submissions
8. Run PageSpeed test and optimize (target 90+ score)

**Landing Page Optimization Checklist:**
- [ ] Single, clear CTA (not multiple competing actions)
- [ ] Headline matches ad/email that brought the visitor
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Mobile-responsive
- [ ] Social proof visible above the fold
- [ ] Form has minimal required fields
- [ ] Thank-you page has next-step CTA
- [ ] GA4 conversion event configured
- [ ] Heatmap tracking installed (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity)

---

### SEO Deliverables

**Minimum: Keyword research, on-page optimization for 5+ pages, technical audit**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Canva** ranks for over 14 million keywords organically. Their SEO strategy included:
- Creating landing pages for every possible template search (e.g., "free resume template," "Instagram story template," "business card maker") — thousands of programmatic SEO pages
- Each page had optimized title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, and internal links
- Technical SEO: fast site speed, proper canonical tags, XML sitemap with dynamic updates
- Content marketing: Design School blog targeting "how to" design queries

**For your capstone (5+ pages):**
1. Keyword research using Ahrefs/SEMrush — find 20-30 target keywords
2. Map keywords to pages (1 primary + 2-3 secondary keywords per page)
3. Optimize title tags (under 60 characters, keyword near front)
4. Optimize meta descriptions (under 155 characters, include CTA)
5. Implement proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3)
6. Optimize images (descriptive file names, alt text, compression)
7. Internal linking structure (pillar-cluster model)
8. Technical audit: fix crawl errors, broken links, duplicate content, missing schema markup

### Content Deliverables

**Minimum: 5 pieces (blog posts, videos, infographics, ebooks, or similar)**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Buffer's content marketing strategy** built their entire brand through content:
- Published 2-3 blog posts per week on social media marketing topics
- Each post was 1,500-3,000 words with original research, data, and visuals
- Repurposed blog content into social posts, email newsletters, and podcast episodes
- Result: Blog grew to 1.5M monthly readers, driving 70% of signups

**Content Mix Strategy for Capstone:**

| Content Type | Quantity | Purpose | Distribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Long-form blog post (1,500+ words) | 2 | SEO, thought leadership | Website, social, email |
| Short-form blog post (500-800 words) | 1 | Quick-win keywords, topical content | Website, social |
| Infographic or visual guide | 1 | Shareability, backlinks | Social, Pinterest, outreach |
| Video (short-form or tutorial) | 1 | Engagement, platform algorithm favor | YouTube, Reels, TikTok |

### Social Media Deliverables

**Minimum: 3+ platforms, 30+ published posts with engagement**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Duolingo's TikTok strategy** transformed their brand perception:
- Posted chaotic, meme-driven content featuring their owl mascot
- Tone: irreverent, Gen Z humor, trend-jacking
- Result: 10M+ TikTok followers, brand became a cultural reference
- Key insight: They did not post product demos. They posted entertainment that happened to feature their mascot.

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Select platforms based on where your personas are (not where you personally spend time)
2. Create platform-specific content (not the same post everywhere)
3. Post consistently: minimum 4-5x/week per platform
4. Engage authentically: respond to every comment in the first hour
5. Use platform-native features (Reels, Stories, Polls, Threads, LinkedIn Articles)
6. Track engagement rate, reach, impressions, and follower growth weekly

### Email Marketing Deliverables

**Minimum: List building, 1 automation sequence (3+ emails), 2+ broadcast campaigns**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Morning Brew** grew to 4M email subscribers by:
- Offering a daily email that was genuinely enjoyable to read (not salesy)
- Using a referral program (refer friends = earn rewards)
- Welcome sequence: 3-email series introducing the brand, setting expectations, and driving engagement
- Result: Acquired by Business Insider for $75M

**Automation Sequence Example (Welcome Series):**

```
Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + Lead Magnet Delivery
├── Subject: "Here's your [lead magnet name] 🎉"
├── Content: Deliver the asset, introduce yourself/brand, set email expectations
└── CTA: Download the resource

Email 2 (Day 2): Value + Story
├── Subject: "The #1 mistake in [topic] (and how to avoid it)"
├── Content: Educational content that builds trust and demonstrates expertise
└── CTA: Read blog post or watch video

Email 3 (Day 4): Social Proof + Soft Offer
├── Subject: "How [customer name] achieved [result]"
├── Content: Case study or testimonial that builds desire
└── CTA: Book a call / try free / shop now

Email 4 (Day 7): Direct Offer
├── Subject: "Ready to [achieve desired outcome]?"
├── Content: Clear offer with benefits, urgency, and objection handling
└── CTA: Primary conversion action
```

### Paid Advertising Deliverables

**Minimum: 1 paid campaign (Google Ads, Meta Ads, or LinkedIn Ads)**

**Real-Time Example:**
**Dollar Shave Club's Meta Ads strategy** (before their viral video):
- Targeted men 25-45 interested in grooming, GQ, men's health
- Ad creative: Simple images of razors with bold copy ("Stop overpaying for shave tech")
- Offer: $1 trial month
- Funnel: Ad → Landing page → Signup
- ROAS: 5:1 in the first year of advertising

**Implementation Steps:**
1. Choose platform based on audience and objective
2. Set up pixel/tag and verify it fires correctly
3. Define campaign structure: Campaign > Ad Set/Ad Group > Ad
4. Create 3-5 ad variations for testing
5. Set daily budget and bid strategy (start with automatic bidding)
6. Launch and monitor daily for first 3 days
7. After learning phase (50+ conversions or 7 days), begin optimization
8. A/B test: creative, copy, audience, and placements
9. Document all results with screenshots

## Best Practices for All Deliverables

1. **Version control everything** — Save drafts with dates. You will need to show "before and after" in your portfolio.
2. **Screenshot obsessively** — Take screenshots of dashboards, ad setups, email campaigns, and social posts as you go. You cannot recreate these after campaigns end.
3. **Write brief rationale** — For every major decision (channel selection, budget split, creative direction), write 2-3 sentences explaining why. This is what separates a marketer from a button-pusher.
4. **Stay within platform policies** — One ad account ban can derail the entire project. Read Google Ads, Meta, and LinkedIn policies before launching anything.
5. **Build redundancy** — If one channel underperforms, have a backup plan. Always have at least one channel that does not depend on ad spend (organic social, SEO, email).
