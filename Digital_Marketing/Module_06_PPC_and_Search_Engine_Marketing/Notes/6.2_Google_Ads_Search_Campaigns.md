## 6.2.1 Campaign Setup & Settings

### Campaign Objectives

Google Ads offers goal-based campaign creation. Your objective determines which features and bidding strategies are available:

| Objective | Best For | Primary KPI | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sales** | E-commerce, direct purchases | Revenue, ROAS | "Buy running shoes online" |
| **Leads** | B2B, services, SaaS signups | Cost per lead, form submissions | "Free CRM demo" |
| **Website Traffic** | Content promotion, blog visits | CPC, sessions | "Digital marketing tips" |
| **Brand Awareness** | Top-of-funnel visibility | Impressions, reach, CPM | "Best project management tool" |
| **App Promotion** | Mobile app installs | Cost per install, in-app actions | "Download fitness app" |
| **Local Store Visits** | Physical retail, restaurants | Store visits, calls, directions | "Gym near Koramangala" |

**Real-Time Example — Swiggy:**
- **Sales objective:** Campaigns targeting "order food online Bangalore" — optimizing for completed orders
- **App promotion:** Campaigns targeting "food delivery app" — optimizing for app installs
- **Local visits:** Campaigns targeting "restaurants near me" — driving to partner restaurants

### Network Settings

| Network | What It Does | When to Use | When to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Search** | Your ads appear on Google.com search results | Always for Search campaigns | Never disable this |
| **Search Partners** | Extends to Google Maps, Google Shopping, partner sites (AOL, Ask.com) | When you want extra reach at lower CPCs | When you need precise quality control |
| **Display Expansion** | Shows Search ads on Display Network placements | Testing broader reach | When you want pure search performance |

**Best Practice:** Start with Google Search only. Add Search Partners after 30 days if performance is good. Avoid Display Expansion for search campaigns (create separate Display campaigns instead).

### Budget Settings

**Daily Budget Calculation:**
```
Monthly budget ÷ 30.4 = Daily budget

Example: Rs. 50,000/month ÷ 30.4 = Rs. 1,645/day

Note: Google can spend up to 2x your daily budget on high-traffic days
      but will never exceed monthly cap (daily × 30.4)
```

**Shared Budgets:**
- Pool budgets across multiple campaigns
- Google auto-distributes based on opportunity
- Use for campaigns with related goals but different keyword volumes

### Bidding Strategies — Complete Guide

```
BIDDING STRATEGY DECISION TREE

                   What is your goal?
                        │
           ┌────────────┼────────────────┐
           │            │                │
        Traffic     Conversions      Revenue
           │            │                │
    ┌──────┴──────┐  ┌──┴──────┐   ┌────┴────┐
    │             │  │         │   │         │
  Manual      Maximize  Target  Maximize  Target
   CPC        Clicks    CPA    Conv.Value  ROAS
                        │
                   Enhanced

```

| Strategy | How It Works | Best For | Minimum Data Needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Manual CPC** | You set each keyword's bid | Full control, new accounts | None |
| **Enhanced CPC** | Manual + Google adjusts bids up/down by up to 30% for likely conversions | Transitioning to automation | 15+ conversions/month |
| **Maximize Clicks** | Google auto-bids to get most clicks within budget | Traffic goals, data gathering | None |
| **Maximize Conversions** | Google auto-bids to get most conversions within budget | Lead gen with sufficient data | 30+ conversions/month |
| **Target CPA** | Google auto-bids to hit your target cost-per-acquisition | Predictable lead cost | 30+ conversions/month |
| **Target ROAS** | Google auto-bids to hit your target return on ad spend | E-commerce with revenue tracking | 50+ conversions/month |
| **Maximize Conversion Value** | Google auto-bids to maximize total conversion value | Revenue maximization | 30+ conversions/month |

**Real-Time Example — Bidding Strategy Evolution for a SaaS Company:**
- **Month 1-2:** Manual CPC (gathering data, understanding which keywords convert)
- **Month 3:** Enhanced CPC (letting Google fine-tune bids with early conversion data)
- **Month 4+:** Target CPA at Rs. 1,500/lead (once they have 30+ conversions/month and know their target CPA)

### Ad Scheduling (Dayparting)

**Why it matters:** Not all hours convert equally. A B2B SaaS product gets most demos booked during business hours (9 AM - 6 PM weekdays), while an e-commerce food delivery business peaks at 11 AM - 2 PM and 7 PM - 10 PM.

**Implementation:**
1. Run campaigns for 2-4 weeks with 24/7 scheduling to gather data
2. Analyze conversion rate by hour and day in Google Ads (Campaigns > Segments > Time > Hour of Day)
3. Apply bid adjustments: +20% during peak hours, -50% during low-converting hours

**Real-Time Example — Domino's Pizza:**
- **Peak hours (7 PM - 10 PM):** +30% bid adjustment
- **Late night (12 AM - 6 AM):** -60% bid adjustment (lower orders)
- **Lunch rush (12 PM - 2 PM):** +20% bid adjustment
- **Monday vs. Saturday:** Saturday gets +15% (higher order volume)

### Device Targeting

| Device | Typical Behavior | Bid Adjustment Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Mobile** | 65% of searches, higher CTR, lower conversion rate, shorter sessions | Default or +10-20% for local/call-focused |
| **Desktop** | 30% of searches, higher conversion rate, longer sessions, larger orders | +15-25% for B2B, complex purchases |
| **Tablet** | 5% of searches, declining usage | Often -20% to -50% |

## 6.2.2 Keyword Strategy for PPC

### Keyword Match Types

```
MATCH TYPE SPECTRUM: Control vs. Reach

Most Control ◄────────────────────────────────► Most Reach
[exact match]    "phrase match"    broad match

┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                          │
│  EXACT MATCH: [running shoes for men]                    │
│  Triggers: running shoes for men, men's running shoes    │
│  Won't trigger: best running shoes, running sneakers     │
│                                                          │
│  PHRASE MATCH: "running shoes for men"                   │
│  Triggers: buy running shoes for men, running shoes      │
│            for men online, affordable running shoes men   │
│  Won't trigger: shoes for running (different order       │
│                 changes meaning)                         │
│                                                          │
│  BROAD MATCH: running shoes for men                      │
│  Triggers: men's athletic footwear, jogging sneakers,    │
│            marathon training shoes, sports shoes guys     │
│  Risk: Also triggers: shoe repair, running shorts        │
│                                                          │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Match Type Strategy by Budget:**

| Budget Level | Strategy |
|---|---|
| **Low (< Rs. 30K/month)** | 70% exact, 30% phrase. No broad match. |
| **Medium (Rs. 30K - 2L/month)** | 50% exact, 35% phrase, 15% broad (with negatives) |
| **High (Rs. 2L+/month)** | 40% exact, 30% phrase, 30% broad (with Smart Bidding) |

**Real-Time Example — Boat (Audio Products):**
- **Exact:** [boat airdopes 141] — captures brand-specific purchase intent
- **Phrase:** "wireless earbuds under 2000" — captures price-sensitive shoppers
- **Broad:** noise cancelling headphones — captures broad audience (with heavy negative keyword lists)

### Negative Keywords

Negative keywords prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches. They're as important as your actual keywords.

**Types of Negative Keywords:**

```
Account-Level Negatives (apply to ALL campaigns):
- free, download, torrent, pirated, hack, crack, salary, jobs, interview

Campaign-Level Negatives (specific to one campaign):
- Campaign "Premium CRM": negative → "free," "open source," "cheap"
- Campaign "CRM India": negative → "USA," "UK," "Australia"

Ad Group-Level Negatives (prevents overlap between ad groups):
- Ad Group "Running Shoes": negative → "formal," "casual," "baby"
- Ad Group "Formal Shoes": negative → "running," "sports," "gym"
```

**Real-Time Example — An Online Course Platform (like Coursera):**
Without negatives, their ad for "Python course" might show for:
- "python snake" (animal)
- "python movie" (entertainment)
- "python course free download" (won't pay)
- "python jobs" (not buying a course)
- "python salary" (research intent)

Negative keyword list: -snake, -movie, -free, -download, -jobs, -salary, -interview, -questions

### Long-Tail vs. Short-Tail Keyword Economics

```
SHORT-TAIL                          LONG-TAIL
"shoes"                             "red nike running shoes size 10 men"
│                                   │
├── Volume: 500K searches/mo        ├── Volume: 50 searches/mo
├── CPC: Rs. 30-80                  ├── CPC: Rs. 5-15
├── Competition: Very High          ├── Competition: Low
├── Conversion Rate: 1-2%           ├── Conversion Rate: 8-15%
├── Intent: Vague                   ├── Intent: Very specific (ready to buy)
└── Cost to Test: Rs. 50,000+       └── Cost to Test: Rs. 2,000-5,000
```

**The Long-Tail PPC Strategy:**
- Target 100+ long-tail keywords instead of 10 expensive short-tail keywords
- Lower CPCs, higher conversion rates, less competition
- Combined volume from 100 long-tail keywords can exceed 1 short-tail keyword

**Real-Time Example — Lenskart:**
- Short-tail: "sunglasses" — CPC Rs. 25, conversion 1.5%
- Long-tail: "ray ban aviator sunglasses gold frame" — CPC Rs. 6, conversion 12%
- Lenskart builds thousands of long-tail ad groups by brand + style + color + feature

### Search Term Reports and Query Mining

The Search Terms Report (found in Keywords > Search Terms) shows the ACTUAL queries users typed before clicking your ad.

**Weekly Query Mining Process:**
1. Export search terms from the last 7 days
2. Sort by cost (highest first) — find expensive terms with no conversions
3. Add irrelevant terms as negative keywords
4. Find high-converting terms not in your keyword list — add them as exact match keywords
5. Identify new ad group ideas from patterns in search terms

**Real-Time Example:**
A real estate company advertising "2BHK flat in Whitefield Bangalore" discovers through search terms:
- "2BHK flat for rent Whitefield" — They sell, not rent. Add "rent" as negative
- "2BHK flat in Whitefield under 50 lakhs" — Their flats start at 70L. Add "under 50 lakhs" as negative
- "2BHK flat Whitefield with parking" — High converter. Create a new ad group for parking-related queries

## 6.2.3 Ad Copy Creation

### Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)

RSAs are the default (and now only) search ad format. You provide multiple headlines and descriptions, and Google automatically tests combinations.

```
RSA STRUCTURE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Headline 1 | Headline 2 | Headline 3                  │
│  (up to 30 chars each)                                  │
│  You provide: up to 15 headlines                        │
│  Google shows: 2-3 per ad                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Description 1 | Description 2                          │
│  (up to 90 chars each)                                  │
│  You provide: up to 4 descriptions                      │
│  Google shows: 1-2 per ad                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Display URL: www.example.com/path1/path2               │
│  Final URL: https://www.example.com/landing-page        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Total possible combinations with 15 headlines and 4 descriptions:
15 × 14 × 13 × 4 × 3 = 32,760 combinations
Google tests these automatically to find winning combos.
```

### Headline Best Practices

**The 15-Headline Framework:**

| Headline # | Category | Example (for CRM Software) |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | **Keyword-focused** | Best CRM Software 2025 / Top-Rated CRM Platform / CRM for Growing Teams |
| 4-5 | **Benefit-focused** | Close Deals 3x Faster / Boost Sales by 40% |
| 6-7 | **Feature-focused** | AI-Powered Lead Scoring / Built-in Email Automation |
| 8-9 | **Trust/Social proof** | Trusted by 50,000+ Companies / 4.8★ Rating on G2 |
| 10-11 | **Offer/CTA** | Start Free 14-Day Trial / Get Your Free Demo Today |
| 12-13 | **Urgency/Scarcity** | Limited Time: 30% Off / Offer Ends This Friday |
| 14-15 | **Differentiator** | No Credit Card Required / Setup in Under 5 Minutes |

### Dynamic Keyword Insertion (DKI)

DKI automatically inserts the user's search query into your ad headline:

```
Headline: Buy {KeyWord:Quality Shoes} Online

If user searches "Nike Air Max" → Ad shows: Buy Nike Air Max Online
If user searches "running shoes" → Ad shows: Buy Running Shoes Online
If keyword is too long → Ad shows: Buy Quality Shoes Online (default text)
```

**When to Use DKI:**
- E-commerce with many product variations
- When you want maximum relevance to search queries
- Large keyword lists where custom ads for each keyword aren't practical

**When to AVOID DKI:**
- Competitor keywords (your ad would show competitor's name)
- Misspelled keywords (ad would show the typo)
- Sensitive industries (legal, medical — needs precise messaging)

### Ad Customizers

| Customizer | What It Does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Countdown** | Shows a live countdown to a deadline | "Sale Ends in 3 Days, 14 Hours!" |
| **Location** | Inserts user's city or region | "Best Dentist in {LOCATION(City)}" |
| **Price** | Shows dynamic pricing | "Starting at {PRICE}" |
| **IF function** | Shows different text based on conditions | IF(device=mobile, "Call Now") ELSE("Book Online") |

**Real-Time Example — Countdown for Amazon Great Indian Festival:**

```
Headline: Great Indian Festival — Ends in {=COUNTDOWN("2025/10/15 23:59:59","en-US",5)}!

Day 1: "Great Indian Festival — Ends in 7 Days!"
Day 5: "Great Indian Festival — Ends in 2 Days!"
Day 7: "Great Indian Festival — Ends in 5 Hours!"

The countdown creates urgency and consistently improves CTR by 15-30%.
```

### Ad Pinning Strategy

Pinning forces a specific headline to always appear in a specific position. Use it sparingly.

**When to Pin:**
- **Pin Position 1:** Brand name or legal requirement (e.g., "UpGrad Online Education")
- **Pin Position 2:** Your strongest value proposition that must always show
- **Never Pin Position 3:** Let Google optimize this slot

**Impact:** Over-pinning reduces Google's ability to test combinations, which lowers Ad Strength and can hurt performance.

### A/B Testing Ad Copy

**Testing Framework:**
```
Test ONE element at a time:

Test 1: Emotional vs. Rational Headline
  A: "Transform Your Career in 6 Months"  (emotional)
  B: "MBA Program — AICTE Approved"        (rational)
  Winner: A (22% higher CTR)

Test 2: CTA Variation
  A: "Start Free Trial" 
  B: "Get Started Today"
  Winner: B (15% higher conversion rate)

Test 3: Social Proof vs. Feature
  A: "Trusted by 10,000+ Companies"
  B: "AI-Powered Analytics Dashboard"
  Winner: A (18% higher CTR)

Run each test for 2-4 weeks or until statistically significant (95% confidence).
Minimum data: 100 clicks per variant, 30 conversions per variant.
```

## 6.2.5 Landing Page Alignment

### Message Match Between Ad and Landing Page

The #1 landing page rule: what the ad promises, the page must deliver immediately.

```
BAD MESSAGE MATCH:
Ad: "Get 50% Off Running Shoes — Nike, Adidas & More"
Landing Page: Homepage showing all product categories
→ User has to SEARCH for running shoes and the 50% offer
→ Bounce rate: 70%+

GOOD MESSAGE MATCH:
Ad: "Get 50% Off Running Shoes — Nike, Adidas & More"
Landing Page: Dedicated page showing ONLY running shoes with "50% OFF" banner
→ User immediately sees what was promised
→ Bounce rate: 25-35%
→ Conversion rate: 3-5x higher
```

### Dedicated PPC Landing Pages vs. Existing Pages

| Aspect | Website Page | Dedicated PPC Landing Page |
|---|---|---|
| **Navigation** | Full site navigation (many exit points) | Minimal or no navigation (focused) |
| **Content** | General content for SEO | Specific content matching the ad |
| **CTA** | Multiple CTAs | Single, clear CTA |
| **Speed** | May be heavy with scripts | Stripped down, fast loading |
| **A/B Testing** | Changes affect SEO | Test freely without SEO impact |
| **Conversion Rate** | 2-5% typical | 8-15% possible |

**Real-Time Example — Razorpay PPC Landing Pages:**

For the keyword "payment gateway for startups," Razorpay doesn't send users to razorpay.com (homepage with enterprise, APIs, banking, payroll, etc.). They send users to a dedicated page:
- **Headline:** "Payment Gateway Built for Indian Startups"
- **Subheadline:** "Go live in 5 minutes. Zero setup fees."
- **Social proof:** "50,000+ startups trust Razorpay"
- **Single CTA:** "Sign Up Free" (only one button on the page)
- **No navigation menu** — nowhere to wander off

### Landing Page Quality Score Optimization Checklist

1. **Load speed:** Under 3 seconds (use Google PageSpeed Insights)
2. **Mobile-friendly:** Responsive, thumb-friendly buttons, readable text
3. **Relevant content:** Headline matches ad promise, keyword present on page
4. **Clear CTA:** Above the fold, contrasting color, action-oriented text
5. **Trust signals:** Testimonials, reviews, security badges, client logos
6. **Original content:** Not duplicated from other sites
7. **Easy navigation:** Simple layout, no distracting elements
8. **Transparency:** Privacy policy linked, business info available

## Implementation Steps

### Step 1: Campaign Planning (Day 1-3)
1. Define campaign objective (sales, leads, traffic)
2. Research keywords using Google Keyword Planner
3. Group keywords into themed ad groups (10-20 keywords per group)
4. Build negative keyword lists
5. Set budget and choose initial bidding strategy (manual CPC)

### Step 2: Campaign Build (Day 3-5)
1. Create campaign with proper settings (location, language, network)
2. Build ad groups with themed keyword lists
3. Write RSAs (15 headlines, 4 descriptions per ad group — minimum 2 ads per group)
4. Add all relevant extensions (sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, etc.)
5. Set ad schedule based on expected peak hours
6. Create or assign dedicated landing pages

### Step 3: Tracking Setup (Day 5-6)
1. Install Google Ads conversion tag via GTM
2. Set up conversion actions (form submit, purchase, phone call)
3. Link Google Ads to GA4
4. Verify conversions are firing correctly with Tag Assistant

### Step 4: Launch & Initial Monitoring (Week 1-2)
1. Launch campaign, monitor hourly on day 1
2. Check Search Terms Report daily — add negatives
3. Monitor Quality Score for each keyword
4. Ensure ads are approved and serving
5. Watch budget pacing — not over/under spending

### Step 5: Optimization Cycle (Ongoing — Weekly)
1. Review search terms → add negatives
2. Pause low-performing keywords (high CPC, no conversions after 50+ clicks)
3. Test new ad copy variants
4. Adjust bids based on device, location, time performance
5. Expand to new keyword opportunities from search term mining

## Gap Finding

| Gap | Symptom | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low Quality Score** | CPC increasing, ad position dropping | Check QS components in keyword tab | Improve ad relevance, landing page, CTR |
| **High CPC, low conversions** | Spending budget but no results | Keywords are too broad or wrong intent | Tighten match types, add negatives |
| **Low impression share** | Ads not showing often enough | Budget too low or bids too low | Increase budget or improve QS to lower CPC |
| **Poor CTR (<2%)** | Lots of impressions, few clicks | Ad copy not compelling | Rewrite headlines, add extensions, test DKI |
| **High bounce rate** | Clicks but users leave immediately | Landing page mismatch | Fix message match, improve page speed |
| **Ad disapprovals** | Ads not running | Policy violations | Review Google Ads policies, fix copy/landing page |
| **Missing extensions** | Lower CTR, less ad real estate | Extensions not set up | Add all 8+ extension types |

## Strategies

1. **Alpha-Beta Campaign Structure** — "Alpha" campaigns contain proven exact-match winners; "Beta" campaigns run broad/phrase match to discover new winners. Winners from Beta move to Alpha.
2. **SKAG to STAG Transition** — Single Keyword Ad Groups (SKAGs) were the gold standard but are now less effective with close variants. Modern approach: Single Theme Ad Groups (STAGs) with 5-15 closely related keywords.
3. **The Branded Moat** — Always bid on your own brand name. It's cheap (Rs. 1-3 CPC), prevents competitors from stealing your brand traffic, and controls your messaging.
4. **Competitor Keyword Bidding** — Create dedicated campaigns bidding on competitor brand names. Use ad copy that differentiates (never use competitor's trademark in ad text).
5. **Funnel-Based Keywords** — Top funnel: "what is CRM" (awareness). Mid funnel: "best CRM software" (consideration). Bottom funnel: "Freshdesk pricing" (decision). Different bids and ads for each stage.

## Best Practices

1. **Write ad copy that mirrors the keyword** — If the keyword is "affordable CRM for startups," the headline should contain those words
2. **Use all 15 headline slots** — Google rewards ad variety with better Ad Strength and more testing
3. **Create at least 2 RSAs per ad group** — Google recommends this for proper A/B testing
4. **Check Quality Score weekly** — It directly impacts your CPC and ad position
5. **Use ad customizers for urgency** — Countdown timers improve CTR by 15-30%
6. **Set up call tracking** — Many conversions happen via phone, especially mobile
7. **Leverage audience layering** — Add audiences in Observation mode to learn which audiences convert best, then bid higher on them
8. **Never stop adding negatives** — The search terms report is a goldmine; mine it weekly
9. **Test one variable at a time** — Don't change headlines, descriptions, AND landing pages simultaneously
10. **Pin strategically** — Only pin headlines that MUST show (brand name, legal disclaimers)
