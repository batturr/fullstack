# Module 14: Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

---

## 14.1 CRO Fundamentals

### 14.1.1 What Is Conversion Rate Optimization
- Definition: systematically improving the percentage of visitors who take a desired action
- Types of conversions: macro (purchase, sign-up) and micro (add to cart, newsletter, click)
- The CRO mindset: experimentation over opinion
- CRO vs. traffic acquisition: improving what you have
- The economics of CRO: impact on CAC, revenue, and profitability
- CRO across the funnel: awareness, consideration, decision, retention

### 14.1.2 The CRO Process
- Research → Hypothesize → Prioritize → Test → Analyze → Implement → Repeat
- Building a CRO program from scratch
- Quick wins vs. strategic improvements
- CRO team roles: strategist, analyst, designer, developer, copywriter
- CRO culture: embedding experimentation into the organization
- CRO roadmap and testing velocity

### 14.1.3 Conversion Rate Benchmarks
- Industry benchmarks by sector and conversion type
- Device-specific benchmarks (mobile vs. desktop vs. tablet)
- Channel-specific benchmarks (organic, paid, email, social)
- Understanding context: why benchmarks are directional, not absolute
- Setting realistic improvement targets

## 14.2 CRO Research & Analysis

### 14.2.1 Quantitative Research
- Google Analytics: funnel analysis, drop-off identification, segment comparison
- Heatmaps: click maps, scroll maps, movement maps (Hotjar, Crazy Egg, Lucky Orange)
- Session recordings: watching real user interactions
- Form analytics: field-level drop-off, completion times
- A/B testing data from previous experiments
- Page-level metrics: bounce rate, exit rate, time on page
- Device and browser-specific performance
- Technical performance data: speed, errors, broken elements

### 14.2.2 Qualitative Research
- User surveys: on-site polls, exit surveys, post-purchase surveys
- User interviews: structured conversations about experience and barriers
- Customer support analysis: common complaints, confusion points, feature requests
- Live chat and chatbot transcript analysis
- Social media sentiment and feedback
- Review analysis: what customers love and hate
- Sales team insights: objections, questions, deal-breakers

### 14.2.3 Usability Testing
- Moderated usability testing: in-person or remote with facilitator
- Unmoderated usability testing: tools like UserTesting.com, Maze, Lookback
- Task-based testing scenarios
- Think-aloud protocol
- Five-second tests (first impressions)
- Card sorting for information architecture
- Tree testing for navigation
- Preference testing for design options
- Accessibility testing as part of usability

### 14.2.4 Competitive & Heuristic Analysis
- Heuristic evaluation frameworks:
  - Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics
  - LIFT model (Value, Clarity, Relevance, Urgency, Anxiety, Distraction)
  - Eisenberg's Persuasion Architecture
  - Cialdini's principles applied to web pages
- Expert walkthrough of key conversion paths
- Competitive analysis: sign-up flows, checkout processes, landing pages
- Identifying friction points and conversion barriers
- Cognitive walkthrough methodology

## 14.3 Hypothesis & Prioritization

### 14.3.1 Building Strong Hypotheses
- Hypothesis structure: "If we [change], then [outcome], because [reason/evidence]"
- Data-informed hypotheses vs. opinion-based guesses
- Linking hypotheses to research findings
- Categorizing hypotheses by page, funnel stage, and element
- Hypothesis documentation and repository
- Examples of strong vs. weak hypotheses

### 14.3.2 Prioritization Frameworks
- ICE framework: Impact, Confidence, Ease (1-10 scoring)
- PIE framework: Potential, Importance, Ease
- RICE framework: Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort
- PXL framework: Widerfunnel's evidence-based prioritization
- Opportunity scoring
- Building a prioritized testing backlog
- Balancing quick wins with high-impact experiments

### 14.4.4 Personalization Testing

## 14.6 Psychology & Persuasion in CRO

### 14.6.1 Behavioral Psychology Principles
- Loss aversion: "Don't miss out" vs. "Get this benefit"
- Anchoring: showing higher price first, then discounted
- The decoy effect: three-tier pricing
- Social proof: bandwagon effect, expert endorsement
- Scarcity: limited time, limited stock, exclusive access
- Reciprocity: giving value before asking for conversion
- Commitment and consistency: micro-commitments leading to larger ones
- The endowment effect: virtual ownership through customization

### 14.6.2 Neuromarketing Insights
- Eye-tracking patterns and attention mapping
- Emotional design and aesthetic usability effect
- Cognitive load reduction
- Choice architecture and default options
- The paradox of choice: reducing options to increase conversions
- Priming and framing effects
- Color psychology in conversion design
