# Module 04: Content Marketing

---

## 4.1 Content Marketing Strategy

### 4.1.1 What Is Content Marketing
- Definition: creating and distributing valuable, relevant content to attract and retain a defined audience
- Content marketing vs. advertising vs. branded content
- The business case for content marketing (ROI data and case studies)
- Content marketing maturity model: from random acts of content to strategic operations
- How content marketing fits within the overall digital strategy

### 4.1.2 Content Strategy Framework
- Content mission statement: who you serve, how, and what outcome
- Content pillars: 3-5 core themes aligned with business goals
- Content tiers: hero (big-bet), hub (recurring), hygiene (always-on) — Google's framework
- Audience-first content planning
- Content differentiation: finding your unique angle
- Documenting your content strategy

### 4.1.3 Content Audit & Gap Analysis
- Inventorying existing content assets
- Evaluating content performance (traffic, engagement, conversions, backlinks)
- Content scoring matrix
- Identifying content gaps vs. competitor coverage
- Decisions: keep, update, merge, redirect, or delete
- Content decay: identifying and refreshing declining content

### 4.1.4 Editorial Calendar & Workflow
- Building an editorial calendar (monthly, quarterly, annual)
- Tools: Notion, Trello, Asana, CoSchedule, Airtable, Monday.com
- Content production workflow: ideation → briefing → creation → editing → approval → publish → promote
- Content briefs: what to include (target keyword, intent, outline, examples, CTA)
- Managing freelance writers and content teams
- Content governance and brand voice guidelines

## 4.2 Content Creation — Written Content

### 4.2.1 Blog Writing Mastery
- Blog post structures: listicles, how-to guides, opinion pieces, case studies, roundups
- Writing compelling headlines (formulas, power words, numbers, curiosity gaps)
- Introduction hooks: statistics, questions, stories, bold statements
- Body structure: subheadings, short paragraphs, bullet points, transitions
- Conclusion and CTA best practices
- Blog post length optimization by intent and competition
- Writing for scannability and readability (Flesch-Kincaid, Hemingway)

### 4.2.2 Long-Form Content & Pillar Pages
- What makes effective long-form content (2,000-10,000+ words)
- Pillar page design and structure
- Topic cluster model: pillar → cluster → supporting content
- Internal linking within clusters
- Visual elements in long-form content
- Updating and maintaining pillar content over time

### 4.2.3 Copywriting for Conversions
- Features vs. benefits writing
- Persuasive copywriting formulas:
  - PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
  - AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
  - BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
  - 4Ps (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push)
  - The StoryBrand framework (Donald Miller)
- Writing CTAs that convert
- Microcopy: button text, form labels, error messages, tooltips
- Headline testing and optimization
- Writing for different funnel stages

### 4.2.4 Other Written Content Types
- Whitepapers and research reports
- E-books and downloadable guides
- Case studies and success stories
- Newsletters and email content
- Press releases
- Product descriptions and catalog copy
- Social media captions and posts
- Scriptwriting for video and podcasts
