## 6.4.1 Google Merchant Center

### Setting Up Merchant Center Account

Google Merchant Center is the central hub where you manage product data that powers Shopping ads, free product listings, and local inventory ads.

**Setup Process:**

```
MERCHANT CENTER SETUP FLOW

Step 1: Create account at merchants.google.com
            │
Step 2: Verify and claim your website
            │ (HTML tag, Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, or DNS)
            │
Step 3: Set up business information
            │ - Business name, address, country
            │ - Return policy, shipping settings
            │ - Tax settings (US only)
            │
Step 4: Create product feed
            │ (manual, Google Sheets, API, or third-party tool)
            │
Step 5: Submit feed for review
            │ (Google reviews within 3-5 business days)
            │
Step 6: Link to Google Ads account
            │
Step 7: Launch Shopping campaigns
```

**Real-Time Example — A Shopify Store Selling Organic Skincare:**
1. Create Merchant Center account for "PureGlow Organics"
2. Verify website pureglow.in via Google Tag Manager
3. Set shipping: Free shipping above Rs. 500, Rs. 49 flat rate below
4. Set return policy: 15-day easy returns
5. Install Shopify's Google channel plugin → auto-syncs 200 products to Merchant Center
6. Products go live within 3-5 days after review

### Product Feed Creation and Optimization

The product feed is a file (XML, CSV, TSV, or Google Sheets) containing all your product data. Feed quality directly impacts Shopping ad visibility and performance.

### Feed Attributes — The Critical Ones

| Attribute | Required? | Max Length | Optimization Tips |
|---|---|---|---|
| **id** | Yes | 50 chars | Use SKU, keep consistent, never change |
| **title** | Yes | 150 chars | Front-load brand + product type + key attributes |
| **description** | Yes | 5,000 chars | Include keywords naturally, describe features and benefits |
| **link** | Yes | — | Direct URL to product page (canonical, HTTPS) |
| **image_link** | Yes | — | High-quality, white background, 800×800 px minimum |
| **price** | Yes | — | Must match landing page price exactly |
| **availability** | Yes | — | in stock / out of stock / preorder |
| **brand** | Yes (most) | 70 chars | Official brand name |
| **GTIN** | Yes (branded) | — | Global Trade Item Number (barcode/EAN) |
| **MPN** | If no GTIN | 70 chars | Manufacturer Part Number |
| **condition** | Yes | — | new / refurbished / used |
| **google_product_category** | Recommended | — | Use Google's taxonomy for accurate categorization |
| **product_type** | Recommended | 750 chars | Your own category hierarchy |
| **color** | Recommended | 100 chars | Improves filtering accuracy |
| **size** | Recommended | 100 chars | Critical for apparel and footwear |
| **custom_label_0 to 4** | Optional | 100 chars | For campaign segmentation (explained below) |

### Product Feed Troubleshooting

```
COMMON FEED ERRORS AND FIXES

Error: "Missing GTIN for branded products"
Fix: Add EAN/UPC/ISBN for all branded products. 
     For custom/handmade products, set identifier_exists = false

Error: "Price mismatch between feed and landing page"
Fix: Ensure feed price matches the price shown on the product page
     Including currency. Schedule frequent feed updates.

Error: "Image too small"
Fix: Use minimum 800×800 px images. 
     Ideal: 1200×1200 px or higher. 
     White background preferred for apparel/electronics.

Error: "Automatic item disapproval — policy violation"
Fix: Check for prohibited content (counterfeit, weapons, supplements).
     Review Google's Shopping ads policies.

Error: "Limited performance — missing attributes"
Fix: Add optional attributes: color, size, material, pattern, age_group.
     More attributes = better matching to user queries.
```

### Free Product Listings

Google offers free product listings in Shopping tab, Google Search, Google Images, and YouTube.

**How to enable:**
1. In Merchant Center, go to Growth > Manage Programs
2. Enable "Free Listings" (previously "Surfaces across Google")
3. Ensure feed meets all quality requirements
4. Products appear in the Shopping tab alongside paid results (labeled differently)

**Real-Time Example:** A small Etsy-like seller of handmade candles with zero ad budget can still appear in Google Shopping's free listings by simply maintaining a clean product feed. They appear below paid Shopping ads but still get clicks — essentially free traffic.

## 6.4.2 Shopping Campaigns

### Standard Shopping vs. Performance Max for Shopping

| Aspect | Standard Shopping | Performance Max (Shopping) |
|---|---|---|
| **Control** | High — choose products, set bids, see search terms | Low — Google automates everything |
| **Targeting** | Product-based (no keyword targeting) | AI-driven across all channels |
| **Channels** | Search, Shopping tab, Search Partners | Search, Shopping, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, Maps |
| **Reporting** | Full search term reports | Limited insights, no search terms |
| **Best for** | Advertisers wanting control and transparency | Advertisers wanting maximum reach with AI |
| **Minimum data** | Works from day one | Needs 30+ conversions/month for optimal performance |

### Campaign Structure and Product Group Segmentation

```
SHOPPING CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

Campaign: Shopping — All Products
│
├── Product Group: Electronics
│   ├── Brand: Samsung → Bid: Rs. 15
│   ├── Brand: Apple → Bid: Rs. 25
│   └── Brand: Everything Else → Bid: Rs. 8
│
├── Product Group: Apparel
│   ├── Custom Label 0: "Bestseller" → Bid: Rs. 12
│   ├── Custom Label 0: "Clearance" → Bid: Rs. 5
│   └── Custom Label 0: "New Arrival" → Bid: Rs. 10
│
└── Product Group: Home & Kitchen
    ├── Category: Kitchen Appliances → Bid: Rs. 10
    └── Category: Furniture → Bid: Rs. 18


PRIORITY-BASED STRUCTURE (Advanced):

Campaign: High Priority (low bid, broad, catches all queries)
├── Negative keywords: None
├── Bid: Rs. 5 (catches general queries like "shoes")
│
Campaign: Medium Priority (medium bid, some negatives)
├── Negative keywords: Brand names
├── Bid: Rs. 12 (catches category queries like "running shoes men")
│
Campaign: Low Priority (high bid, heavy negatives)
├── Negative keywords: Generic + category terms
├── Bid: Rs. 25 (catches high-intent queries like "Nike Air Max 90 size 10")
```

### Custom Labels for Product Grouping

Custom labels (0-4) are your own tags added to the feed to segment products beyond Google's taxonomy.

**Strategic Custom Label Framework:**

| Custom Label | Strategy | Values |
|---|---|---|
| **custom_label_0** | Margin tiers | "High Margin," "Medium Margin," "Low Margin" |
| **custom_label_1** | Performance tiers | "Bestseller," "Average," "Slow Mover" |
| **custom_label_2** | Seasonality | "Summer," "Winter," "Year-Round," "Diwali" |
| **custom_label_3** | Price range | "Under 500," "500-2000," "2000-5000," "Above 5000" |
| **custom_label_4** | Promotion status | "On Sale," "Full Price," "Clearance," "New" |

**Real-Time Example — Nykaa Custom Labels:**
- custom_label_0: "High Margin" for store-brand products, "Low Margin" for third-party brands
- custom_label_1: "Top 100 Bestsellers" (bid higher), "New Launch" (test bids)
- custom_label_2: "Monsoon" for hair care products, "Summer" for sunscreens
- Strategy: Bid 40% higher on "High Margin + Bestseller" products vs. "Low Margin + Slow Mover"

### Shopping Ad Optimization

**Title Optimization Checklist:**
1. Front-load the most important keywords (Brand + Product Type)
2. Include size, color, material where applicable
3. Match titles to how users actually search
4. Use A/B testing tools (DataFeedWatch) to test title variations
5. Use supplemental feeds to update titles without changing the main feed

**Image Optimization:**
- White background performs best for product images
- Show the product in use for lifestyle categories
- Multiple images (additional_image_link) show in free listings
- Avoid text, watermarks, or promotional overlays on images
- Optimal size: 1200×1200 px (square format)

**Reviews and Ratings:**
- Enable Google Product Ratings (requires 50+ reviews)
- Use approved third-party review partners: Trustpilot, Yotpo, Bazaarvoice
- Products with reviews get 17% higher CTR on average
- Star ratings appear below the product image in Shopping ads

## 6.4.3 Dynamic Remarketing

### Setting Up Dynamic Remarketing

Dynamic remarketing shows users the exact products they viewed on your website, personalized at scale.

**Setup Requirements:**

```
DYNAMIC REMARKETING SETUP

1. Google Ads Remarketing Tag (global site tag)
   ├── Must fire on ALL pages
   └── Must pass product IDs that match your Merchant Center feed

2. Audience Lists Created:
   ├── All Visitors (tag fires on any page)
   ├── Product Viewers (tag fires on product pages with product ID)
   ├── Cart Abandoners (tag fires on cart page)
   ├── Purchasers (tag fires on thank-you page with transaction data)
   └── Custom combinations (e.g., viewed 3+ products but didn't buy)

3. Link Merchant Center feed to Google Ads
   └── Products dynamically pulled from feed into ad creatives

4. Create Dynamic Remarketing campaign (Display)
   ├── Select audience list
   ├── Google automatically creates personalized ads
   └── Each user sees different products based on their browsing
```

### Audience List Strategies

```
REMARKETING AUDIENCE PYRAMID

                    ┌─────────────────────┐
                    │  Past Purchasers    │ ← Cross-sell, upsell
                    │  (30-90 day window) │    "You bought X, try Y"
                    ├─────────────────────┤
                    │  Cart Abandoners    │ ← Urgency, discount
                    │  (1-7 day window)   │    "Complete your order, 10% off"
                    ├─────────────────────┤
                    │  Product Viewers    │ ← Product reminder, reviews
                    │  (7-14 day window)  │    "Still interested in X?"
                    ├─────────────────────┤
                    │  Category Browsers  │ ← Category-level ads
                    │  (14-30 day window) │    "Explore our running shoes"
                    ├─────────────────────┤
                    │  All Visitors       │ ← Brand awareness
                    │  (30-60 day window) │    "Discover PureGlow Organics"
                    └─────────────────────┘

                Higher in pyramid = Higher intent = Higher bid
```

### Cross-Sell and Upsell Remarketing Strategies

| Strategy | Audience | Ad Message | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cross-sell** | Past purchasers | "Customers who bought X also love Y" | Bought a phone → show phone cases |
| **Upsell** | Past purchasers | "Upgrade to premium version" | Bought basic plan → show Pro plan |
| **Replenishment** | Past purchasers (time-based) | "Time to restock?" | Bought protein powder 30 days ago → remind to reorder |
| **Win-back** | Inactive customers (90+ days) | "We miss you! Here's 20% off" | Hasn't purchased in 3 months → special offer |

**Real-Time Example — BigBasket Dynamic Remarketing:**
- You browse BigBasket, view "Amul Butter 500g" and "Tata Tea Gold 1kg" but don't order
- Next day, browsing Times of India, you see a BigBasket display ad showing those exact products with "Order now, delivery by 8 PM today"
- You order. BigBasket acquired that sale for Rs. 3 CPC instead of Rs. 15 for a cold acquisition

### Frequency and Duration Settings

| Audience | Membership Duration | Frequency Cap | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cart Abandoners | 7 days | 10-15/week | High intent, short window before they buy elsewhere |
| Product Viewers | 14 days | 5-7/week | Moderate intent, give enough reminders |
| Category Browsers | 30 days | 3-5/week | Low intent, don't annoy |
| Past Purchasers | 90-180 days | 2-3/week | Relationship maintenance, not aggressive |
| All Visitors | 30 days | 3-4/week | Gentle reminder, brand recall |

## Implementation Steps

### Google Merchant Center Setup
1. Create account at merchants.google.com
2. Verify and claim your website (HTML tag, GTM, or DNS)
3. Configure shipping settings (rates, speed, free shipping thresholds)
4. Set return policies (window, conditions, process)
5. Create product feed (Google Sheets for small catalogs, API/tool for large)
6. Submit feed and resolve any errors/warnings
7. Enable free listings program
8. Link Merchant Center to Google Ads account

### Shopping Campaign Setup
1. In Google Ads, create new Shopping campaign
2. Link the Merchant Center account
3. Select target country and campaign priority
4. Set daily budget and bidding strategy (start with Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks)
5. Create product groups (segment by brand, category, or custom labels)
6. Set individual bids for each product group based on margin and performance
7. Add negative keywords (Shopping campaigns do support negative keywords)
8. Launch and monitor product-level performance daily

### Dynamic Remarketing Setup
1. Add Google Ads remarketing tag to all pages via GTM
2. Configure tag to pass dynamic parameters (product ID, page type, total value)
3. Verify tag fires correctly using Google Tag Assistant
4. Create audience lists (all visitors, product viewers, cart abandoners, purchasers)
5. Create dynamic remarketing display campaign targeting cart abandoners first
6. Set frequency caps and membership durations
7. Launch and monitor audience-level performance

## Gap Finding

| Gap | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Thin product titles** | Low impressions despite product availability | Optimize titles with brand + product type + attributes |
| **Missing GTIN/MPN** | Product disapprovals or limited distribution | Add accurate GTIN (EAN/UPC/ISBN) for all branded products |
| **No custom labels** | Can't segment bids by margin or performance | Add custom labels for margin tiers and bestseller status |
| **Price mismatch errors** | Products disapproved | Ensure feed prices match landing page prices exactly (sync frequency) |
| **No product reviews** | Lower CTR than competitors | Integrate with Google-approved review partner |
| **No remarketing tag** | Can't retarget visitors or cart abandoners | Install remarketing tag via GTM with dynamic parameters |
| **Same bid for all products** | Budget wasted on low-margin products | Segment products and bid based on margin and conversion rate |
| **No negative keywords in Shopping** | Budget wasted on irrelevant searches | Add negatives from search term reports (Shopping campaigns support negatives) |
| **Missing supplemental feed** | Can't test title changes without affecting main feed | Create supplemental feed for title/description testing |

## Strategies

1. **Tiered Bidding by Margin** — Bid 3x higher on high-margin products, 1.5x on medium margin, 1x baseline on low margin. Custom labels make this possible.
2. **Query Sculpting with Campaign Priority** — High priority campaign (low bid, no negatives) catches broad queries. Low priority campaign (high bid, heavy negatives) captures high-intent, brand-specific queries.
3. **Supplemental Feed for Title Testing** — Use supplemental feeds to test different product titles without modifying the main feed. Compare impression and click changes.
4. **Seasonal Promotion Rotation** — Use Merchant Center promotions to display deals ("15% off with code SUMMER"). Rotate promotions every 2-4 weeks to keep ads fresh.
5. **Competitive Pricing Strategy** — Use pricing benchmark data (available in Merchant Center) to identify products where you're priced above market. Either reduce price or pause ads for uncompetitive products to save budget.
6. **Cart Abandonment Escalation** — Day 1: Reminder ad (no discount). Day 3: Social proof ad ("15,000 customers love this"). Day 5: Discount ad (10% off). Day 7: Urgency ad ("Last chance, stock running low").

## Best Practices

1. **Update feeds at least daily** — Price and availability changes must sync within 24 hours to avoid disapprovals
2. **Use high-quality product images** — White background, multiple angles, minimum 800×800 px
3. **Optimize titles for search intent** — Front-load brand and product type; include color, size, and material
4. **Segment products by performance** — Don't use one bid for all; group by margin, brand, and conversion rate
5. **Monitor the competitive landscape** — Check price benchmarks in Merchant Center; adjust pricing strategy
6. **Enable product ratings** — Products with star ratings get 17% higher CTR
7. **Use Merchant Center promotions** — Promotional badges increase CTR by 10-15%
8. **Check feed errors daily** — One pricing error can get your entire feed suspended
9. **Create separate campaigns for branded vs. non-branded queries** — Branded queries convert higher and deserve higher bids
10. **Don't ignore free listings** — They provide incremental traffic at zero cost; keep your feed healthy to qualify
