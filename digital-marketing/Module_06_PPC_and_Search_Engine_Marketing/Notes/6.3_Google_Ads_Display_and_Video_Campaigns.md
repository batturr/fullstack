## 6.3.1 Google Display Network (GDN)

### What is GDN and How It Works

The Google Display Network is a collection of over 2 million websites, apps, and videos where your ads can appear. GDN reaches 90% of internet users worldwide.

**Search vs. Display — The Fundamental Difference:**

```
SEARCH ADS (Pull Marketing):
User has intent → Searches actively → Sees your ad → Clicks
"I'm looking for a CRM" → Types query → Clicks your ad
👤➡️🔍➡️📋➡️🖱️

DISPLAY ADS (Push Marketing):
User is browsing → Your ad interrupts → Creates awareness → Clicks (maybe later)
User reads a tech blog → Sees your CRM banner → Remembers the brand → Searches later
👤➡️📰➡️🎨➡️🧠
```

**GDN covers:**
- News sites (Times of India, NDTV, Economic Times)
- Blogs and content sites (Medium, WordPress blogs)
- Apps (mobile game apps, utility apps)
- Gmail (Promotions and Social tabs)
- YouTube (sidebar, in-video overlays)

**Real-Time Example — Nykaa Display Ads:**
A user browses Vogue India reading about summer skincare. Nykaa's display ad for "Summer Sunscreen Sale — Up to 40% Off" appears in the sidebar. The user wasn't searching for sunscreen, but the contextual relevance (skincare article) makes the ad relevant. Later that day, the user searches "Nykaa sunscreen" on Google — the display ad created awareness that drove a search conversion.

### Display Campaign Types

| Type | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Display** | Full manual control over targeting, bidding, creatives | Experienced advertisers wanting control |
| **Smart Display** | Automated targeting, bidding, and creative optimization | Beginners or advertisers wanting automation |
| **Demand Gen** | Runs across YouTube, Gmail, and Discover with visual-first creatives | Mid-funnel engagement and consideration |

### Targeting Options (Deep Dive)

```
GDN TARGETING LAYERS — Stack These for Precision

Layer 1: CONTEXTUAL (Where the ad shows)
├── Keywords: Ad shows on pages containing specific words
│   Example: Keywords "running shoes" → Ad on running blog articles
├── Topics: Broad category targeting
│   Example: Topic "Health & Fitness" → Ad across fitness sites
└── Placements: Hand-picked specific websites
    Example: Placement "timesofindia.com" → Ad only on TOI

Layer 2: AUDIENCE (Who sees the ad)
├── Affinity: Broad interest groups
│   Example: "Fitness Enthusiasts" — people who regularly visit gym/health sites
├── In-Market: People actively researching/ready to buy
│   Example: "In-market for Running Shoes" — recently searching/comparing
├── Custom Segments: Your own combined signals
│   Example: People who search "marathon training" AND visit Nike.com
├── Demographics: Age, gender, income, parental status
│   Example: Age 25-34, Male, Top 30% household income
└── Remarketing: People who already visited your site
    Example: Cart abandoners from the last 30 days

Layer 3: CONTENT EXCLUSIONS (Where NOT to show)
├── Sensitive categories: Gambling, politics, tragedy
├── Specific placements: Competitor sites, low-quality apps
└── Content types: Games, parked domains, below-the-fold
```

**Real-Time Example — Layered Targeting for a Luxury Watch Brand (Titan Raga):**
- **Audience:** Women, 25-45, Top 20% household income, Affinity: "Luxury Shoppers"
- **Context:** Topics: "Fashion & Style," "Jewelry & Watches"
- **Placements:** Vogue India, Elle India, Femina
- **Exclusion:** Games, kids' content, political content
- This combination ensures ads show only to affluent women reading fashion content on premium sites.

### Display Remarketing Strategies

```
REMARKETING FUNNEL — Show the RIGHT ad to the RIGHT visitor

All Visitors (30 days)
│
├── Homepage Visitors (didn't explore)
│   → Show: Brand awareness ad with value proposition
│   → Frequency: 3-5x per week
│   → Duration: 30 days
│
├── Product Page Viewers (explored but didn't add to cart)
│   → Show: The exact product they viewed + reviews
│   → Frequency: 5-7x per week
│   → Duration: 14 days
│
├── Cart Abandoners (added to cart but didn't buy)
│   → Show: "You left something behind" + 10% discount
│   → Frequency: 7-10x per week (high urgency)
│   → Duration: 7 days
│
├── Converters (already purchased)
│   → Show: Cross-sell/upsell products
│   → Frequency: 2-3x per week
│   → Duration: 60-90 days
│
└── High-Value Customers (repeat purchasers)
    → Show: Loyalty program, exclusive offers
    → Frequency: 2x per week
    → Duration: 180 days
```

**Real-Time Example — Amazon Remarketing:**
You search for a laptop bag on Amazon, view 3 options but don't buy. For the next 14 days, every website you visit shows you those exact laptop bags — sometimes with a "Price dropped!" message. If you still don't buy, they expand to show "customers also bought" items. This is dynamic remarketing at scale.

### Frequency Capping and Ad Fatigue Management

**Frequency capping** limits how many times one person sees your ad.

| Campaign Goal | Recommended Frequency Cap |
|---|---|
| Brand Awareness | 3-5 impressions per user per week |
| Remarketing (general) | 5-7 impressions per user per week |
| Remarketing (cart abandoners) | 7-15 impressions per user per week |
| Prospecting (new audiences) | 3-4 impressions per user per week |

**Signs of Ad Fatigue:**
- CTR declining week-over-week for the same audience
- Frequency exceeding 10+ without conversion improvement
- User complaints or negative brand sentiment

**Fix:** Rotate creatives every 2-4 weeks. Maintain 3-5 creative variations per ad group.

### Brand Safety Controls

| Control | What It Does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Content exclusions** | Block ads from appearing next to sensitive content | Exclude: tragedy, conflict, explicit content |
| **Placement exclusions** | Block specific sites or apps | Exclude: adsenseformobileapps.com (low-quality apps) |
| **Topic exclusions** | Block entire content categories | Exclude: gambling, politics, mature content |
| **Digital content labels** | Filter by content maturity rating | Show only on DL-G (General) and DL-PG content |
| **Third-party verification** | DoubleVerify, IAS for viewability and brand safety | Ensure 70%+ viewability, brand-safe placements |

### Video Ad Creative Best Practices

**The ABCD Framework (Google's recommendation):**

| Element | What | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Attract** | Hook attention in first 5 seconds | Start with a bold question, surprising visual, or motion |
| **B — Brand** | Show your brand early and often | Logo in first 5 seconds, product by second 10 |
| **C — Connect** | Create emotional connection | Tell a story, use humor, show relatable scenarios |
| **D — Direct** | Clear call-to-action | "Download Now," "Visit our site," "Subscribe today" |

**The First 5 Seconds Rule:**
- 65% of YouTube ad value is delivered in the first 5 seconds
- Lead with the most compelling message (don't save the punchline)
- Show the brand/product within 5 seconds
- Use movement, faces, or text hooks to retain attention

**Real-Time Example — Cred's YouTube Ads:**
Cred's viral ads featuring Rahul Dravid ("Indiranagar ka Gunda"), Neeraj Chopra, and others:
- **Attract:** Unexpected celebrity in unusual behavior (first 3 seconds)
- **Brand:** "Cred" logo and app name displayed early
- **Connect:** Humor — audiences can't stop watching
- **Direct:** "Pay credit card bills on Cred" — simple CTA at the end
- Result: 25M+ views per ad, massive brand awareness, high completion rates

### YouTube Campaign Optimization

**Key Metrics to Monitor:**

| Metric | Target | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **View Rate** | >15% for skippable | % of viewers who watched vs. skipped |
| **Average % Viewed** | >50% | How much of the video users watched |
| **CPV** | Below industry average | Cost efficiency per view |
| **Earned Actions** | High ratio | Free likes, shares, subscribes driven by the ad |
| **View-through conversions** | Track these! | Users who saw the ad and converted later (didn't click) |

## 6.3.3 Performance Max Campaigns

### What is Performance Max and How It Works

Performance Max (PMax) is Google's AI-driven campaign type that runs ads across ALL Google properties from a single campaign.

```
PERFORMANCE MAX — ONE CAMPAIGN, ALL CHANNELS

                    ┌──────────────────┐
                    │  PERFORMANCE MAX │
                    │    CAMPAIGN      │
                    └────────┬─────────┘
                             │
        ┌────────┬───────────┼───────────┬────────┬──────────┐
        │        │           │           │        │          │
     Search   Display    YouTube     Gmail    Discover    Maps
      Ads      Ads        Ads        Ads       Ads        Ads
        │        │           │           │        │          │
        └────────┴───────────┴───────────┴────────┴──────────┘
                             │
                    Google's AI decides:
                    - Which channel
                    - Which audience
                    - Which creative combo
                    - What bid
                    - What time
```

### Asset Groups

Think of asset groups like ad groups but with rich multimedia:

| Asset Type | Specifications | Quantity |
|---|---|---|
| **Text — Headlines** | 30 chars | Up to 15 |
| **Text — Long headlines** | 90 chars | Up to 5 |
| **Text — Descriptions** | 90 chars | Up to 5 |
| **Images — Landscape** | 1200×628 | Up to 20 |
| **Images — Square** | 1200×1200 | Up to 20 |
| **Images — Portrait** | 960×1200 | Up to 20 |
| **Logos** | 1200×1200 | Up to 5 |
| **Videos** | YouTube hosted, 10+ sec | Up to 5 |
| **Call-to-action** | Auto or selected | 1 |
| **Final URL** | Landing page | 1 |

### Audience Signals

Audience signals are suggestions (not hard targeting) that help Google's AI find the right audience faster.

**Types of audience signals:**
1. **Custom segments** — Keywords people search, URLs they visit, apps they use
2. **Your data (first-party)** — Website visitors, customer lists, app users
3. **Interests & demographics** — In-market, affinity, life events, demographics

**Real-Time Example — PMax for a D2C Shoe Brand (Wrogn):**
- **Audience signal — Custom segment:** People searching "trendy men's shoes," "casual shoes for men online"
- **Audience signal — Your data:** Past purchasers, cart abandoners, app users
- **Audience signal — In-market:** In-market for men's footwear
- Google uses these signals as starting points, then finds similar audiences it predicts will convert

### When to Use PMax vs. Traditional Campaigns

| Use Performance Max When | Use Traditional Campaigns When |
|---|---|---|
| You have limited time/resources for multi-channel management | You need full control over keywords and placements |
| E-commerce with product feeds (Shopping) | B2B with specific keyword targeting needs |
| You want automated multi-channel reach | You need granular search term visibility |
| You have strong conversion data (50+ conversions/month) | You have limited conversion data |
| You want to reach new audiences across Google | You want to test specific channels in isolation |

```
Performance Max + Merchant Center = Automated Shopping

Product Feed ──► Google Merchant Center ──► Performance Max Campaign
                                               │
                                    ┌──────────┴──────────┐
                                    │                     │
                               Shopping Ads          Dynamic Ads
                               (Search/Shopping)     (Display/YouTube)
                                    │                     │
                                    └──────────┬──────────┘
                                               │
                                    Product shown across

```

**Real-Time Example — Pepperfry PMax:**
Pepperfry uploads 10,000+ furniture products to Merchant Center. PMax automatically:
- Shows sofa images in Google Shopping when someone searches "L-shaped sofa"
- Displays retargeting ads on GDN for users who viewed a specific sofa
- Runs YouTube ads featuring their bestselling dining tables
- Places ads in Gmail for users in-market for furniture

## 6.3.4 Demand Gen Campaigns

### What Are Demand Gen Campaigns

Demand Gen campaigns (replacing Discovery Ads) are designed for the middle of the funnel — creating interest and consideration before users are ready to search.

**Placements:** YouTube (Home, Watch Next, In-stream, Shorts), Gmail (Promotions, Social), Google Discover feed

### Creative Requirements

| Format | Specs |
|---|---|
| **Single Image** | 1200×628 (landscape), 1200×1200 (square), 960×1200 (portrait) |
| **Image Carousel** | 2-10 images, swipeable, each with its own headline and URL |
| **Video** | Horizontal, vertical, or square; 10 sec minimum |
| **Headlines** | Up to 5 (40 chars each) |
| **Descriptions** | Up to 5 (90 chars each) |

### Lookalike Segments

Demand Gen campaigns allow "lookalike segments" — finding new users who behave similarly to your existing customers.

```
YOUR SEED AUDIENCE              LOOKALIKE EXPANSION
(Past purchasers)               
├── Age 25-34                   ├── Similar age demographics
├── Interest: Tech              ├── Similar interest signals
├── Behavior: High spender      ├── Similar spending behavior
├── Location: Tier 1 cities     ├── Similar geographic profile
│                               │
└── 10,000 known customers      └── 500,000+ new prospects
                                    who look like your best customers
```

**Lookalike Expansion Levels:**
- **Narrow:** Most similar to seed (smallest reach, highest quality)
- **Balanced:** Moderate expansion (recommended starting point)
- **Broad:** Widest reach (highest volume, lower precision)

**Real-Time Example — BoAt Demand Gen Campaign:**
BoAt uploads their customer list of 50,000 buyers to Google. They create a Demand Gen campaign:
- **YouTube Home:** Video ad showing new BoAt earbuds with Hardik Pandya
- **Gmail Promotions:** Image carousel of 5 bestselling products
- **Discover Feed:** "BoAt Airdopes 141 — Now at Rs. 999" card appears while users scroll news
- Targeting: Lookalike of their customer list + In-market for "Wireless Earbuds"
- Result: 3x higher engagement rate than standard display campaigns

## Implementation Steps

### Display Campaign Setup
1. Define campaign goal (awareness, consideration, or remarketing)
2. Set targeting layers (audience + context + exclusions)
3. Create responsive display ad with 5+ images, 5 headlines, 5 descriptions
4. Set frequency cap (start at 5 impressions/user/week)
5. Add placement exclusions (low-quality apps, competitor sites)
6. Launch with CPM or Target CPA bidding

### YouTube Campaign Setup
1. Upload video to YouTube (unlisted or public)
2. Create Video campaign in Google Ads
3. Select ad format (skippable, non-skippable, bumper)
4. Set audience targeting (demographics + interests + placements)
5. Set bid strategy (CPV for views, CPM for awareness)
6. Create companion banner (300×60 for desktop)
7. Launch and monitor view rate, completion rate, earned actions

### Performance Max Setup
1. Connect Merchant Center (for e-commerce)
2. Create asset groups with all creative formats
3. Add audience signals (customer lists, custom segments, in-market)
4. Set conversion goals and target CPA/ROAS
5. Provide at least 1 video (Google auto-generates if missing but quality is poor)
6. Set budget (minimum Rs. 500/day recommended for learning)
7. Allow 2-4 weeks for machine learning to optimize

## Gap Finding

| Gap | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **No remarketing set up** | Missing retargeting of warm audiences | Install Google Ads remarketing tag, build audience lists |
| **Only one creative per ad group** | Ad fatigue, declining CTR | Add 3-5 creative variations per ad group |
| **No frequency cap** | Users annoyed, brand perception drops | Set caps at 5-7 impressions/user/week |
| **Poor video hook** | View rate below 10%, high skip rate | Restructure first 5 seconds with stronger hook |
| **PMax with no video** | Google auto-generates low-quality video | Upload proper video assets |
| **No audience signals in PMax** | Slow learning, poor initial performance | Add customer lists and custom segments as signals |
| **No placement exclusions** | Budget wasted on mobile game apps | Exclude adsenseformobileapps.com and low-quality placements |
| **Missing brand safety controls** | Ads appearing on inappropriate content | Enable content exclusions and use verification tools |

## Strategies

1. **Sequential Storytelling on YouTube** — Show Ad 1 (15 sec brand intro) to cold audience. Show Ad 2 (30 sec product demo) to viewers of Ad 1. Show Ad 3 (15 sec CTA + offer) to viewers of Ad 2. Each step qualifies the audience further.
2. **Remarketing Ladder** — Different creatives for 0-3 day visitors (soft reminder), 3-7 day visitors (testimonial/social proof), 7-14 day visitors (discount offer), 14-30 day visitors (urgency/last chance).
3. **PMax + Dedicated Search** — Run PMax for broad automation AND keep separate Search campaigns for your top 20% keywords. PMax handles the long tail; Search handles the high-value terms.
4. **YouTube + Search Retargeting** — Run awareness video ads on YouTube, then retarget video viewers with Search ads when they search related keywords (RLSA for video viewers).
5. **Context + Audience Stacking** — Don't just target fitness enthusiasts on any site; target fitness enthusiasts reading fitness articles. The intersection delivers higher conversion rates.

## Best Practices

1. **Start display with remarketing** — Remarketing has the highest ROI among display strategies; launch it first before prospecting
2. **Never run PMax without video** — Auto-generated videos underperform; invest in at least one proper video
3. **Use high-quality images** — Display ads with lifestyle imagery outperform product-only images by 30-50%
4. **Monitor placement reports** — Review where ads appear weekly; exclude poor-performing placements
5. **Set conversion minimums** — PMax and Smart Display need 30+ conversions per month to optimize effectively
6. **Separate brand and non-brand in PMax** — Use URL expansion settings and brand exclusions to control where PMax appears
7. **Test vertical video for Shorts/Discover** — Vertical format gets 10-20% better engagement on mobile
8. **Layer targeting incrementally** — Start with one audience type, measure, then add layers; don't over-restrict from day one
