## 6.7.1 Key PPC Metrics

### The Complete PPC Metrics Framework

Understanding metrics is the difference between spending money and investing money. Every metric tells a story — your job is to read it correctly.

```
PPC METRICS FUNNEL

VISIBILITY METRICS (Are people seeing your ads?)
│  Impressions → Impression Share → Lost IS (Budget) → Lost IS (Rank)
│
▼
ENGAGEMENT METRICS (Are people clicking your ads?)
│  Clicks → CTR → Average Position → Quality Score
│
▼
COST METRICS (How much are you spending?)
│  Cost → CPC → CPM → Cost per View
│
▼
CONVERSION METRICS (Are clicks turning into results?)
│  Conversions → Conversion Rate → CPA → Conversion Value
│
▼
ROI METRICS (Are you making money?)
│  ROAS → ROI → Profit → Lifetime Value
```

### Metric-by-Metric Deep Dive

**1. Impressions, Clicks, and CTR**

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range | What It Tells You |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Impressions** | Times your ad was shown | Varies by budget | Reach and visibility |
| **Clicks** | Times your ad was clicked | Varies by budget | Interest in your ad |
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | Clicks ÷ Impressions × 100 | Search: 3-8%, Display: 0.5-1.5% | Ad relevance and appeal |

**CTR Benchmarks by Industry:**

| Industry | Average Search CTR | Average Display CTR |
|---|---|---|
| E-commerce | 2.5-4.0% | 0.5-0.8% |
| Technology/SaaS | 2.0-3.5% | 0.3-0.6% |
| Finance/Insurance | 2.5-4.5% | 0.3-0.5% |
| Healthcare | 3.0-5.0% | 0.4-0.6% |
| Real Estate | 2.5-4.5% | 0.3-0.6% |
| Education | 3.5-5.5% | 0.4-0.7% |
| Travel | 3.0-5.0% | 0.3-0.5% |
| Local Services | 4.0-7.0% | 0.6-1.0% |

**2. CPC (Cost Per Click) and CPM (Cost Per Thousand)**

```
CPC = Total Cost ÷ Total Clicks

Example: Rs. 10,000 spent, 500 clicks
CPC = Rs. 10,000 ÷ 500 = Rs. 20 per click

CPM = Total Cost ÷ (Impressions ÷ 1,000)

Example: Rs. 5,000 spent, 100,000 impressions
CPM = Rs. 5,000 ÷ 100 = Rs. 50 per 1,000 impressions
```

**3. Conversion Rate and CPA**

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Conversion Rate** | Conversions ÷ Clicks × 100 | E-commerce: 2-4%, Lead gen: 5-15%, SaaS: 3-7% |
| **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)** | Total Cost ÷ Conversions | Industry and business specific |

**Real-Time Example — CPA Analysis for Byju's:**

```
Campaign: "Online Learning Platform"
Spend: Rs. 15,00,000/month
Clicks: 75,000
CPC: Rs. 20
Leads (form fills): 3,750 (5% conversion rate)
CPA (per lead): Rs. 400
Sales-qualified leads: 750 (20% of leads)
Cost per SQL: Rs. 2,000
Enrolled students: 150 (20% of SQLs)
Cost per enrollment: Rs. 10,000
Average student LTV: Rs. 50,000

ROI: (Rs. 75,00,000 revenue - Rs. 15,00,000 ad spend) ÷ Rs. 15,00,000 = 400% ROI

Even though CPA per lead (Rs. 400) seems low, the real metric is
cost per enrollment (Rs. 10,000) vs. LTV (Rs. 50,000) = 5:1 return.
```

**4. ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) and ROI**

```
ROAS = Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend

Example: Rs. 5,00,000 revenue from Rs. 1,00,000 ad spend
ROAS = Rs. 5,00,000 ÷ Rs. 1,00,000 = 5.0x (or 500%)

Interpretation:
Every Rs. 1 spent on ads generates Rs. 5 in revenue.

ROAS Targets by Business Type:
├── E-commerce (low margin): 4x-6x minimum
├── E-commerce (high margin): 2x-3x acceptable
├── SaaS (high LTV): 1x-2x acceptable (LTV makes up for it)
├── Lead gen: Track CPA instead of ROAS
└── Brand awareness: Don't measure ROAS (use reach/impressions)

ROI = (Revenue - Total Cost) ÷ Total Cost × 100

Total Cost includes: ad spend + product cost + overhead
More accurate than ROAS but harder to calculate.
```

**5. Impression Share Metrics**

```
IMPRESSION SHARE BREAKDOWN

Total Eligible Impressions (100%)
│
├── Impression Share (60%) ← Your ads appeared this often
│
├── Lost IS (Budget) (25%) ← Ads didn't show because budget ran out
│   FIX: Increase daily budget or reduce wasted spend
│
└── Lost IS (Rank) (15%) ← Ads didn't show because Ad Rank was too low
    FIX: Improve Quality Score or increase bids

If Lost IS (Budget) > 20%: You're leaving money on the table
If Lost IS (Rank) > 30%: Your Quality Score or bids are too low
If Impression Share > 90%: You're dominating (but check if spend is efficient)
```

**Real-Time Example — Impression Share Diagnosis:**

| Metric | Campaign A (Brand) | Campaign B (Non-Brand) | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Impression Share | 95% | 35% | Brand is dominant; non-brand has room to grow |
| Lost IS (Budget) | 2% | 40% | Non-brand budget limited; increase or reallocate |
| Lost IS (Rank) | 3% | 25% | Non-brand QS/bids too low; improve both |
| Abs. Top IS | 82% | 12% | Non-brand rarely shows in top position |

Action: Shift 20% of brand budget to non-brand (brand has excess share). Improve non-brand QS to reduce rank loss.

### Custom Columns and Segments

**Useful Custom Columns to Create in Google Ads:**

| Custom Column | Formula | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit per click** | (Conv. value / clicks) - CPC | True profitability per click |
| **Revenue per impression** | Conv. value / impressions | Revenue efficiency of visibility |
| **Conversion gap** | Clicks × (industry avg conv rate - your conv rate) | Potential conversions you're missing |
| **Wasted spend** | Cost of clicks with 0 conversions | Budget leakage identification |
| **Cost per SQL** | Cost / (conversions × SQL rate) | True cost of qualified leads |

**Useful Segments to Apply:**

| Segment | What It Reveals |
|---|---|
| **Device** | Mobile vs. desktop vs. tablet performance |
| **Day of Week** | Which days convert best |
| **Hour of Day** | Peak and off-peak hours |
| **Network** | Search vs. Search Partners vs. Display |
| **Top vs. Other** | Ads at top of page vs. bottom/other positions |
| **Click Type** | Sitelinks, headline, phone call — which generates conversions |
| **Conversion Action** | Breakdown by conversion type (call vs. form vs. purchase) |

### Client Reporting Frameworks

**The 3-Level Reporting Approach:**

```
LEVEL 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (C-Suite / Business Owner)
├── Format: 1-page visual dashboard or 5-minute video
├── Metrics: Revenue, ROAS, CPA, total conversions
├── Focus: Business impact, ROI, trends
├── Frequency: Monthly
├── Language: Business language, not PPC jargon
└── Example: "We spent Rs. 8.5L and generated Rs. 42.5L in revenue.
             That's Rs. 5 back for every Rs. 1 invested."

LEVEL 2: TACTICAL REPORT (Marketing Manager)
├── Format: 5-10 page Looker Studio dashboard
├── Metrics: Campaign-level performance, keyword analysis, A/B test results
├── Focus: What's working, what's not, what we changed, what we'll do next
├── Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
└── Example: "Shopping campaigns ROAS improved 12% after title optimization.
             Brand campaign CPA increased 8% — competitor bidding suspected."

LEVEL 3: DETAILED ANALYSIS (PPC Specialist / Internal Review)
├── Format: Spreadsheet with raw data + pivot tables
├── Metrics: Keyword-level, ad-level, audience-level, search terms
├── Focus: Granular optimization opportunities, wasted spend, QS tracking
├── Frequency: Weekly
└── Example: Keyword-by-keyword analysis with QS trends, match type
             performance, and negative keyword recommendations.
```

## 6.7.3 PPC Audit Framework

### The Comprehensive PPC Audit

A PPC audit is a systematic review of every aspect of an account to identify waste, missed opportunities, and improvement areas.

**When to Audit:**
- Before taking over a new account
- Quarterly for existing accounts
- After any significant performance change (positive or negative)
- Before scaling budgets
- When switching agencies or in-house management

### 15-Point PPC Audit Checklist

```
PPC AUDIT CHECKLIST — 15 CRITICAL AREAS

FOUNDATION (Account Health)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 1. CONVERSION TRACKING
   ├── Are all conversion types tracked? (forms, calls, purchases, chats)
   ├── Is tracking firing correctly? (verify with Tag Assistant)
   ├── Are conversion values assigned accurately?
   ├── Is enhanced conversions enabled?
   └── Are offline conversions imported? (for lead-gen businesses)
   
   RED FLAG: No conversion tracking = the entire account is flying blind

☐ 2. ACCOUNT STRUCTURE
   ├── Logical campaign organization (brand, non-brand, competitor, product)
   ├── Ad groups tightly themed (5-20 related keywords per group)
   ├── Consistent naming conventions
   ├── Labels applied for easy filtering
   └── No duplicate keywords across campaigns
   
   RED FLAG: 200+ keywords in a single ad group

☐ 3. BUDGET ALLOCATION
   ├── Budget aligned with business priorities
   ├── Top-performing campaigns adequately funded
   ├── Impression share metrics (is budget limiting performance?)
   ├── No budget locked on underperforming campaigns
   └── Budget pacing on track (not overspending early in month)
   
   RED FLAG: High IS (budget) loss on best-performing campaigns

KEYWORDS (Traffic Quality)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 4. KEYWORD MATCH TYPES
   ├── Match type distribution (% exact, phrase, broad)
   ├── Broad match used responsibly (with Smart Bidding or heavy negatives)
   ├── Keyword intent alignment (informational vs. transactional)
   └── Close variant impact assessment
   
   RED FLAG: 80%+ broad match with manual bidding

☐ 5. NEGATIVE KEYWORDS
   ├── Account-level negative list exists
   ├── Campaign-level negatives in place
   ├── Ad group-level negatives preventing cannibalization
   ├── Regular search term report mining
   └── No keyword-negative conflicts (keyword blocked by own negative)
   
   RED FLAG: No negative keywords at all (extremely common and wasteful)

☐ 6. SEARCH TERMS ANALYSIS
   ├── Review last 90 days of search terms
   ├── Identify irrelevant terms wasting budget
   ├── Find high-converting terms not yet added as keywords
   ├── Analyze match type effectiveness
   └── Calculate % of spend going to irrelevant queries
   
   RED FLAG: >20% of spend going to clearly irrelevant search terms

ADS (Message Quality)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 7. AD COPY PERFORMANCE
   ├── Minimum 2 RSAs per ad group
   ├── All RSAs using 10+ headlines and 3+ descriptions
   ├── Ad strength indicator (Good or Excellent target)
   ├── Regular ad copy testing
   ├── Pinning used strategically (not excessively)
   └── CTR trends stable or improving
   
   RED FLAG: 1 ad per ad group with "Poor" ad strength

☐ 8. AD EXTENSIONS / ASSETS
   ├── Sitelinks: minimum 4, with descriptions
   ├── Callouts: 4-8 highlighting key benefits
   ├── Structured snippets: relevant header and values
   ├── Call extensions: phone number with hours
   ├── Image extensions: high-quality, relevant images
   ├── Other: price, promotion, lead form, location
   └── Automated extensions reviewed (disable irrelevant ones)
   
   RED FLAG: No extensions at all (missing 10-15% CTR improvement)

LANDING PAGES (Post-Click Experience)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 9. LANDING PAGE ALIGNMENT
   ├── Message match: ad promise = landing page delivery
   ├── Dedicated PPC pages vs. generic website pages
   ├── Mobile responsiveness and usability
   ├── Page load speed (< 3 seconds)
   ├── Clear CTA above the fold
   └── Trust signals present (reviews, badges, testimonials)
   
   RED FLAG: All ads pointing to the homepage

☐ 10. LANDING PAGE TESTING
    ├── A/B tests running on high-traffic pages
    ├── Conversion rate compared to industry benchmarks
    ├── Form field optimization (fewer fields = higher conversion)
    └── Heatmap/session recording analysis (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity)
    
    RED FLAG: No landing page testing in the last 6 months

BIDDING & TARGETING (Optimization Quality)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 11. BIDDING STRATEGY
    ├── Strategy appropriate for conversion volume
    ├── Smart Bidding with sufficient data (30+ conv/month)
    ├── Targets realistic (CPA/ROAS achievable based on history)
    ├── Learning period respected (2-4 weeks after change)
    ├── Bid adjustments for device, location, time, audience
    └── Portfolio strategies used where appropriate
    
    RED FLAG: Target CPA set at 50% below actual CPA (will limit traffic)

☐ 12. AUDIENCE TARGETING
    ├── RLSA set up for remarketing audiences
    ├── Customer Match lists uploaded and active
    ├── In-market and affinity audiences in Observation mode
    ├── Audience performance analyzed and bid adjustments applied
    ├── Combined segments for precision targeting
    └── Exclusion lists for current customers (if appropriate)
    
    RED FLAG: No audience targeting or remarketing of any kind

☐ 13. QUALITY SCORE
    ├── Average QS across all keywords (target 7+)
    ├── QS trend over time (improving, stable, declining)
    ├── Keywords with QS 1-4 identified with improvement plan
    ├── QS component analysis (CTR, relevance, landing page)
    └── QS correlation with CPC trends
    
    RED FLAG: Average QS below 5, trending downward

ANALYTICS & REPORTING (Measurement Quality)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

☐ 14. ANALYTICS INTEGRATION
    ├── Google Ads linked to GA4
    ├── GA4 audiences shared with Google Ads
    ├── Cross-platform tracking consistent
    ├── UTM parameters on all ad URLs
    ├── Attribution model appropriate for business
    └── Conversion lag accounted for in reporting
    
    RED FLAG: Google Ads and GA4 show different conversion numbers

☐ 15. WASTED SPEND IDENTIFICATION
    ├── Keywords with spend but zero conversions (last 90 days)
    ├── Ads with spend but zero conversions
    ├── Devices, locations, or times with negative ROI
    ├── Audience segments with negative ROI
    ├── Search terms clearly irrelevant
    └── Total wasted spend as % of total spend (target < 15%)
    
    RED FLAG: >25% of total spend going to zero-conversion elements
```

### PPC Audit Scoring Framework

**Score each area on a 1-5 scale:**

| Score | Rating | Description |
|---|---|---|

| 4 | Good | Well set up with minor improvements possible |
| 3 | Average | Functional but significant optimization opportunities |
| 2 | Poor | Major issues impacting performance |

**Real-Time Example — Audit Finding for a Travel Agency:**

During a PPC audit, the agency discovered:
- **Wasted spend:** Rs. 3.2 lakh/month on keywords like "travel agent jobs," "travel agent salary," "free travel" — none of which generate bookings
- **Missing negative keywords:** The word "free" alone was responsible for Rs. 45,000/month in wasted clicks
- **No remarketing:** 80% of website visitors who checked flight prices left without booking, and the agency had no remarketing campaign
- **Fix impact:** Adding negatives saved Rs. 3.2L/month. Launching remarketing generated 120 additional bookings/month at Rs. 350 CPA
- **Net effect:** Monthly profit increased by Rs. 8.5 lakh

## Implementation Steps

### PPC Reporting Setup
1. Link Google Ads to Looker Studio
2. Create executive dashboard (page 1 — key metrics)
3. Create campaign detail page (page 2 — campaign performance table)
4. Create keyword analysis page (page 3 — keyword-level data)
5. Add date range controls and campaign filters
6. Schedule automated email delivery (weekly or monthly)
7. Set up automated alerts in Google Ads for budget/CPA/CTR thresholds

### PPC Audit Process
1. Export all campaigns, ad groups, keywords, and ads to spreadsheet
2. Run through the 15-point checklist systematically
3. Score each area (1-5)
4. Identify the top 3 priority issues by impact
5. Create an action plan with estimated impact and timeline
6. Present findings to stakeholders with clear recommendations
7. Implement fixes in priority order over 2-4 weeks
8. Re-audit in 90 days to measure improvement

### Wasted Spend Audit Process
1. Export search terms report (last 90 days)
2. Filter by cost descending, conversions = 0
3. Categorize irrelevant terms into negative keyword themes
4. Calculate total wasted spend
5. Export keywords with zero conversions and 100+ clicks
6. Evaluate: pause, reduce bid, or restructure?
7. Calculate potential savings and present to stakeholders

## Gap Finding

| Gap | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **No dashboards** | Reporting done manually in spreadsheets every month | Set up Looker Studio dashboard with auto-refresh |
| **No regular audits** | Performance declines unnoticed for months | Schedule quarterly PPC audits (use the 15-point checklist) |
| **Vanity metric focus** | Reports highlight impressions/clicks, not conversions/revenue | Restructure reporting around business outcomes (CPA, ROAS, revenue) |
| **No competitive monitoring** | Unaware of competitor bidding changes | Check Auction Insights monthly; set up SpyFu alerts |
| **No wasted spend tracking** | Budget leaking to irrelevant queries and non-converting keywords | Weekly search term analysis + monthly zero-conversion keyword review |
| **Reporting lag** | Reports delivered 2+ weeks after month-end | Set up Looker Studio with auto-refresh (data updates daily) |
| **No benchmarking** | No context for whether performance is good or bad | Compare against industry benchmarks and own historical trends |
| **Single attribution model** | Over-crediting last-click, under-valuing awareness keywords | Switch to data-driven attribution (or compare multiple models) |

## Strategies

1. **The Weekly 30-Minute Audit** — Every Monday, spend 30 minutes: review search terms (10 min), check QS changes (5 min), review ad performance (5 min), check budget pacing (5 min), review competitive insights (5 min). Document findings.

2. **The 80/20 Focus** — 80% of your conversions come from 20% of keywords/campaigns. Identify your top 20% and protect them. Monitor their QS, impression share, and competitive landscape weekly.

3. **Narrative Reporting** — Don't just show numbers; tell a story. Instead of "CPA increased 15%," say "CPA increased 15% because competitor X entered the auction (visible in Auction Insights), increasing CPCs by 20%. Our response: improved QS on 15 keywords, reducing the CPA impact to just 15% instead of the expected 20%."

4. **Rolling 13-Week Trend** — Always show 13-week (quarterly) trends in reports. This reveals patterns that monthly snapshots miss: seasonal trends, gradual QS erosion, slow competitor build-up.

5. **Anomaly Detection Alerts** — Set up automated alerts for: CPA >20% above target, CTR drops >25%, budget depleted before noon, impression share drops >15%, CPC spike >30%. React within 24 hours.

6. **Cohort Analysis** — Track leads generated in Month 1 through to conversion in Month 3-6. This reveals true ROI for long-sales-cycle businesses and prevents premature budget cuts on campaigns that generate quality leads.

## Best Practices

1. **Report on business outcomes, not PPC metrics** — Stakeholders care about revenue, profit, and qualified leads — not CTR or QS
2. **Automate repetitive reporting** — Use Looker Studio with scheduled email delivery; don't waste hours building manual reports
3. **Audit quarterly at minimum** — Even well-managed accounts drift; quarterly audits catch issues before they become expensive
4. **Keep a change log** — Document every change made to the account with date, rationale, and expected impact. This is invaluable for diagnosing performance shifts.
5. **Compare apples to apples** — When comparing periods, account for: seasonality, budget changes, new campaigns, competitor entry, and external events
6. **Use conversion windows** — A 30-day conversion window means today's click might not convert for 30 days. Don't make decisions based on the most recent 7 days (data is incomplete)
7. **Segment everything** — Never look at aggregate numbers without segmenting by device, location, time, audience, and campaign type
8. **Calculate true CPA** — Include all costs: ad spend + agency fees + creative production + landing page hosting + tool subscriptions ÷ conversions
9. **Set up automated alerts** — Don't wait for the weekly report to discover a problem; alerts catch issues in real-time
10. **Benchmark against yourself** — Industry benchmarks are useful but your own historical data is the most relevant comparison
