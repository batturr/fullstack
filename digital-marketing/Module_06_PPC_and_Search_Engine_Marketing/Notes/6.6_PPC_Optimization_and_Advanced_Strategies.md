## 6.6.1 Bid Management & Automation

### Manual vs. Automated Bidding — When to Use Each

```
BIDDING STRATEGY MATURITY PATH

New Account (0-30 conversions/month):
├── Start with: Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks
├── Why: Gathering data, understanding keyword performance
└── Duration: 4-8 weeks

Growing Account (30-50 conversions/month):
├── Switch to: Enhanced CPC or Maximize Conversions
├── Why: Enough data for basic automation
└── Duration: 4-8 weeks to stabilize

Mature Account (50+ conversions/month):
├── Use: Target CPA or Target ROAS
├── Why: Sufficient conversion data for Smart Bidding
└── Duration: Ongoing with periodic target adjustments

High-Volume Account (200+ conversions/month):
├── Use: Portfolio bid strategies across campaigns
├── Why: Cross-campaign optimization, budget fluidity
└── Duration: Ongoing with automated rules
```

### Smart Bidding Deep Dive

| Strategy | How Google's AI Decides | Best For | Setting Tip |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Target CPA** | Predicts likelihood of conversion for each auction; bids higher when conversion is likely, lower when it's not | Lead gen with stable CPA target | Set target CPA at your current CPA or 10-15% above it initially |
| **Target ROAS** | Predicts conversion value for each auction; bids to hit your target return | E-commerce with revenue tracking | Start with your average ROAS (e.g., 400% = Rs. 4 revenue per Rs. 1 spent) |
| **Maximize Conversions** | Uses full daily budget to get as many conversions as possible | When volume matters more than CPA | Set a reasonable daily budget cap; no CPA floor |
| **Maximize Conversion Value** | Uses full daily budget to get highest total conversion value | Revenue maximization | Assign accurate conversion values to each action |

**Real-Time Example — Smart Bidding for Lenskart:**

```
Scenario: Lenskart has 500+ conversions/month

Before (Manual CPC):
├── Average CPC: Rs. 18
├── Conversion Rate: 3.5%
├── CPA: Rs. 514
├── Monthly conversions: 480
└── Monthly spend: Rs. 2,47,000

After (Target CPA at Rs. 450):
├── Average CPC: Rs. 22 (higher for high-intent)
│   But also Rs. 8 (lower for low-intent)
├── Conversion Rate: 4.2% (better targeting)
├── CPA: Rs. 438 (below target)
├── Monthly conversions: 560 (+17%)
└── Monthly spend: Rs. 2,45,000 (similar budget)

Google's AI identifies:
- Which searches at which time by which user type are most likely to convert
- Bids high at 8 PM for a 28-year-old on mobile who previously visited the site
- Bids low at 3 AM for a first-time visitor on desktop browsing casually
```

### Portfolio Bid Strategies

Portfolio strategies apply a single bidding strategy across multiple campaigns, allowing Google to shift budget to the best-performing campaign in real time.

```
PORTFOLIO BID STRATEGY: Target CPA Rs. 800

Campaign A: "CRM Software"        → Getting conversions at Rs. 600 → Gets MORE budget
Campaign B: "Project Management"   → Getting conversions at Rs. 900 → Gets LESS budget
Campaign C: "Help Desk Software"   → Getting conversions at Rs. 750 → Gets MODERATE budget

Result: Portfolio CPA across all three = Rs. 780 (under target)
Instead of each campaign having its own rigid budget.
```

### Bid Adjustments

Bid adjustments let you increase or decrease bids based on specific dimensions:

| Dimension | Range | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Device** | -100% to +900% | Mobile: -20% (lower conversion rate on mobile) |
| **Location** | -90% to +900% | Bangalore: +25% (highest converting city) |
| **Time/Schedule** | -90% to +900% | 7 PM-10 PM: +30% (peak buying hours) |
| **Audience** | -90% to +900% | Cart abandoners: +50% (high intent) |
| **Demographics** | -90% to +900% | Age 25-34: +15% (target demographic) |

**Important:** With Smart Bidding (Target CPA, Target ROAS), device bid adjustments are overridden by Google's AI. Only manual and enhanced CPC respect your bid adjustments.

## 6.6.2 Quality Score Optimization

### Understanding Quality Score in Detail

Quality Score is the single most impactful lever in PPC — it determines what you pay and where you rank.

```
QUALITY SCORE ANATOMY

Quality Score: 7/10
├── Expected CTR: Above Average ✓
│   ├── Your ad's historical CTR for this keyword
│   ├── Normalized for ad position (removes positional bias)
│   └── Compared to other advertisers bidding on the same keyword
│
├── Ad Relevance: Average ⚠
│   ├── How well your ad copy matches the keyword's intent
│   ├── Does the keyword appear in headlines/descriptions?
│   └── Is the ad addressing what the searcher is looking for?
│
└── Landing Page Experience: Above Average ✓
    ├── Page load speed (Core Web Vitals)
    ├── Mobile-friendliness and responsiveness
    ├── Content relevance to the keyword and ad
    ├── Original, useful content (not thin/duplicated)
    ├── Easy navigation and clear next steps
    └── Trustworthiness (contact info, privacy policy, HTTPS)
```

### Quality Score Improvement Playbook

**Improving Expected CTR:**

| Tactic | Expected Impact | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Include keyword in Headline 1 | +15-25% CTR | Keyword "CRM software" → Headline: "Best CRM Software 2025" |
| Add numbers and statistics | +10-20% CTR | "Trusted by 50,000+ Companies" |
| Use strong CTAs | +10-15% CTR | "Get Free Demo" vs. "Learn More" |
| Include offers/pricing | +15-20% CTR | "Starting at Rs. 499/month" |
| Use all ad extensions | +10-15% CTR | Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets |
| Dynamic keyword insertion | +10-20% CTR | {KeyWord:Default Text} in headlines |

**Improving Ad Relevance:**

1. Tighten ad group themes — max 10-15 closely related keywords per ad group
2. Mirror keyword language in ad headlines and descriptions
3. Create multiple RSAs per ad group with varied headlines
4. Use ad group-level negative keywords to prevent internal cannibalization
5. Match ad copy intent to keyword intent (informational vs. transactional)

**Improving Landing Page Experience:**

```
LANDING PAGE QUALITY SCORE CHECKLIST

Speed:
☐ Page loads in < 3 seconds (test with Google PageSpeed Insights)
☐ Core Web Vitals pass: LCP < 2.5s, FID < 100ms, CLS < 0.1
☐ Images optimized (WebP format, compressed)
☐ Lazy loading for below-fold content

Mobile:
☐ Responsive design (works on all screen sizes)
☐ Thumb-friendly buttons (min 44px × 44px)
☐ No horizontal scrolling
☐ Readable text without zooming (16px+ font)

Content:
☐ Headline matches ad promise (message match)
☐ Keyword appears naturally in page content
☐ Unique, original content (not duplicated)
☐ Clear value proposition above the fold

Trust:
☐ HTTPS enabled
☐ Privacy policy linked
☐ Contact information visible
☐ Reviews/testimonials present
☐ Security badges (SSL, payment badges)

UX:
☐ Single, clear CTA above the fold
☐ Minimal form fields (name, email, phone — max 3-5 fields)
☐ No intrusive pop-ups that block content
☐ Easy back-navigation
```

**Real-Time Example — Impact of Improving QS from 5 to 8:**

|---|---|---|---|

**Improvement from QS 5→8 gave 61 additional conversions without spending a single extra rupee.**

## 6.6.3 Audience Strategies in Search

### RLSA (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads)

RLSA lets you customize search campaigns for people who have previously visited your website.

```
HOW RLSA WORKS

Normal Search Campaign:
User searches "CRM software" → Sees your ad → Standard bid

RLSA-Enhanced Search Campaign:
User visited your site last week → 
User searches "CRM software" → 
Google recognizes them → 
You bid +50% higher → 
Your ad appears in position #1 → 
Custom ad copy: "Welcome back! Start your free trial"

TWO MODES:

1. Targeting Mode (restrictive):
   Only show ads to people on your remarketing list
   Use case: Bid on broad/expensive keywords only for past visitors
   Example: Bid on "software" (too expensive for cold traffic)
           but only show to people who visited your CRM product page

2. Observation Mode (layered):
   Show ads to everyone, but bid differently for remarketing audience
   Use case: Bid +30% higher for past visitors on all existing keywords
   Example: Everyone sees your ad for "CRM software"
           but past visitors trigger a higher bid (+30%)
```

**Real-Time Example — UpGrad RLSA Strategy:**
- A user visits UpGrad's MBA program page but doesn't sign up
- 3 days later, they search "online MBA worth it" on Google
- UpGrad's RLSA campaign detects the past visitor and:
  - Bids +40% higher (ensuring position #1)
  - Shows custom ad: "Still considering? Chat with an advisor today — UpGrad MBA"
  - Sends to a page with a chat widget pre-loaded
- Result: 3x higher conversion rate vs. cold traffic on the same keyword

### Customer Match

Upload your customer email list, phone numbers, or physical addresses to Google Ads. Google matches these to Google accounts and lets you target/exclude them.

**Use Cases:**

| Strategy | Audience | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Upsell** | Current customers on lower-tier plans | Show ads for premium features |
| **Win-back** | Churned customers (cancelled in last 90 days) | Show "We miss you" offers |
| **Exclusion** | Current paying customers | Exclude from acquisition campaigns (don't waste budget) |
| **Lookalike** | Top 10% highest-spending customers | Create Similar Audience for prospecting |
| **Cross-sell** | Customers who bought Product A | Show ads for complementary Product B |

### In-Market vs. Affinity Audiences

```
AFFINITY AUDIENCES (Who they ARE):
├── Long-term interests and identity
├── Examples: "Fitness Enthusiasts," "Foodies," "Tech Savvy"
├── Best for: Brand awareness, top-of-funnel
└── Signal: Consistent behavior over weeks/months

IN-MARKET AUDIENCES (What they're DOING RIGHT NOW):
├── Active purchase research and comparison shopping
├── Examples: "In-Market for Running Shoes," "In-Market for CRM Software"
├── Best for: Consideration, bottom-of-funnel
└── Signal: Recent search and browsing behavior indicating purchase intent
```

### Combined Segments

Create complex audience combinations by stacking:

**Example — Premium Customer Segment:**
```
Combined Segment: "High-Value B2B Prospect"
├── AND: In-Market for "Business Software"
├── AND: Affinity "Technology Early Adopters"  
├── AND: Demographics: Age 25-44
├── NOT: Already visited our site (exclude existing traffic)
└── NOT: Current customers (Customer Match exclusion)

Result: Fresh prospects who are actively researching business software,
are tech-savvy, and in the right age range — but haven't interacted
with your brand yet. Premium acquisition target.
```

### Observation vs. Targeting Mode

| Mode | What It Does | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Observation** | Show ads to everyone; collect data on how the audience performs; adjust bids | Discovery phase — learn which audiences convert best |
| **Targeting** | ONLY show ads to the selected audience | Restriction phase — focus budget on proven audiences |

**Best Practice:** Start all audiences in Observation mode for 30 days. After identifying high-performing audiences, switch them to Targeting mode (or apply bid adjustments in Observation).

### Google Tag Manager for Conversion Tracking

**Why GTM instead of direct tag installation:**
- No developer needed for tag changes
- Version control (roll back if something breaks)
- Centralized tag management
- Easy debugging with Preview mode
- Can fire tags based on complex conditions (scroll depth, time on page, button clicks)

**GTM Setup for Google Ads Conversions:**

```
Step 1: Create GTM container and install on website
Step 2: Create Google Ads Conversion Linking tag (fires on all pages)
Step 3: Create conversion action in Google Ads → get Conversion ID and Label
Step 4: In GTM, create Google Ads Conversion Tracking tag
        ├── Conversion ID: AW-XXXXXXXXX
        └── Conversion Label: XXXXXXXXXXXXX
Step 5: Create trigger (when to fire):
        ├── Form submission: Trigger on form_submit event
        ├── Purchase: Trigger on purchase event with transaction value
        └── Page view: Trigger on thank-you page URL
Step 6: Test in GTM Preview mode
Step 7: Publish container
```

### Enhanced Conversions

Enhanced conversions improve conversion tracking accuracy by sending hashed first-party user data (email, phone, name) to Google for better cross-device and cross-browser matching.

**How it works:**
1. User fills out a form on your site (email: battu@example.com)
2. Enhanced conversions hashes the email (SHA-256) before sending to Google
3. Google matches the hash to signed-in Google accounts
4. Even if cookies are blocked or the user switches devices, the conversion is attributed

**Real-Time Example:** A user clicks your ad on their phone at work, but fills out the form on their laptop at home. Without enhanced conversions, this is an untracked conversion. With enhanced conversions, Google matches the email hash and attributes the conversion correctly.

### Offline Conversion Tracking

For businesses where the conversion happens offline (phone call, in-store visit, signed contract), offline conversion tracking closes the loop.

```
OFFLINE CONVERSION FLOW

Day 1: User clicks ad → lands on your page → fills form → lead captured
       Google captures gclid (Google Click Identifier) with the form submission

Day 15: Sales team closes the deal (offline)

Day 16: Upload conversion data to Google Ads:
        ├── gclid: xxxxxxxxxxxxx
        ├── Conversion name: "Closed Deal"
        ├── Conversion time: 2025-10-16 14:30:00
        └── Conversion value: Rs. 50,000

Google Ads now knows:
- Which keyword led to this sale
- Which ad was clicked
- Which audience segment converted
- Smart Bidding uses this data to find more similar users
```

**Real-Time Example — A Real Estate Company:**
- Ads drive inquiries (form fills) — tracked as online conversions
- Sales team calls leads, gives site visits, negotiates for weeks
- When a deal closes (Rs. 75 lakh flat sold), they upload the gclid + conversion value to Google Ads
- Google Ads now optimizes for leads that actually close deals (not just any form fill)
- CPA drops from Rs. 5,000/lead to Rs. 3,000/qualified lead because Smart Bidding targets users who match closed-deal profiles

### Attribution Models

| Model | How It Assigns Credit | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Data-Driven (Default)** | Uses AI to distribute credit based on actual contribution of each touchpoint | Most accounts (recommended by Google) |
| **Last-Click** | 100% credit to the last clicked ad | Simple tracking, small accounts |
| **First-Click** | 100% credit to the first clicked ad | Understanding discovery channels |
| **Linear** | Equal credit to all touchpoints | Understanding full journey |
| **Time-Decay** | More credit to touchpoints closer to conversion | Longer consideration cycles |
| **Position-Based** | 40% first, 40% last, 20% distributed in middle | Balanced view of funnel |

**Real-Time Example — Attribution Impact for a SaaS Company:**

```
Customer Journey:
Click 1: "What is CRM" (awareness keyword) → Blog post
Click 2: "Best CRM for startups" (consideration keyword) → Comparison page
Click 3: "FreshCRM pricing" (decision keyword) → Pricing page → Signs up

Last-Click Attribution:
"FreshCRM pricing" gets 100% credit → You think brand keywords drive all revenue

First-Click Attribution:
"What is CRM" gets 100% credit → You think awareness keywords drive all revenue

Data-Driven Attribution:
"What is CRM" gets 30% credit
"Best CRM for startups" gets 45% credit
"FreshCRM pricing" gets 25% credit
→ You understand the FULL picture and fund each stage appropriately
```

### Brand Protection Campaigns

Always bid on your own brand name:

**Why:**
1. **Cheap clicks:** Brand CPCs are Rs. 1-5 (vs. Rs. 20-100 for non-brand)
2. **Competitor defense:** If you don't bid, competitors will take position #1 for YOUR name
3. **Control messaging:** Your ad copy says exactly what you want (vs. Google choosing organic snippets)
4. **Additional SERP real estate:** Paid ad + organic result = more visibility

**Real-Time Example:** When you search "Zerodha" on Google, Zerodha's own ad appears first. Below it, you might see Angel Broking's ad: "Better than Zerodha? Try Angel One." If Zerodha didn't run brand ads, Angel One would be the first thing users see when searching for Zerodha.

## Implementation Steps

### Bid Management Optimization
1. Analyze 90-day performance data by keyword, device, location, and time
2. Identify top 20% keywords (by conversion volume) and protect their bids
3. Identify bottom 20% keywords (high spend, no conversions) and pause/reduce
4. Calculate target CPA or ROAS based on unit economics
5. Switch to Smart Bidding after 30+ conversions/month
6. Set up portfolio bid strategies for related campaigns
7. Schedule monthly bid adjustment reviews

### Quality Score Improvement
1. Export all keywords with Quality Score < 6
2. Check each component (CTR, relevance, landing page)
3. Rewrite ad copy for low-relevance keywords
4. Improve landing pages for low landing page experience keywords
5. Restructure ad groups where keywords don't share the same intent
6. Track QS changes weekly using scripts or manual exports

### Conversion Tracking Overhaul
1. Audit existing conversion tracking (are all conversion types tracked?)
2. Implement enhanced conversions for better cross-device tracking
3. Set up offline conversion imports for sales-cycle businesses
4. Review attribution model (switch to data-driven if sufficient data)
5. Assign accurate conversion values to each action type
6. Set appropriate conversion windows (7-90 days depending on sales cycle)

## Strategies

1. **The Conversion Cascade** — Assign different values to different conversion actions (whitepaper: Rs. 100, demo: Rs. 500, free trial: Rs. 1,000, purchase: Rs. 5,000). Use Maximize Conversion Value — Google optimizes for the highest total value, not just the most conversions.
2. **RLSA Power Play** — Create a campaign targeting broad, expensive keywords (like "software") but only show to RLSA audiences (past visitors). Affordable bids on premium keywords because the audience is pre-qualified.
3. **Day-of-Week Optimization** — Analyze 90 days of data by day of week. If Tuesday has 2x the conversion rate of Sunday, apply bid adjustments: Tuesday +40%, Sunday -30%.
4. **The Quality Score Sprint** — Pick your top 50 keywords by spend. For each one with QS < 7, create a dedicated improvement plan (better ad copy, better landing page, tighter ad groups). Target: move all top-50 keywords to QS 7+.
5. **Auction Insights Warfare** — Monitor Auction Insights monthly. If a competitor's impression share is growing, investigate their ad copy (using SpyFu) and respond with better messaging and bid adjustments.

## Best Practices

1. **Don't change bidding strategies during low-volume periods** — Smart Bidding needs consistent data; changing during holidays or weekends disrupts learning
2. **Allow 2-4 weeks for learning period** — After switching to Smart Bidding, resist the urge to adjust targets for at least 2 weeks
3. **Use experiments for big changes** — Never switch bidding strategies, landing pages, or match types for an entire campaign without testing first
4. **Track all conversion types** — Phone calls, form fills, chats, purchases — if you only track one type, Smart Bidding optimizes for an incomplete picture
5. **Set realistic targets** — If your current CPA is Rs. 1,200, don't set Target CPA at Rs. 400. Start at Rs. 1,200 and gradually reduce by 10-15% every 2-4 weeks
6. **Monitor Auction Insights monthly** — Know who you're competing against and how the competitive landscape is changing
7. **Layer audiences in Observation mode first** — Collect 30 days of data before making bid adjustments or switching to Targeting mode
8. **Keep your conversion tracking clean** — One wrong tag can poison your Smart Bidding by feeding it false signals
