## 6.5.1 Bing Ads Overview

### Bing's Market Share and Unique Audience

Most advertisers overlook Microsoft Advertising, and that's exactly why it's an opportunity. Bing isn't just "that other search engine" — it has a unique, high-value audience.

**Market Share (2025):**

| Market | Google | Bing (Microsoft) | Others |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Global Desktop** | 81% | 11% | 8% |
| **US Desktop** | 73% | 15% | 12% |
| **US Mobile** | 95% | 3% | 2% |
| **India** | 96% | 2% | 2% |
| **Enterprise/Corporate** | 65% | 30% | 5% |

**Why those numbers matter:** Bing is the default search engine on Windows PCs (85%+ of enterprise desktops), Microsoft Edge browser, and Cortana. Corporate employees who don't change browser defaults search on Bing — and these tend to be higher-income, older professionals.

**Bing's Unique Audience Demographics:**

```
BING USERS VS. GOOGLE USERS

Age Distribution:
├── 35-54 years old: 40% of Bing users (vs. 30% Google)
├── 55+ years old: 28% of Bing users (vs. 18% Google)
└── 18-34 years old: 32% of Bing users (vs. 52% Google)

Income:
├── Household income $75K+: 38% of Bing users
└── Higher average disposable income than Google users

Education:
├── College degree+: Higher proportion than Google
└── Professional/managerial roles: Overrepresented

Device:
├── Desktop dominant (enterprise Windows PCs)
└── Lower mobile share (users who don't change defaults)
```

**Key Insight:** Bing users are older, wealthier, and more likely to be in decision-making roles at companies. For B2B, financial services, travel, and luxury products, Bing delivers a premium audience at lower CPCs.

### Bing vs. Google Ads: Key Differences

| Feature | Google Ads | Microsoft Advertising |
|---|---|---|
| **Market share** | 80%+ | 10-15% (desktop) |
| **Average CPC** | Higher (more competition) | 20-35% lower CPCs |
| **Competition** | Intense across most verticals | Significantly less competition |
| **Audience** | Broad, younger skewing | Older, higher income, B2B |
| **LinkedIn targeting** | Not available | Unique — target by company, industry, job function |
| **Import from Google** | N/A | One-click campaign import |
| **Automation maturity** | Highly advanced Smart Bidding | Improving but less mature |
| **Shopping** | Google Merchant Center | Microsoft Merchant Center |
| **AI features** | Gemini-integrated | Copilot-integrated |
| **Minimum budget** | $5/day | $5/day |

### The Lower CPC Opportunity

**Real-Time Example — CPC Comparison for Same Keywords:**

| Keyword | Google CPC | Bing CPC | Savings |
|---|---|---|---|
| "CRM software" | Rs. 180 | Rs. 95 | -47% |
| "Car insurance quote" | Rs. 350 | Rs. 190 | -46% |
| "Online MBA program" | Rs. 250 | Rs. 140 | -44% |
| "Business accounting software" | Rs. 120 | Rs. 65 | -46% |
| "Luxury watches online" | Rs. 80 | Rs. 40 | -50% |

**The Math:** If you spend Rs. 2 lakh/month on Google Ads at Rs. 50 average CPC (4,000 clicks), the same Rs. 2 lakh on Bing at Rs. 30 CPC gives you 6,667 clicks — 67% more clicks for the same budget. Even if Bing's volume is smaller, the ROI per rupee is often higher.

### Microsoft Audience Network

The Microsoft Audience Network (MSAN) is Microsoft's display/native ad network:

- **Placements:** MSN, Outlook.com, Microsoft Edge homepage, publisher partner sites
- **Ad format:** Native ads that blend with content (not banner ads)
- **Targeting:** Uses LinkedIn profile data + browsing signals
- **Best for:** B2B awareness, content promotion, audience building

**Real-Time Example — HubSpot on Microsoft Audience Network:**
HubSpot runs native ads on MSN and Outlook targeting:
- LinkedIn Job Function: "Marketing Manager"
- LinkedIn Industry: "Technology"
- Ad: "Free Marketing Automation Guide — Download Now"
- Result: Qualified B2B leads at 35% lower cost than LinkedIn Ads

## 6.5.2 Importing from Google Ads

### Campaign Import Process

Microsoft Advertising allows direct import of Google Ads campaigns, saving hours of manual setup.

**Import Steps:**

```
GOOGLE ADS → MICROSOFT ADVERTISING IMPORT

Step 1: In Microsoft Advertising, click "Import" > "Import from Google Ads"
            │
Step 2: Sign in to your Google account and authorize access
            │
Step 3: Select campaigns to import
            │ ☑ Campaign 1: Brand Search
            │ ☑ Campaign 2: Non-Brand — Product A
            │ ☐ Campaign 3: Display Remarketing (not supported 1:1)
            │
Step 4: Review import settings
            │ - Adjust bids (recommend starting 20% lower)
            │ - Adjust budgets
            │ - Review location targeting
            │ - Check extension compatibility
            │
Step 5: Import and review
            │
Step 6: Post-import optimization
            │ - Remove unsupported features
            │ - Adjust for Bing-specific audience
            │ - Set up UET tracking (Bing's conversion tag)
```

### What Transfers and What Doesn't

| Transfers Successfully | Does NOT Transfer |
|---|---|
| Campaign structure (campaigns, ad groups) | Performance Max campaigns |
| Keywords and match types | Google-specific Smart campaigns |
| Ad copy (RSAs) | Conversion tracking (need UET) |
| Bid amounts | Google Analytics linking (need separate) |
| Location targeting | Some automated extensions |
| Ad scheduling | Customer Match lists (need separate upload) |
| Sitelinks, callouts | Dynamic remarketing setup |
| Budget settings | Audience lists (need UET-based lists) |
| Negative keywords | Google Merchant Center feed (need Microsoft Merchant Center) |

### Post-Import Optimization Checklist

1. **Reduce bids by 15-25%** — Lower competition means lower CPCs needed
2. **Install UET tag** — Microsoft's equivalent of the Google Ads tag for conversion tracking
3. **Set up Microsoft Merchant Center** — If running Shopping campaigns, import or create a new feed
4. **Adjust location targeting** — Verify settings; some granular targeting may differ
5. **Add LinkedIn profile targeting** — Bing's unique advantage; layer it on existing campaigns
6. **Review match types** — Bing's broad match can be even broader than Google's; consider tightening
7. **Check ad extensions** — Some may not import; add Bing-specific multimedia ads
8. **Set up automated imports** — Schedule daily/weekly sync to keep Bing campaigns aligned with Google changes
9. **Create Bing-specific ad copy** — Test variations that mention Bing-specific value props
10. **Monitor separately** — Don't just mirror Google; optimize Bing based on its own performance data

## 6.5.3 Bing-Specific Features

### LinkedIn Profile Targeting (Unique to Bing)

This is Microsoft's killer feature — no other search engine can target users by their LinkedIn professional profile.

**Available LinkedIn Targeting Dimensions:**

| Dimension | Options | Example Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| **Company** | Specific company names (10,000+ companies) | Target employees of Infosys, TCS, Wipro |
| **Industry** | 150+ industries | Target "Financial Services" or "Healthcare" |
| **Job Function** | Marketing, Sales, Finance, IT, HR, etc. | Target "IT Decision Makers" |

**Targeting Modes:**
- **Target & Bid:** Only show ads to users matching LinkedIn criteria (restricts reach)
- **Bid Only (Observation):** Show ads to everyone but bid higher for LinkedIn-matched users (recommended starting point)

**Real-Time Example — A B2B SaaS Tool (Freshservice):**

```
Campaign: Search — IT Helpdesk Software
├── Keywords: "IT helpdesk software," "service desk tool," "ITSM platform"
├── LinkedIn Targeting (Bid Only):
│   ├── Job Function: IT +25% bid adjustment
│   ├── Industry: Technology +15% bid adjustment
│   ├── Company: Fortune 500 companies +30% bid adjustment
│
│   Result: Same keywords, but IT managers at large tech companies
│   see higher bids → more likely to see the ad → higher quality leads
│
│   Without LinkedIn targeting: CPA Rs. 2,500 per lead
│   With LinkedIn targeting: CPA Rs. 1,800 per qualified lead
│   Quality: LinkedIn-targeted leads close at 2x the rate
```

### Universal Event Tracking (UET)

UET is Microsoft's equivalent of the Google Ads tag — a single tag that enables:
- Conversion tracking
- Remarketing audience creation
- Automated bidding optimization

**Setup:**
1. Create UET tag in Microsoft Advertising (Tools > UET Tags)
2. Install on all pages via Tag Manager or directly
3. Define conversion goals (URL destination, event, duration, pages per visit)
4. Verify with UET Tag Helper (Chrome extension)

### Microsoft Advertising Editor

Desktop tool (similar to Google Ads Editor) for bulk management:
- Offline editing of campaigns, ad groups, keywords, and ads
- Bulk import/export via CSV
- Find and replace across entire account
- Copy campaigns between accounts
- Available for Windows and Mac

## Implementation Steps

### Step 1: Account Setup (Day 1)
1. Create Microsoft Advertising account at ads.microsoft.com
2. Set billing information
3. Install UET tag via Google Tag Manager
4. Define conversion goals (purchases, form submissions, calls)
5. Verify UET tag fires using UET Tag Helper Chrome extension

### Step 2: Campaign Import (Day 2-3)
1. Import campaigns from Google Ads
2. Reduce bids by 15-25%
3. Adjust budgets (start at 10-15% of Google budget)
4. Remove unsupported features
5. Set up Microsoft Merchant Center if running Shopping

### Step 3: Bing-Specific Enhancements (Day 4-5)
1. Add LinkedIn profile targeting (Bid Only mode)
2. Create Multimedia Ads for top campaigns
3. Add Bing-specific ad extensions
4. Set up Microsoft Audience Network campaigns
5. Create Bing-specific remarketing audiences via UET

### Step 4: Launch & Monitor (Week 1-2)
1. Launch imported campaigns
2. Monitor CPC, CTR, and conversion differences vs. Google
3. Check search term reports (Bing's broad match can be very broad)
4. Add Bing-specific negative keywords
5. Analyze LinkedIn targeting performance

### Step 5: Optimize (Ongoing)
1. Adjust bids based on Bing-specific performance data
2. Test Bing-specific features (multimedia ads, LinkedIn targeting)
3. Scale budget on well-performing campaigns
4. Set up automated import schedule to keep sync with Google changes
5. Report on Bing vs. Google CPA, ROAS, and volume separately

## Strategies

1. **The 10% Rule** — Allocate 10-15% of your total PPC budget to Bing. It's low risk, and the lower CPCs often deliver better CPA than Google.
2. **LinkedIn Layer for B2B** — For every B2B search campaign, add LinkedIn targeting as a bid-only layer. Bid +25% for target job functions, +30% for target companies.
3. **Enterprise Audience Play** — Bing over-indexes on corporate users. If you sell B2B software, financial services, or professional services, Bing should be a priority, not an afterthought.
4. **Import + Customize** — Don't just mirror Google. After importing, create Bing-specific ad copy, adjust bids for Bing's audience, and add multimedia ads.
5. **DuckDuckGo Presence** — DuckDuckGo serves Bing ads. Privacy-conscious users who use DuckDuckGo are reachable only through Microsoft Advertising.
6. **Shopping Arbitrage** — Shopping CPCs on Bing are often 40-60% lower than Google. For e-commerce, this is free money. Import your feed and capture the volume.

## Best Practices

1. **Treat Bing as a separate channel** — Don't just import and forget; optimize based on Bing's own data
2. **Use LinkedIn targeting immediately** — It's Bing's most powerful differentiator; test it from day one
3. **Monitor broad match closely** — Bing's broad match interpretation can be extremely broad; tighten or add negatives aggressively
4. **Leverage multimedia ads** — They dominate the SERP on Bing; higher CTR than standard text ads
5. **Set up automated import** — Keep Bing campaigns in sync with Google changes automatically (daily or weekly)
6. **Test different bids** — Start 20% lower than Google and adjust based on performance
7. **Install Microsoft Clarity** — It's free and provides heatmaps/session recordings that complement your PPC optimization
8. **Track cross-platform attribution** — Users may see a Bing ad and then convert on Google (or vice versa); consider this in your attribution model
9. **Don't ignore the Microsoft Audience Network** — MSAN delivers quality native ad placements at competitive CPMs
10. **Report on Bing ROI separately** — Show stakeholders the lower CPA on Bing vs. Google to justify continued investment
