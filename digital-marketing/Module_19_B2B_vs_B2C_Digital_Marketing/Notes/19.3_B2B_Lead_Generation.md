# 19.3 B2B Lead Generation — Comprehensive Notes

## 19.3.1 Demand Generation vs. Lead Generation

### Understanding the Difference

This is one of the most important distinctions in modern B2B marketing. Most companies confuse the two, leading to wasted budget and poor results.

| Dimension | Demand Generation | Lead Generation |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | Create awareness and desire for your category/solution | Capture contact information for sales follow-up |
| **Content** | Ungated — freely available | Gated — requires form fill |
| **Metrics** | Brand awareness, share of voice, organic traffic, podcast downloads, social engagement | MQLs, form fills, demo requests, trial signups |
| **Timeframe** | Long-term (6–18 months to see results) | Short-term (results within weeks) |
| **Channels** | Podcasts, social media, communities, PR, events, ungated content | Landing pages, gated ebooks, webinar registrations, paid ads |
| **Buyer Experience** | Educational, no-pressure | Transactional, requires exchange of info |
| **Attribution** | Difficult to measure directly (dark funnel) | Easy to track (form fills, ad clicks) |

**The Key Insight:**
Demand generation creates the desire; lead generation captures it. You need both — but most companies over-invest in lead capture (gated content, forms) and under-invest in demand creation (brand, education, community).

**Real-Time Example:**
Gong.io's demand gen vs. lead gen strategy:

**Demand Gen (creating demand):**
- "Revenue Intelligence" podcast — 500K+ total downloads
- LinkedIn posts by CEO Amit Bendov — 50K+ followers, daily posts on sales leadership
- Data-driven research reports released ungated (State of Sales, Remote Selling Report)
- Community events (Celebrate, Revenue Intelligence Summit) with 10K+ attendees
- Result: "Gong" became synonymous with "revenue intelligence" — buyers search for Gong by name

**Lead Gen (capturing demand):**
- Demo request form on website (bottom-funnel)
- Gated industry benchmarks and templates
- Free tools (Gong Labs — sales call analysis insights)
- Webinars with lead capture
- Result: Inbound demo requests grew 300% because demand gen created brand awareness

**The Relationship:**
```
Demand Gen (top of funnel) → Brand Awareness → Buyer Education → Trust
                                                                    ↓
                                                        Buyer decides to evaluate
                                                                    ↓
Lead Gen (bottom of funnel) → Form Fill → MQL → Sales Follow-Up → Revenue
```

### The Pendulum: When to Gate vs. Ungate

The industry is moving from "gate everything" to a hybrid model. Here's a decision framework:

**The Gating Decision Tree:**

```
Is the content truly premium and unique?
├── YES → Is it available for free elsewhere?
│        ├── YES → Ungate (you'll lose to the free version)
│        └── NO → Gate it (original research, proprietary tools)
└── NO → Ungate (blog posts, general guides, product info)
```

**What to Gate:**
- Original research reports with proprietary data
- ROI calculators and assessment tools
- Premium templates and frameworks
- Exclusive event recordings (for a limited time)
- Advanced certification courses

**What to Ungate:**
- Blog posts and thought leadership
- Product information, pricing, and feature descriptions
- Case study summaries (gate the full version)
- Podcasts and YouTube videos
- General how-to guides

**Real-Time Example:**
HubSpot's evolution:
- **2010–2018**: Gated everything — built a massive email list of 15M+ contacts
- **2019–2022**: Started ungating more content as competition caught up
- **2023–Present**: Hybrid model — ungated blog, ungated basic tools (Website Grader), gated premium research and certifications
- Key learning: early gating built their list and brand; now that they have awareness, ungating drives more reach

### Creating Demand Before Capturing Leads

**The Demand Creation Playbook:**

| Phase | Activity | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Define POV** | Establish your unique perspective on the problem space | Month 1 |
| **Phase 2: Build Channels** | Launch podcast, social, community, newsletter | Months 1–3 |
| **Phase 3: Create Content Engine** | Consistent ungated content across channels | Months 3–6 |
| **Phase 4: Build Community** | Engage in discussions, host events, create belonging | Months 6–12 |
| **Phase 5: Earn Trust** | Demonstrate expertise through original research and results | Months 6–18 |
| **Phase 6: Capture Demand** | Introduce lead capture for buyers who self-select | Month 12+ |

**Real-Time Example:**
Lavender (AI email coaching platform) built demand before lead gen:
1. CEO Will Allred posted 3x/day on LinkedIn about cold email techniques (demand gen)
2. Built a 100K+ LinkedIn following over 18 months
3. Created free email analysis tools (demand gen)
4. Only then launched paid product and lead gen campaigns
5. Result: By the time they launched lead gen, thousands of SDRs already knew and trusted Lavender

### Demand Gen Metrics vs. Lead Gen Metrics

| Demand Gen Metrics | Lead Gen Metrics |
|---|---|
| Branded search volume (Google Trends) | Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) |
| Share of voice (social mentions, media coverage) | Cost Per Lead (CPL) |
| Organic website traffic growth | Conversion Rate (visitor → lead) |
| Podcast downloads / YouTube views | Lead-to-Opportunity Rate |
| Community membership and engagement | Cost Per Opportunity |
| Direct traffic | Pipeline Created ($) |
| "How did you hear about us?" survey responses | Revenue Attributed to Marketing |
| Social media follower growth and engagement | Demo Requests |

**Real-Time Example:**
Refine Labs' approach to measuring demand:
- Added "How did you hear about us?" as a free-text field on demo request forms
- Found that 60%+ of buyers said "podcast," "LinkedIn," or "a friend told me" — channels that traditional attribution couldn't track
- Called this the "dark funnel" — the invisible journey that happens before a buyer fills out a form
- Result: Shifted 40% of budget from gated content lead gen to ungated demand gen channels

### SEO and Organic Content

Organic search is the #1 lead gen channel for most B2B companies by volume and quality.

**B2B SEO Lead Gen Funnel:**
```
Awareness Content          → Blog subscriber → Newsletter nurture
(blog posts, guides)                              ↓
                                          Consideration Content
                                     (comparison, case study pages)
                                                  ↓
                                          Decision Content
                                    (pricing, demo, free trial pages)
                                                  ↓
                                               LEAD
```

**Real-Time Example:**
Zapier generates majority of revenue from organic search:
- 25,000+ landing pages targeting integration-specific searches
- Each page targets a long-tail keyword ("connect Slack to Google Sheets")
- Pages include setup instructions, driving users to create a free account
- Result: Organic search drives the majority of Zapier's 5M+ users

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)

PLG is a B2B lead gen strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of customer acquisition, expansion, and retention.

**PLG Models:**

| Model | How It Works | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Freemium** | Free forever tier with limited features | Slack (free for small teams, paid for enterprise) |
| **Free Trial** | Full product access for limited time (7–30 days) | Salesforce (30-day free trial) |
| **Reverse Trial** | Start with full features, downgrade to free after trial | Ahrefs (7-day full access, then limited free tools) |
| **Open Source** | Core product is free; premium features are paid | GitLab (open source core, paid enterprise) |

**PLG Lead Qualification — Product Qualified Leads (PQLs):**

| Signal | Score | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Signed up for free trial | 10 | Add to nurture sequence |
| Invited 3+ team members | 25 | Alert sales for outreach |
| Used core feature 5+ times | 20 | Send advanced use case content |
| Connected integration | 15 | Highlight enterprise features |
| Hit usage limit | 30 | Trigger upgrade prompt + sales outreach |
| Viewed pricing page | 20 | Real-time sales alert |

**Real-Time Example:**
Slack's PLG flywheel:
1. One person downloads Slack and creates a workspace (free)
2. They invite their team (viral loop — free for up to 10K messages)
3. Team becomes dependent on Slack for daily communication
4. They hit the 10K message limit — old messages disappear
5. Team asks IT to upgrade to paid plan
6. IT evaluates Slack Pro ($7.25/user/month) vs. Slack Business+ ($12.50/user/month)
7. Enterprise features (compliance, SSO, data retention) drive larger deal
8. Result: Bottom-up adoption drives $1.5B+ ARR

## 19.3.3 B2B Lead Qualification

### Lead Qualification Frameworks

| Framework | Stands For | Best For | Key Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BANT** | Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline | Established companies with known budgets | "Do they have the budget and authority to buy?" |
| **MEDDIC** | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion | Enterprise, complex sales | "Do we have a champion and know the decision process?" |
| **CHAMP** | Challenges, Authority, Money, Prioritization | Challenger/consultative selling | "What challenge are they trying to solve?" |
| **GPCTBA/C&I** | Goals, Plans, Challenges, Timeline, Budget, Authority / Consequences, Implications | HubSpot's framework for inbound leads | "What are their goals and what happens if they don't achieve them?" |

**Real-Time Example — MEDDIC in Action (Enterprise Software Sale):**

| Element | Question | Answer for Deal: "Acme Corp evaluating CRM" |
|---|---|---|
| **Metrics** | What quantifiable outcome will success look like? | "Reduce sales cycle by 20%, increase win rate by 15%" |
| **Economic Buyer** | Who has final budget authority? | "VP of Sales, Sarah Chen" |
| **Decision Criteria** | What criteria will they use to evaluate options? | "Integration with existing ERP, mobile access, AI forecasting" |
| **Decision Process** | What is the step-by-step evaluation process? | "Demo → POC → Security review → Board approval → Contract" |
| **Identify Pain** | What is the pain driving this evaluation? | "Lost 3 enterprise deals because sales couldn't forecast accurately" |
| **Champion** | Who inside the account is advocating for us? | "Director of Sales Ops, who attended our webinar and requested the demo" |

### Lead Qualification Stages

```
VISITOR → LEAD → MQL → SAL → SQL → OPPORTUNITY → CUSTOMER
  |         |      |      |     |        |            |
  ↓         ↓      ↓      ↓     ↓        ↓            ↓
Anonymous  Known  Meets   Sales  Sales   Qualified    Closed
visitor    contact marketing accepts qualifies deal in   won
           info    criteria lead   as real pipeline
                           within opportunity
                           SLA
```

**Definitions:**

| Stage | Definition | Owner | SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lead** | Known contact with basic info (name, email, company) | Marketing | N/A |
| **MQL** | Lead meets marketing's qualification criteria (score, fit, engagement) | Marketing | Pass to sales within 24 hours |
| **SAL** | Sales acknowledges receipt and agrees lead meets criteria | Sales | Accept/reject within 4 hours |
| **SQL** | Sales confirms opportunity through discovery call | Sales | Update CRM within 48 hours |
| **Opportunity** | Qualified deal entered in pipeline with close date and amount | Sales | Progress through stages |
| **PQL** | Free user hits product usage thresholds indicating purchase readiness | Product/Sales | Sales outreach within 24 hours |

**Real-Time Example — Lead Scoring Model:**

| Action | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Visits website | +1 | Low intent signal |
| Downloads ebook | +5 | Content engagement |
| Attends webinar | +10 | Active learning |
| Views pricing page | +15 | High intent |
| Requests demo | +25 | Bottom-funnel |
| Company fits ICP | +20 | High fit |
| Company doesn't fit ICP | -20 | Low fit |
| VP/C-suite title | +15 | Decision-maker |
| Individual contributor title | +5 | Influencer/user |
| No activity in 30 days | -10 | Decay |

**MQL threshold**: Score ≥ 50

### Handoff Process and SLAs

The lead handoff is where most B2B companies lose deals. Speed and clarity matter.

**SLA Framework:**

| Metric | Standard | Best-in-Class |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing → Sales handoff time | Within 24 hours | Within 1 hour |
| Sales follow-up on MQL | Within 4 hours | Within 5 minutes |
| Sales feedback on lead quality | Within 48 hours | Within 24 hours |
| MQL → SQL conversion rate | 20–30% | 40–50% |
| SQL → Opportunity rate | 50–60% | 70–80% |

**Real-Time Example:**
InsideSales.com (now XANT) research on lead response time:
- Leads contacted within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert than leads contacted after 30 minutes
- After 1 hour, the probability of qualifying a lead drops by 60x
- Best practice: Use real-time alerts (Slack notification, CRM workflow) to notify sales the moment a high-intent action occurs (demo request, pricing page visit)

### Feedback Loops Between Sales and Marketing

**The Feedback Loop System:**

| Feedback Type | Frequency | Format | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lead quality feedback** | Weekly | CRM disposition codes (Good Lead, Wrong Fit, Not Ready) | Adjust scoring model and targeting |
| **Content effectiveness** | Monthly | "Which content helped close deals?" survey | Create more of what works |
| **Competitive intelligence** | Ongoing | Sales shares competitor mentions from calls | Update battle cards and competitive content |
| **Objection tracking** | Monthly | Sales logs common objections in CRM | Create content addressing top objections |
| **Win/loss analysis** | Quarterly | Structured interviews with closed accounts | Refine ICP, messaging, and content strategy |

**Real-Time Example:**
Clari's feedback loop system:
- Every closed-lost deal triggers an automated survey to the AE asking "Why did we lose?"
- Top reasons are categorized: Price, Feature Gap, Competitor, Timing, No Decision
- Marketing receives a monthly report of loss reasons
- Result: When "Feature Gap: AI Forecasting" appeared in 30% of losses, marketing created a targeted campaign highlighting their new AI features → recaptured 15% of lost deals

### Implementation Steps for B2B Lead Generation

1. **Audit current channels**: Map all lead sources, volume, quality, and cost
2. **Define qualification criteria**: Build MQL/SQL definitions with sales input
3. **Build scoring model**: Assign scores to demographic and behavioral signals
4. **Set up SLAs**: Document response times and feedback commitments
5. **Launch multi-channel campaigns**: Diversify across SEO, paid, events, and PLG
6. **Implement feedback loops**: Create weekly sales-marketing syncs focused on lead quality
7. **Optimize continuously**: Review scoring model quarterly, adjust thresholds based on conversion data

### Gap Analysis

| Common Gap | Impact | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No demand gen, only lead gen | Expensive leads, low brand awareness | Invest 30–40% of budget in ungated demand creation |
| Slow lead response time | Leads go cold, competitors win | Implement real-time routing and 5-minute SLA |
| No lead scoring | Sales wastes time on unqualified leads | Build and calibrate a scoring model |
| No feedback loops | Marketing optimizes in the dark | Weekly sales-marketing sync with CRM data |
| Single-channel dependency | Fragile pipeline | Diversify to 3–5 lead gen channels |
| No PQL tracking (for PLG companies) | Free users never convert | Build PQL scoring based on product usage data |

### Strategies

| Strategy | Approach | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **Dark Funnel Investment** | Shift 30% of budget to unmeasurable but high-impact channels (podcast, community, social) | Higher quality inbound leads with lower CAC |
| **Speed-to-Lead** | Implement real-time routing and instant scheduling for demo requests | 3–5x improvement in lead-to-meeting conversion |
| **Content-Led PLG** | Offer free tools (calculators, assessments) that demonstrate product value | Organic lead gen with high PQL conversion |
| **Multi-Channel Nurture** | Nurture leads across email, retargeting, LinkedIn, and direct mail simultaneously | 2–3x improvement in MQL-to-SQL conversion |
| **Win/Loss Program** | Interview every closed-lost deal to understand why you lost | Targeted content and campaigns to address top loss reasons |

### Best Practices

- Never optimize for MQL volume alone — optimize for pipeline and revenue
- Speed kills competition: the first vendor to respond wins 35–50% of the time
- Use "How did you hear about us?" as a free-text field to understand the dark funnel
- Build a "surround sound" strategy: be present on the podcasts, communities, and review sites your buyers trust
- Gate less, engage more: the future of B2B lead gen is helping buyers buy, not extracting their email addresses
- Invest in customer marketing: referrals and word-of-mouth are the highest-quality, lowest-cost leads
- Align incentives: don't pay marketing for MQLs and sales for revenue — both should be measured on pipeline
