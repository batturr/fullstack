## 5.6.1 Social Media Metrics

### Core Metrics Every Marketer Must Track

Understanding metrics is the difference between guessing and knowing. Every metric tells a story — the key is knowing which story matters for your goals.

### Reach and Impressions

| Metric | Definition | Example | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Reach** | Number of unique people who saw your content | 10,000 reach = 10,000 individual people | Tells you audience breadth |
| **Impressions** | Total number of times content was displayed | 25,000 impressions from 10,000 reach = avg 2.5 views each | Tells you content frequency/exposure |

**Key Relationship:**
- Impressions are always ≥ Reach
- Impressions / Reach = Frequency (how many times avg person saw it)
- High frequency (>3-4) can indicate ad fatigue
- Low reach but high impressions can mean your content is shown repeatedly to same people (filter bubble)

**Benchmarks by Platform:**

| Platform | Average Organic Reach (% of followers) | Average Paid Reach (per $1) |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 1.5-2.5% | 70-120 people |
| Instagram | 3-5% (feed), 15-30% (Reels) | 80-150 people |
| LinkedIn | 8-12% | 30-50 people |
| TikTok | 10-20%+ (FYP-driven) | 100-200 people |
| X (Twitter) | 2-5% | 50-100 people |
| Pinterest | Varies (search-driven) | 60-130 people |

### Engagement Rate

**Three Ways to Calculate Engagement Rate:**

| Method | Formula | When to Use | Industry Avg |
|---|---|---|---|
| By followers | (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Followers x 100 | Comparing accounts of different sizes | 1-5% |
| By reach | (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Reach x 100 | Measuring content quality (most accurate) | 3-8% |
| By impressions | (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Impressions x 100 | Ad performance measurement | 1-3% |

**Engagement Rate Benchmarks by Platform (2025-2026):**

| Platform | Low | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | <1% | 1-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| TikTok | <2% | 3-6% | 6-10% | 10%+ |
| LinkedIn | <1% | 2-4% | 4-6% | 6%+ |
| Facebook | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |
| X (Twitter) | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |
| YouTube | <1% | 1-3% | 3-5% | 5%+ |
| Pinterest | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |

**Engagement Hierarchy (most to least valuable):**
1. **Saves** — "I want to come back to this" (highest intent signal)
2. **Shares/Sends** — "Others need to see this" (distribution signal)
3. **Comments** — "I want to engage with this" (conversation signal)
4. **Likes** — "I acknowledge this" (lowest effort signal)

**Real-Time Example:**
A skincare brand compared two posts: Post A had 5,000 likes but 20 saves. Post B had 800 likes but 400 saves. Despite Post A having 6x more likes, Post B drove 3x more website traffic and 5x more sales. The saves indicated purchase intent — people bookmarked the product for later. The brand shifted their strategy to optimize for saves over likes, increasing monthly revenue from social by 25%.

### Click-Through Rate (CTR)

| Context | Formula | Average | Good | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Social post to link | Clicks / Impressions x 100 | 0.5-1% | 1-2% | 2%+ |
| Social ad (Meta) | Clicks / Impressions x 100 | 0.9-1.2% | 1.2-2% | 2%+ |
| Social ad (LinkedIn) | Clicks / Impressions x 100 | 0.4-0.6% | 0.6-1% | 1%+ |
| Email → social | Clicks / Opens x 100 | 2-4% | 4-6% | 6%+ |

### Follower Growth Rate

**Formula:** ((New Followers - Unfollows) / Total Followers at Start) x 100

| Growth Rate | Assessment | Action |
|---|---|---|
| <0% (declining) | Losing audience, content not resonating | Audit content, test new formats |
| 0-1% / month | Stagnant, maintaining | Experiment with new content types |
| 1-3% / month | Healthy organic growth | Continue and optimize |
| 3-5% / month | Strong growth | Double down on what's working |
| 5%+ / month | Exceptional (or viral moment) | Scale, but ensure quality followers |

### Share of Voice (SOV)

**Formula:** Your brand mentions / (Your mentions + Competitor mentions) x 100

| SOV Range | Market Position | Action |
|---|---|---|
| <10% | Invisible | Aggressive awareness campaigns, PR push |
| 10-25% | Challenger | Differentiated content, niche domination |
| 25-40% | Contender | Capitalize on momentum, expand topics |
| 40-60% | Leader | Defend position, innovate |
| 60%+ | Dominant | Watch for complacency; diversify |

**Real-Time Example:**
In the CRM space, HubSpot monitors Share of Voice against Salesforce, Zoho, and Pipedrive on social media. Despite being smaller, HubSpot's SOV on LinkedIn is 38% (vs. Salesforce's 42%) because they invest heavily in educational content and employee advocacy. Their SOV growth from 22% to 38% over 3 years correlates directly with their 35% annual revenue growth.

### Sentiment Score

**Net Sentiment Score (NSS):**

Formula: ((Positive Mentions - Negative Mentions) / Total Mentions) x 100

| NSS Range | Assessment | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 80-100% | Extremely positive | Maintain; leverage for testimonials |
| 60-80% | Healthy positive | Normal range for most brands |
| 40-60% | Mixed sentiment | Investigate negative drivers |
| 20-40% | Concerning | Address issues, improve customer experience |
| <20% | Crisis zone | Immediate intervention required |

### Video Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| Views | Number of times video was watched (varies by platform) | Platform-specific count | Awareness indicator |
| Watch time | Total minutes/hours people spent watching | Higher = better | Algorithm ranking signal |
| Average view duration | Avg seconds/minutes per view | >50% of video length = strong | Content quality indicator |
| Completion rate | % who watched to the end | 30-40% for short, 15-25% for long | Hooks and pacing effectiveness |
| 3-second views | % who watched at least 3 seconds | 40-60% | Hook effectiveness |
| ThruPlay (Meta) | Views of 15s+ (or full video if <15s) | Higher = better | Quality view indicator |
| Replays | Number of times video was rewatched | Any replays are strong signal | Content loop-worthiness |

**Video View Counting by Platform:**

| Platform | What Counts as a "View" |
|---|---|
| TikTok | Video starts playing (instant) |
| Instagram Reels | 3+ seconds (or replay) |
| Facebook | 3+ seconds |
| YouTube | 30 seconds or full video (whichever is shorter) |
| LinkedIn | 2+ seconds with 50%+ in viewport |
| X (Twitter) | 2+ seconds with 50%+ in viewport |

### Stories Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark | Optimization |
|---|---|---|---|
| Story reach | Unique accounts that viewed | 5-15% of followers | Post at optimal times |
| Exits | Left Stories at this frame | <5% per frame | Shorten, improve content |
| Taps forward | Tapped to next frame quickly | <50% per frame | Content isn't engaging enough |
| Taps back | Went back to re-read/re-watch | Higher = content was interesting | Create save-worthy Stories |
| Replies | DMs in response to Story | 1-5% of viewers | Use question stickers |
| Link clicks | Tapped link sticker | 1-3% of viewers | Compelling CTA + teaser |
| Sticker interactions | Votes, quiz answers, slider | 10-30% of viewers | Use interactive stickers daily |

### Conversion Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Formula | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leads from social | People who submitted info via social | Platform-tracked lead events | Varies by industry |
| Social-attributed revenue | Revenue tracked back to social | CRM + UTM + pixel attribution | 5-25% of total revenue |
| Social conversion rate | % of social visitors who convert | Conversions / Social traffic x 100 | 1-3% (e-commerce) |
| Cost per lead (CPL) | Cost to acquire one lead from social | Ad spend / Leads | $5-50 (B2C), $30-200 (B2B) |
| ROAS | Revenue generated per $1 of ad spend | Revenue / Ad spend | 2-5x (healthy), 5x+ (excellent) |
| Customer acquisition cost (CAC) | Total cost to acquire one customer from social | Total social spend / New customers | Varies by industry |

## 5.6.4 Social Media Reporting

### Building a Social Media Report

**Report Structure:**

```
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
   - Key wins, key challenges, 3-5 top-line metrics
   - 2-3 sentences max

2. PERFORMANCE OVERVIEW
   - Follower growth (all platforms)
   - Total reach and impressions
   - Overall engagement rate
   - Website traffic from social

3. PLATFORM BREAKDOWN
   - Per-platform performance with top 3 posts
   - Platform-specific KPIs
   - Observations and trends

4. CONTENT ANALYSIS
   - Top-performing content (and why)
   - Worst-performing content (and why)
   - Content mix performance (by pillar)
   - Format performance (video vs. image vs. carousel)

5. AUDIENCE INSIGHTS
   - Demographic changes
   - Growth/decline analysis
   - Audience behavior patterns

6. COMPETITIVE BENCHMARKS
   - SOV comparison
   - Engagement rate comparison
   - Content strategy observations

7. PAID SOCIAL PERFORMANCE (if applicable)
   - Spend, ROAS, CPA, CTR
   - Top-performing campaigns/creatives
   - Budget recommendations

8. ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATIONS
   - 3-5 specific next steps with rationale
   - Priorities for next period

9. APPENDIX
   - Raw data tables
   - Methodology notes
```

### ROI Calculation for Social Media

**Social Media ROI Formula:**

```
ROI = ((Revenue from Social - Cost of Social) / Cost of Social) x 100
```

**Cost Components:**

| Category | Include in Cost |
|---|---|
| Team salaries | % of time allocated to social |
| Tools & software | All social media tools subscriptions |
| Paid advertising | Total ad spend across platforms |
| Content creation | Photography, videography, design costs |
| Influencer/creator | Creator fees, product gifting costs |
| Training | Courses, conferences, certifications |
| Agency fees | If outsourcing any social functions |

**Revenue Attribution Methods:**

| Method | Accuracy | Complexity | Tools |
|---|---|---|---|
| Last-click UTM | Medium | Low | Google Analytics + UTMs |
| Platform pixel | Medium-High | Medium | Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag |
| Multi-touch attribution | High | High | Triple Whale, Hyros, GA4 |
| Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) | Highest | Very High | Custom statistical models |
| Incrementality testing | Highest | Very High | Holdout/geo-lift experiments |

**Real-Time Example — Calculating Social Media ROI:**

A DTC brand's social media monthly costs:
- Social media manager salary (allocated): $4,000
- Tools (Sprout, Canva, CapCut): $300
- Paid ad spend: $8,000
- UGC creators (5 videos): $1,500
- Total cost: **$13,800**

Revenue attributed to social:
- Direct social commerce (Instagram Shop): $12,000
- Website purchases tracked via UTM from social: $18,000
- Website purchases tracked via Meta Pixel: $22,000
- Estimated dark social influence (conservative 10% of total): $5,000
- Total revenue: **$57,000**

**ROI = (($57,000 - $13,800) / $13,800) x 100 = 313% ROI**

For every $1 invested in social media, they earned $4.13 back.

### Actionable Insights and Recommendations

**The Insight → Action Framework:**

Every data point should connect to an action:

| Data Observation | Insight | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Reels get 3x more reach than images | Algorithm prioritizes video content | Shift content mix to 50% Reels |
| Best engagement at 11am Tuesdays | Audience is most active mid-morning weekdays | Reschedule key posts to Tue/Wed 11am |
| Carousel saves are 5x higher than images | Educational carousels have high intent value | Create 2 carousels per week |
| Follower growth stalled at 15K | Organic discovery plateaued | Invest in collaboration and paid growth |
| Top 3 posts all featured customer stories | UGC and storytelling resonate most | Increase customer story content by 30% |
| LinkedIn comments dropped 40% after algorithm change | LinkedIn deprioritized certain content types | Test new formats: polls, documents, newsletters |
| CPA increased 25% in Meta ads | Audience fatigue or creative stale | Refresh creatives, test new audiences |

**Real-Time Example:**
Netflix's social media team runs weekly "insight sprints" — they review the week's analytics, identify the top 3 insights, and translate each into a specific action for the following week. When they noticed that posts about "behind-the-scenes production" got 4x more engagement than standard show announcements, they shifted 30% of their content to BTS content. Within 2 months, their overall engagement rate increased from 1.8% to 3.1%.

## Implementation Steps for Section 5.6

### Step 1: Define Your KPI Dashboard (Week 1)
- Select 5-8 primary KPIs aligned with business goals
- Define how each KPI is calculated (formula, source)
- Set benchmarks (historical, competitive, industry)
- **Tools:** Google Sheets (KPI definition), Looker Studio (dashboard)

### Step 2: Set Up Tracking Infrastructure (Week 1-2)
- Ensure all platform analytics are accessible (business/creator accounts)
- Install UTM parameters on all social links
- Set up Google Analytics 4 with social traffic segments
- Create UTM naming convention: utm_source=platform, utm_medium=social, utm_campaign=campaign_name
- **Tools:** GA4, Google Tag Manager, UTM builder (campaign-url-builder)

### Step 3: Build Reporting Templates (Week 2-3)
- Create weekly, monthly, and quarterly report templates
- Set up automated data pulls (Supermetrics → Google Sheets → Looker Studio)
- Build competitive benchmarking tracker
- Create executive-level 1-page report template
- **Tools:** Looker Studio (dashboards), DashThis (automated reports), Google Sheets (tracking)

### Step 4: Establish Reporting Cadence (Week 3)
- Weekly: Quick performance check (15 min review)
- Monthly: Full analysis and optimization recommendations (1-2 hour review)
- Quarterly: Strategic review with leadership (half-day workshop)
- **Schedule:** Block recurring calendar time for each cadence

### Step 5: Build Insight-to-Action Culture (Ongoing)
- Every report must end with 3-5 specific action items
- Track if recommendations were implemented and their impact
- Monthly team meeting to discuss findings and align on next steps
- **Tools:** Notion (action item tracking), Slack/Teams (weekly updates)

## Strategies

1. **KPI-First Reporting Strategy:** Before launching any campaign, define what success looks like with specific KPI targets. Measure against those targets, not arbitrary comparisons.
2. **Data Storytelling Strategy:** Present analytics as narratives, not spreadsheets. "Our Reels strategy drove 40% of social traffic this quarter, up from 15%" tells a story that data tables don't.
3. **Insight-to-Action Loop Strategy:** Every metric observed must connect to a decision. If a metric doesn't change what you do, stop tracking it.
4. **Competitive Intelligence Strategy:** Track 3-5 competitors monthly. Benchmark your performance against them, study their top content, and identify gaps in their strategy that you can fill.
5. **Attribution Stack Strategy:** Don't rely on a single attribution method. Use platform pixels + UTMs + CRM data + incrementality testing for a holistic view of social's true impact.
6. **Automated Reporting Strategy:** Invest 4-6 hours upfront to build automated dashboards. This saves 2-4 hours per week in manual reporting — paying for itself within a month.

---

## Best Practices

1. **Track fewer metrics, but track them well.** 5-8 meaningful KPIs are better than 50 vanity metrics. Focus on metrics that drive decisions.
2. **Always include context.** A 3% engagement rate means nothing without context. Is it up or down? How does it compare to competitors? What caused the change?
3. **Report insights, not data.** Executives don't want to see 50 data points. They want to know: Is it working? What should we change? How much should we invest?
4. **Automate data collection.** Manual data pulling is error-prone and time-consuming. Use tools like Supermetrics, Looker Studio, or DashThis.
5. **Benchmark against yourself first.** Your own historical data is the most relevant benchmark. Industry averages are useful for context, not goals.
6. **Separate organic and paid metrics.** Mixing organic and paid data distorts both. Report them separately to understand each channel's true performance.
7. **Review weekly, optimize monthly, strategize quarterly.** Different time horizons require different analyses and decisions.
8. **Calculate ROI, even imperfectly.** An imperfect ROI calculation is infinitely better than no ROI calculation. Leadership needs to see revenue impact.

## Additional Insights

### The Measurement Paradox
The most impactful social media outcomes (brand perception, trust, word-of-mouth) are the hardest to measure. The easiest-to-measure outcomes (likes, impressions) are often the least impactful. Great social media analytics balance quantitative metrics (what happened) with qualitative insights (why it matters).

### The Dark Social Attribution Problem
An estimated 80%+ of social sharing happens in private channels (DMs, WhatsApp, email, Slack) that analytics tools can't track. When someone texts a friend "check out this brand I found," there's no UTM parameter. Accept that your reported social ROI is likely 30-50% lower than actual social ROI. Some brands add a "How did you hear about us?" field to capture dark social.

### The Metrics That Actually Predict Revenue
After studying hundreds of brands, the metrics most predictive of future social-driven revenue are:
1. **Save rate** (intent to purchase later)
2. **Share rate** (trusted enough to recommend)
3. **DM volume** (purchase consideration happening)
4. **Branded search volume** (social driving brand awareness)
5. **Email sign-ups from social** (capturing the relationship)

Likes, follower count, and reach are leading indicators at best. The above five are the true business-impact metrics.

### Building a Culture of Data
The best social media teams don't have one "analytics person." Every team member understands the KPIs, reviews performance, and proposes optimizations. Make analytics accessible (dashboards on a shared screen, weekly 15-minute reviews), not locked in one person's spreadsheet. When everyone owns the data, everyone improves the results.
