## 5.3.1 Social Media Copywriting

### Writing for Each Platform

Every platform has its own language, tone, and structural expectations. Writing a LinkedIn post the same way you write a TikTok caption is a guaranteed way to underperform.

**Platform Writing Guide:**

| Platform | Character Limit | Ideal Length | Tone | Structure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 2,200 | 125-150 (above fold) | Casual, visual-first | Hook → Story → CTA → Hashtags |
| LinkedIn | 3,000 | 1,200-1,500 | Professional, insightful | Hook → Empty line → Short paragraphs → Question |
| TikTok | 4,000 | 5-50 words | Ultra-casual, trend-aware | Hook keyword + context + #hashtags |
| X (Twitter) | 280 (free) / 10K (premium) | <200 | Punchy, witty, concise | One idea per tweet; threads for depth |
| Facebook | 63,206 | 40-80 words | Conversational, community | Question or statement → context → CTA |
| Pinterest | 500 (description) | 100-200 | Descriptive, keyword-rich | What it is → why it helps → keywords |
| YouTube | 5,000 (description) | 200-500 | Informative, searchable | Summary → timestamps → links → keywords |

### Hook Formulas for Social Media Captions

The hook is the first 1-2 lines visible before "...more." It determines whether someone stops scrolling or keeps moving.

**15 Proven Hook Formulas:**

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Controversial statement** | "Email marketing is dead. Here's what replaced it." |
| 2 | **Number + benefit** | "7 tools that saved me 10 hours every week" |
| 3 | **Question** | "What if everything you know about SEO is wrong?" |
| 4 | **Story opener** | "Last year, I lost my biggest client. It was the best thing that happened." |
| 5 | **"Stop doing X"** | "Stop posting carousels. Here's what to do instead." |
| 6 | **Result first** | "We grew from 0 to 50K followers in 90 days. Here's exactly how." |
| 7 | **"Most people"** | "Most brands waste 80% of their social media budget. Don't be one of them." |
| 8 | **"Here's what no one tells you"** | "Here's what no one tells you about running TikTok ads." |
| 9 | **"I studied X"** | "I analyzed 500 viral posts. Here's the one thing they all had in common." |
| 10 | **Fear/loss** | "Your competitors are doing this. You probably aren't." |
| 11 | **"The truth about"** | "The truth about social media algorithms no one wants to admit." |
| 12 | **"If you're a [role]"** | "If you're a social media manager, you need to hear this." |
| 13 | **Comparison** | "I tested Canva vs. Adobe Express for 30 days. The winner surprised me." |
| 14 | **Challenge** | "Try this for 7 days and watch your engagement double." |
| 15 | **"This changed everything"** | "One small change in our posting strategy changed everything." |

**Real-Time Example:**
Sahil Bloom (3M+ LinkedIn followers) uses the "I studied X" hook formula consistently. His post "I spent 1,000 hours studying the world's most successful people. Here are 10 lessons:" received 50K+ likes and 2,000+ comments. The hook creates instant credibility and curiosity.

### Storytelling in Social Posts

**The AIDA Storytelling Framework for Social:**

```
A — ATTENTION: Hook that stops the scroll
I — INTEREST: Relatable problem or situation
D — DESIRE: Present the solution or transformation
A — ACTION: Clear CTA (comment, save, share, click)
```

**The PAS Framework (Problem → Agitate → Solution):**

| Step | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | Identify the pain point | "You post 5 times a week but engagement keeps dropping." |
| **Agitate** | Make the pain feel urgent | "Meanwhile, your competitor with half the followers is getting 3x your engagement." |
| **Solution** | Present the answer | "Here's the content framework they're using (and how you can too)." |

**Real-Time Example — Storytelling Post on LinkedIn:**

> I was 26, broke, and sleeping on my friend's couch.
>
> My startup had just failed. I had $200 in my bank account.
>
> A recruiter called me about a marketing role at a company I'd never heard of.
>
> I almost didn't take the call.
>
> That company was Shopify. That decision changed my life.
>
> Here's what I learned about saying YES when everything tells you to quit:
>
> [Thread continues with 5 lessons]

This type of post gets 10x engagement because it follows the hero's journey: struggle → turning point → lesson. People don't engage with facts. They engage with feelings.

### Call-to-Action Techniques

**CTA Types by Objective:**

| Objective | CTA Examples | Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement | "Comment 'YES' if you agree" / "Tag someone who needs this" | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Save/Bookmark | "Save this for later" / "Bookmark this thread" | Instagram, X |
| Share | "Share this with someone who needs to hear it" | All |
| Click | "Link in bio" / "Full guide at [link]" | Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Follow | "Follow for daily tips" / "Turn on notifications" | TikTok, Instagram |

| Comment | "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?" | All |
| Purchase | "Shop now — link in bio" / "Tap to shop" | Instagram, TikTok |

**CTA Placement Best Practice:**
- Instagram: End of caption + in last carousel slide
- LinkedIn: Last 2 lines of post
- TikTok: On-screen text + voiceover + caption
- YouTube: Verbal CTA at 30% and 90% of video + end screen

### Thread Writing Mastery (X and LinkedIn)

**Thread Structure Formula:**

```
Tweet/Post 1: HOOK — bold claim or stat (the "why should I read this?")
Tweet/Post 2-3: CONTEXT — background, problem setup
Tweet/Post 4-8: VALUE — main points, one per tweet/section
Tweet/Post 9: SUMMARY — recap key takeaways
Tweet/Post 10: CTA — "Retweet thread if helpful" / "Follow for more"
```

**Thread Best Practices:**
- Number each point for scannability
- Use line breaks between ideas
- Include visuals (screenshots, graphics) where possible
- End each tweet with a hook to the next ("But here's the twist...")
- Add the thread to your profile as a pinned post

**Real-Time Example:**
A marketing consultant wrote a 15-tweet thread: "I've reviewed 200 landing pages this year. 90% make the same 7 mistakes." It received 25K retweets and drove 3,000 new followers in 48 hours. The numbered format made it scannable, each tweet delivered one clear mistake, and the CTA at the end offered a free landing page audit. Threads that teach perform best.

### Graphic Design Principles for Social

**The C.R.A.P. Principles:**

| Principle | Definition | Social Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Contrast** | Elements should look noticeably different | Bold headlines vs. light body text; dark bg with white text |
| **Repetition** | Repeat visual elements for consistency | Same color palette, fonts, and style across all posts |
| **Alignment** | Every element should have a visual connection | Left-align text consistently; center logos and headings |
| **Proximity** | Related items should be grouped together | Keep headline and subhead close; separate from CTA |

**The 3-Second Test:** Your social media graphic must communicate its main message within 3 seconds. If someone scrolling at speed can't understand the point, the design fails. Use large text, minimal elements, and clear hierarchy.

**Typography Rules for Social:**
- Maximum 2 fonts per graphic (1 headline, 1 body)
- Minimum font size: 24pt for mobile readability
- High contrast between text and background
- Avoid script fonts for body text — they're hard to read small

### Carousel and Slider Design

Carousels are the highest-engagement format on Instagram (1.4x more reach than images) and LinkedIn.

**Carousel Design Framework:**

```
Slide 1: HOOK — Bold headline + visual hook (determines if user swipes)
Slide 2: PROBLEM — State the challenge or pain point
Slide 3-8: SOLUTION — One tip/point per slide with visual
Slide 9: SUMMARY — Recap key takeaways
Slide 10: CTA — "Save this post" / "Follow for more" / "Comment your favorite"
```

**Design Best Practices:**
- Consistent color palette across all slides
- One main idea per slide (don't overcrowd)
- Readable text at Instagram feed size (not just full-screen)
- Progress indicator (1/10, 2/10) or visual flow between slides
- First and last slides are most critical

**Real-Time Example:**
Chris Do (@thechrisdo, 1M+ followers) built his Instagram following almost entirely through educational carousels about design and business. His signature style: black background, white text, bold typography, one idea per slide. His carousels average 50K+ likes and 2,000+ saves. The format works because it delivers actionable value in a swipeable format.

## 5.3.3 Video Content for Social Media

### Short-Form Video Strategy (Reels, TikTok, Shorts)

Short-form video is the #1 content type across all major platforms in 2025-2026. It gets 2.5x more engagement than static content.

**Short-Form Video Formats That Work:**

| Format | Description | Example | Engagement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Talking head | Person speaking to camera with on-screen text | "3 things I wish I knew about marketing" | High (relatable) |
| Tutorial/How-to | Step-by-step demonstration | "How to create a carousel in Canva in 60 seconds" | Very High (saveable) |
| Before/After | Transformation reveal | "This brand's Instagram before and after our redesign" | High (satisfying) |
| Trending sound + original spin | Using popular audio with your niche twist | Trending dance with marketing tips on screen | Viral potential |
| Day in the life | Behind-the-scenes of work/process | "Day in the life of a social media manager" | High (authentic) |
| POV | First-person perspective scenario | "POV: Client asks for 'something viral'" | High (relatable humor) |
| Listicle | Numbered list delivered verbally | "5 tools every marketer needs" | Very High (saves) |
| Myth-busting | Debunking common misconceptions | "Stop believing these SEO myths" | High (controversial) |
| Green screen | Person in front of relevant image/article | Reacting to industry news or stats | Medium-High |

**Video Creation Workflow:**

```
Step 1: CONCEPT (5 min) — Hook + main point + CTA
Step 2: SCRIPT (10 min) — Write word-for-word or bullet points
Step 3: FILM (15 min) — Record in natural light, eye-level camera
Step 4: EDIT (20 min) — Add captions, music, cuts, text overlays
Step 5: OPTIMIZE (5 min) — Write caption, hashtags, thumbnail
Step 6: PUBLISH (2 min) — Post at optimal time
Step 7: ENGAGE (30 min) — Reply to comments in first hour
```

**Real-Time Example:**
A small accounting firm started creating 30-second TikToks explaining tax tips ("Did you know you can deduct your home office?" with on-screen text and trending audio). Within 6 months, they gained 200K followers and booked $400K in new client revenue directly from TikTok leads. Production budget: $0 (filmed on iPhone, edited in CapCut).

### Long-Form Video for YouTube and Facebook

**Long-Form Video Structure:**

```
0:00-0:30 — HOOK: Open with the most interesting point
0:30-2:00 — INTRO: What you'll cover and why it matters
2:00-X:00 — BODY: Main content divided into chapters
X:00-X:30 — SUMMARY: Recap key points
X:30-END — CTA: Subscribe, comment, next video link
```

**Ideal Length by Platform:**
- YouTube: 8-15 minutes (optimal for monetization and depth)
- Facebook: 3-5 minutes (attention span is shorter)
- LinkedIn: 1-3 minutes (professional, concise)

### Subtitles and Captions

**85% of social media video is watched without sound.** Captions are not optional — they are essential.

**Caption Methods:**

| Method | Tool | Accuracy | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Auto-generated (platform) | Instagram, TikTok, YouTube | 80-90% | Free |
| Auto-generated (third-party) | CapCut, Descript | 90-95% | Free-$24/mo |
| Manual (SRT file upload) | YouTube, Facebook | 100% | Time investment |
| Professional transcription | Rev.com, GoTranscript | 99%+ | $1.50-5/min |

**Caption Styling Best Practices:**
- White text with black outline or background box
- Large enough to read on mobile (minimum 24pt equivalent)
- Positioned in lower third (not blocking faces)
- Keyword-highlighted words in a different color for emphasis
- 1-2 lines max visible at any time

This sequential storytelling approach increases link clicks by 40% compared to a single story with a link.

## Strategies

1. **Hook-First Content Strategy:** Invest 50% of your creative effort on the first 3 seconds (video) or first line (text). The hook determines everything.
2. **Template-Driven Efficiency Strategy:** Build 10-15 templates that cover 80% of your content needs, reducing creation time by 60%.
3. **Video-First, Repurpose-Second Strategy:** Create one quality video, then extract stills, quotes, carousels, and text posts from it.
4. **Authentic Over Polished Strategy:** In 2025-2026, raw iPhone footage outperforms studio production on TikTok and Reels. Authenticity drives engagement.
5. **Storytelling Over Selling Strategy:** Lead with stories and value in 80% of content. The 20% that sells will perform better because the audience already trusts you.
6. **Trend Surfing Strategy:** Dedicate 20% of content to trending sounds, formats, and topics. Use them within the first 7 days of trending.

## Best Practices

1. **Write for mobile screens.** Most social media is consumed on phones. Short paragraphs, large text in graphics, and vertical formats.
2. **The 3-second rule.** If your content doesn't grab attention in 3 seconds, it won't get seen. Hook, hook, hook.
3. **One idea per post.** Don't try to say 5 things in one post. Say one thing clearly, and say it well.
4. **Captions are non-negotiable on video.** 85% of video is watched muted. No captions = invisible.
5. **Batch create, drip publish.** Spend focused time creating content in bulk, then schedule it over the week.
6. **Always include a CTA.** Every piece of content should ask the audience to do something: comment, save, share, click, follow.
7. **Test relentlessly.** Try different hooks, formats, posting times, and CTAs. Let performance data guide your creative decisions.
8. **Consistency beats creativity.** A mediocre post published on schedule beats a brilliant post published "whenever." The algorithm rewards consistency.

## Additional Insights

### The Content Quality Paradox
Counter-intuitively, highly polished, expensive content often underperforms on social media. Users scroll past branded-looking content because it feels like an ad. Raw, authentic, personality-driven content stops the scroll because it feels like a friend talking. Invest in ideas and hooks, not production value.

### The Save:Like Ratio
Saves are the most valuable engagement signal. A post that gets saved tells the algorithm "this content has lasting value." Aim for a save:like ratio of at least 1:5. Content that is reference-worthy (checklists, tutorials, frameworks) gets saved the most. Optimize for saves, not likes.

### The Content Flywheel
The best social media creators operate a content flywheel: Post → Analyze what works → Double down on winners → Post more of what works → Grow audience → Get more data → Analyze → Repeat. Each cycle makes your content better because you learn what your specific audience values. This flywheel compounds over time.
