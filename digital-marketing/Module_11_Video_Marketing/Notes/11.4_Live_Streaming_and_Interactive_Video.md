### Planning & Promoting Live Streams

**Pre-stream checklist (2-3 weeks before):**

```
WEEK 2-3 BEFORE:
├── Define the topic, format, and goals
├── Select platform(s) and format (solo, interview, panel)
├── Create promotional assets (banner, social graphics)
├── Schedule the stream on the platform
├── Send email announcement to list
├── Post "save the date" on social channels
└── Invite guests/co-hosts if applicable

WEEK 1 BEFORE:
├── Promote daily on social media (countdown)
├── Send reminder email (3 days before)
├── Test all equipment (camera, mic, internet, software)
├── Prepare outline/talking points (NOT a full script)
├── Brief guests on format and flow
├── Prepare engagement elements (polls, questions, giveaways)
└── Set up backup internet (mobile hotspot)

DAY OF:
├── Post "Going live in 1 hour / 30 min / NOW" reminders
├── Test stream privately (5 min before)
├── Start 5 minutes early for "waiting room" chat
├── Begin with energy and a clear agenda
└── Pin welcome comment with topic overview
```

**Real-Time Example:**
When Apple announces a keynote, the promotion cycle begins 2 weeks prior with a cryptic invitation, continues with media teaser coverage, and peaks with a "Watch the event live" push across Apple.com, YouTube, and social media. The anticipation cycle is as engineered as the event itself. Result: 10M+ concurrent viewers and global media coverage.

## 11.4.2 Interactive Video

### What Is Interactive Video?

Interactive video transforms passive viewing into active participation. Viewers can click, choose, explore, shop, and make decisions within the video itself. It's the evolution from "lean back and watch" to "lean forward and engage."

**Interactive video engagement statistics:**

| Metric | Interactive vs. Standard |
|---|---|
| **Completion rate** | 90% vs. 50-65% for linear video |
| **Engagement rate** | 3-4x higher than standard video |
| **Conversion rate** | 11% higher than static landing pages |
| **Lead capture rate** | 4-5x more form completions when embedded in video |
| **Time on page** | 47% longer when interactive video is present |

## Implementation Steps for Live Streaming & Interactive Video

### Phase 1: Live Streaming Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
1. Choose your primary live platform based on audience
2. Set up streaming software (start with StreamYard for simplicity)
3. Test equipment: camera, mic, lighting, internet speed
4. Create branded overlays: lower thirds, intro/outro screens
5. Schedule your first test stream (private, team-only)

### Phase 2: First Live Streams (Weeks 3-4)
6. Announce your first public live stream 1-2 weeks in advance
7. Prepare a loose outline (not a script) with talking points
8. Assign a chat moderator (or use moderation bots)
9. Go live with a Q&A format (easiest to start with)
10. Record the stream for repurposing

### Phase 3: Interactive Video (Months 2-3)
11. Audit existing video content for interactive opportunities
12. Choose an interactive platform based on your use case (shopping, education, storytelling)
13. Create your first interactive video (start simple: 2-3 hotspots or choices)
14. A/B test interactive vs. standard video performance
15. Track interactive-specific analytics (interaction rate, choice distribution)

### Phase 4: Scale & Optimize (Month 3+)
16. Establish a regular live streaming schedule (weekly or bi-weekly)
17. Build a simulcasting workflow for multi-platform reach
18. Develop a live stream → content repurposing pipeline
19. Expand interactive video into sales, training, and onboarding
20. Invest in personalized video for sales/customer success teams

## Best Practices — Live Streaming & Interactive Video

1. **Test everything before going live** — Do a 10-minute private stream. Check audio, video, overlays, screen sharing, and internet stability.
2. **Start 5 minutes early** — Let the "waiting room" fill up. Play music, chat with early arrivals, build anticipation.
3. **Have a loose outline, not a script** — Live streams should feel conversational, not rehearsed. Bullet points keep you on track without sounding robotic.
4. **Engage the chat relentlessly** — Acknowledge viewers by name, answer questions, run polls. The #1 reason people watch live is interaction.
5. **Keep energy high** — Live requires 30% more energy than pre-recorded. Stand up if possible, move around, vary your tone.
6. **Promote the replay** — 70% of your live stream views will come from the replay. Edit it, add timestamps, and promote it like a regular video.
7. **Go live consistently** — Weekly live streams at the same time train your audience to show up. Random streams get random attendance.
8. **Have a clear CTA for every stream** — Don't just chat aimlessly. Every stream should have a goal: sign up, download, subscribe, share.
9. **Invest in internet reliability** — A 2-second buffer can lose 30% of viewers. Use wired ethernet, test upload speed, have a mobile hotspot backup.
10. **Start interactive small** — Don't build a 20-branch choose-your-own-adventure for your first project. Add 3 hotspots to an existing video and measure results.
